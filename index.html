These Are Not Unreasonable Feelings Daily Dad

Kids don’t know, as you know, that they’ll survive this bit of discomfort or awkwardness or boredom. That’s the whole thing — they haven’t been through it enough times yet.

It’s a short period when the idea of sleeping on a pull-out couch in a hotel room is actually thrilling to them. It’s a short period when they get excited about going for a drive or going through a car wash. It’s a brief window when they want to play catch in the backyard or want you to tell them a story before bed. It’s not long that a $1 popsicle serves as motivation.

We are in a losing battle with cynicism and experience. We can only hold out for so long. Enjoy it while you have it. Let that joy be infectious. Blow their minds while you can. Take the trips while you can. Appreciate the leverage while you have it. Because you’re losing a little bit of it every day.

These moments don’t announce when they’re over. There’s no final exciting car wash. No bedtime story you recognize as the last. They just slip away. So catch them while you can.

Emily Wilson’s Odyssey: the translation that changed everything

Ryan Holiday is reading the Emily Wilson translation of Homer’s Odyssey — the first English translation of the epic by a woman, widely considered the most accessible and alive version in the language.
