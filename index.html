Paramount Wins Warner Bros. Discovery in $111B Bidding War — Netflix Walks Away

In a stunning reversal, Warner Bros. Discovery's board declared Paramount Skydance's $111 billion hostile takeover offer superior to Netflix's deal — and Netflix chose not to counter, handing control of HBO, CNN, and two Hollywood studios to Paramount.

The monthslong corporate saga resolved in just a few hours on Thursday. WBD's board announced Paramount Skydance's offer of $31 per share was superior to Netflix's $27.75/share for the WBD streaming and studio assets. Netflix had 4 days to counter — and declined, citing the deal as a "nice to have" at the right price, not a "must have" at any price.

Paramount's overall offer valued the deal at roughly $111 billion, compared with Netflix's ~$83 billion. Netflix will still collect a $2.8 billion breakup fee covered by Paramount under its terms. Netflix stock jumped 10% in after-hours trading after the announcement — a sign investors viewed the walk-away as financially prudent.

If regulators clear the deal, the combined Paramount Skydance Warner Bros. Discovery will control HBO, CNN, CBS, major cable networks, and two Hollywood studios. Both Larry Ellison — bankrolling the Paramount deal — and CEO David Ellison have personal relationships with President Trump, which Paramount has argued provides "a more certain, expedited path to completion."

Create an AI Assistant with Its Own Phone Number

Set up your own AI personal assistant that you can call from any phone — using ElevenLabs for the voice agent and Twilio for the phone number. The whole process takes about 10 minutes and is free to test.

  1. In ElevenLabs, go to Agents → New Agent → Personal Assistant. Pick a voice, time zone, and first message. Add your website and calendar to its knowledge base.
  2. Go to Twilio, create an account ($15 free credits), and buy a phone number (~$1). Copy the number, account SID, and auth token from the dashboard.
  3. Back in ElevenLabs, go to Phone Numbers → Import from Twilio. Paste your SID, auth token, and phone number. Assign your agent — and you're done.
  4. Call the number. Your AI assistant picks up and can answer questions about your calendar, your business, or whatever you put in the agent's knowledge base.

Pro tip: The knowledge base is static — great for business info. For live data, add a webhook to Make.com or n8n so your agent can pull real-time calendar events.

Americans Are Leaving the US in Record Numbers Morning Brew

At least 180,000 US citizens relocated overseas in 2025 — pushing departures above arrivals for the first time since 1935, according to Brookings Institution data cited by the Wall Street Journal.

An estimated 4–9 million Americans are now expats, with growth accelerating. The American population of Portugal grew more than fivefold from 4,768 in 2020 to 26,000 in 2025. Americans moving to Ireland more than doubled year-over-year (4,900 to 9,600), and British citizenship applications from US nationals hit a record 8,790 — 42% above the previous high.

Motivations include lower cost of living on a US income, Euro lifestyle perks, and concerns about the US political climate. The share of US adults who desire to relocate overseas permanently doubled between 2010 and 2025, to 20%. Among American women aged 15–44, that figure is now 40%, per Gallup.

Pew Study: Teens Use AI for School — and 60% Say AI Cheating Is Widespread Rundown AI

A new Pew Research study of 1,458 US teens and parents finds AI adoption at mainstream levels among adolescents — with school use, entertainment, and significant cheating all reported at scale.

Around 60% of teens polled believe AI-assisted cheating is widespread among classmates, rising to 75% among teens who themselves use the technology. Teens tend to see AI as a personal positive — citing ease, learning, and efficiency — while negatives cited include job and creativity loss.

Critically, 40% of parents reported never having a conversation about AI with their child, reflecting a significant knowledge gap. Sam Altman recently said this generation will grow up in a world where AI intelligence "will just be normal" — but current teens are caught between an education system struggling to adapt and a technology moving faster than policy.
