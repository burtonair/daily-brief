FAA Will Let Boeing Self-Certify Its Own 737 Max and 787 Dreamliner

The FAA will restore Boeing's authority to issue its own airworthiness certificates for the 737 Max and 787 Dreamliner — a privilege revoked after two fatal Max crashes in 2018 and 2019 that killed 346 people.

The reinstatement marks a significant regulatory rollback. The FAA stripped Boeing's self-certification following the Lion Air and Ethiopian Airlines disasters, which were linked to a faulty automated flight control system Boeing had failed to adequately disclose to regulators or pilots. Aviation safety advocates have questioned whether the company has made sufficient reforms to warrant the restoration.
