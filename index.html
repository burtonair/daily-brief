Summer Travel in Crisis as Oil Tops $111 and Airfares Surge 24%

The war in Iran has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, sending oil past $111 a barrel and triggering a cascade of flight cuts, fare hikes, and fuel surcharges that threaten to ground Americans' summer plans. Morning Brew

Average global airfares jumped 24% to $465 compared with the same week last year, according to OAG. Transatlantic tickets already cost $200 more on average than a month ago. Delta said jet fuel costs ballooned by $400 million in March alone, and United is cutting ~5% of flights in off-peak periods.

Gas topped $4 a gallon for the first time in two years, and cruises face similar fuel crunches. The share of Americans planning international trips fell to 17% — the lowest since 2022. Budget airlines with thinner margins face the steepest fare hikes, with Ryanair's CEO warning the UK is especially vulnerable given its reliance on Kuwait for nearly 25% of its jet fuel. Amazon is also adding a 3.5% fuel and logistics surcharge for many sellers.

Turn Any Flat Image into a Fully Editable Design

Use Canva's new Magic Layers feature to turn any flat AI image into a fully editable design — enabling you to fix small details without recreating entire outputs.

  1. On the Canva homepage, select Magic Layers, click Select Media, and choose an image
  2. Wait 30–60 seconds while Canva processes the image. It reads the layout, identifies text, objects, and background, and splits them into individual layers
  3. Click any layer and edit it directly — swap a date, change a tagline, fix a typo. Or click an object layer to move it, resize it, or delete it entirely
  4. Drop in a replacement image (new product photo, logo) and it slots into the existing layout. Adjust the background color using the color picker if needed

Pro tip: To use Magic Layers on an existing design, open the design, click Uploads to add your image, select it, click Edit in the toolbar, then select Magic Layers.

Tesla Can't Get Out of Its Slump Morning Brew

Tesla delivered just 358K cars last quarter — a letdown given Q1 2025 was already weak from production pauses and Musk backlash.

Wall Street expected more. Deliveries significantly undershot analyst projections, and the 408K vehicles produced left thousands of unsold cars. Budget Models 3 and Y accounted for 95% of deliveries while the Cybertruck remained an offbeat purchase.

Tesla's stock fell nearly 6% and is down almost 20% since January. Analysts blame the aging lineup, rising competition, and the loss of the $7,500 EV tax credit. On the bright side, soaring gas prices may be nudging some buyers to go electric — Hyundai Ioniq 5 sales rose 13% last month, and Cadillac EV sales were up 20% in Q1.

OpenAI Acquires TBPN in First Media Deal — and Hands It to Its Political Fixer Rundown AI 1% Better

OpenAI bought the daily live tech talk show for a reported low hundreds of millions — its first-ever media acquisition.

TBPN pulls around 70K viewers per episode, drawn almost entirely from the founder-investor class. Co-founders Jordi Hays and John Coogan now report to Chris Lehane, OpenAI's chief global affairs officer and veteran political operative known for Clinton-era crisis management.

1% Better's Grant Janich notes the deeper story: the show's existing $30M ad business is being killed immediately, signaling OpenAI is buying the talent and rolodex, not the revenue. The move comes as OpenAI navigates copyright lawsuits, safety departures, and a likely IPO within 18 months. Coogan's relationship with Altman predates TBPN by over a decade — Altman funded his first company in 2013.

AI & Strategy

Microsoft Abandons "Off-Frontier" Strategy, Will Build Its Own State-of-the-Art AI Models 1% Better

AI chief Mustafa Suleyman told Bloomberg that Microsoft intends to build frontier models across text, images, and audio by 2027 — burying last year's "off-frontier" doctrine.

Copilot had just 6 million daily active users in February vs. 440 million for ChatGPT and 82 million for Gemini. Among paid AI subscribers, Copilot's market share fell from 18.8% to 11.5% — a 39% decline that let Gemini leapfrog it into second place.

The renegotiated OpenAI partnership freed Microsoft to build independently but cost it the right of first refusal as OpenAI's compute provider. Frontier training is already underway on a cluster of Nvidia GB200 chips deployed last October. The company spent $37.5 billion on AI infrastructure in a single quarter.
