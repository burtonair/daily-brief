The Rundown AI How To • AI Training

Build a Custom Blog Writing Agent With No Code

Learn how to build a subagent in Langflow that writes blog posts in your website's style. The agent runs locally for free (besides LLM calls) and can be triggered by Claude, Codex, etc.

  1. Download Langflow, open it on your computer (it runs locally), click New Flow, go to the content generation section, and choose the Blog Writer template.
  2. Add a text input called "topic" and link your best blog in the reference input so the agent can copy your writing style.
  3. Add your OpenAI or Anthropic API key under the language model settings. Select the model you want the flow to use.
  4. Click on Playground and test it with a topic. That runs the flow, pulls in your reference style, and gives you a first draft.

Pro tip: If you want Claude to use this as a subagent, click Share > MCP Server > Claude MCP. Then Claude can call that blog-writing tool for you whenever you need it.

AI & Healthcare The Rundown AI

Mayo Clinic AI Spots Pancreatic Cancer Up to 3 Years Early

REDMOD reads invisible tissue patterns on standard CT scans, nearly doubling specialist accuracy in early detection.

Mayo Clinic published new data on REDMOD, an AI that reviewed nearly 2,000 routine CT scans originally read as normal before later diagnoses, catching 73% of cases early. At the two-year mark, the AI spotted roughly 3x as many early cancers as experienced radiologists.

Pancreatic cancer's 5-year survival rate is below 15%, making early diagnosis critical. Running on scans patients already get, AI screening could become a standard part of routine care.

AI Research The Rundown AI

Food AI's 'ChatGPT Moment' — Tastes Like a Chef

Startup KAIKAKU AI published Epicure, claiming its model can pick up on flavor, cuisine, and texture from how chefs combine ingredients.

Researchers cleaned 6,653 messy ingredient entries into 1,032 usable foods, then mapped how they relate across recipes with AI. Despite never seeing chemistry data or taste labels, the model identified all 5 basic tastes, ordered peppers by spiciness, and tagged cuisines by region. KAIKAKU is pairing this with their robotics arm, pitching "autonomous food infrastructure" for commercial kitchens.
