Columnist | Daily Dad

This Is Worth It

It's too expensive. It's too much to plan. You're too busy. You're saving for a home project or new car. Who will watch the dog? Look, there are a bunch of reasons not to go on vacation — especially with kids.

But the benefits outweigh pretty much everything else. It doesn't have to be anything extravagant or in a far-flung place. Whatever kind of vacation it is — a weekend away, a road trip to the beach, a week with the grandparents, or time in a special place — it's an investment in family time, in experiences, in memories.

Your kids likely won't remember their first toys or books or stuffed animals. But you know what they will remember? Their first time exploring a national park with you. Their first time visiting a new city as a family. Their first time going anywhere new, experiencing something new — together.

Vacations mean dedicated time when you're not pulled away by errands, work emails, or cleaning up the house. They mean intentional time to fully enjoy being with each other, trying new things, exploring new places, playing board games together at the hotel before bed. So do it while you can. They won't forget it. You won't forget it. And that means you won't regret any of it, either.

Bezos's Prometheus: An AI Bob the Builder for Jet Engines, Skyscrapers, and Phones

Amazon founder Jeff Bezos is betting that "artificial general engineer" AI will make the dream-to-product loop run 10x faster — and that it'll create more jobs, not fewer.

Bezos started Prometheus in late 2024 alongside Vik Bajaj, a physicist and chemist who helped create Verily, Alphabet's life-sciences arm. Bajaj says engineers designing the world's most complex machines like jet engines still "use tools that really haven't changed for decades." A request for 10% more jet engine thrust can take a decade today — Bezos wants to close that gap dramatically.

The $12B raise brings Prometheus to a $41B valuation. Investors include JPMorgan, BlackRock, and Goldman Sachs. Bezos is also reportedly looking to raise $100 billion to buy industrial companies that could feed data to Prometheus and use its AI modeling.

Bezos took the opposite stance on AI job fears from most CEOs — saying the productivity boost will create "more than 10x" the opportunities. Manufacturing accounts for ~8% of US nonfarm employment.

Fable's Launch Was Anything But Smooth — Scientists Can't Even Say Hello

Anthropic apologized for safety features in its Fable model that invisibly downgraded answers about AI development, coming amid broader backlash over filters blocking chemistry, biology, and cybersecurity questions.

Fable 5 arrived Tuesday as the first public Mythos-class model, with filters screening chats across chemistry, biology, cybersecurity, and AI development topics. The model initially weakened answers for suspected AI development use without telling users. Anthropic is now providing on-screen alerts of model re-routing.

Dean Ball, who advised the White House on AI, called downgrading research answers without disclosure "shockingly hostile and a terrible look." Several scientists reported being unable to say "hello" to the model without getting flagged. OpenAI has another opening — similar to the Anthropic credit split anger — with its coming 5.6 release to take the user-first path.
