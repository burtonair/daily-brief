AI & Culture — Graduation Season

AI Is the Unavoidable Guest at Every Graduation This Year

Morning Brew

From Carnegie Mellon to Emory, commencement speakers can’t stop talking about artificial intelligence — and graduates are sending very mixed signals back.

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang told Carnegie Mellon grads to run, not walk, toward AI. He framed this moment as historically opportune and insisted AI won’t replace them — it will enhance them — as long as they embrace it. Magic Johnson delivered a similar message at Stillman College and Tuskegee University: grind hard, master AI.

But real estate executive Gloria Caulfield got booed at the University of Central Florida’s College of Arts and Humanities after calling AI “the next industrial revolution.” Young creatives, it turns out, don’t find that framing comforting — they see AI as a threat to their livelihoods.

Delta CEO Ed Bastian read the room best at Emory: he told grads he used AI to draft his speech, found it lacked “soul or warmth,” and rewrote it by hand. The crowd applauded. And country singer Eric Church went viral at UNC Chapel Hill with a guitar metaphor about faith, family, ambition, and authenticity — zero mention of AI.

Courts

Weinstein Mistrial: Jury Deadlocks a Second Time

Morning Brew

A New York judge declared a mistrial in Harvey Weinstein’s retrial on third-degree rape charges after the jury deadlocked following three days of deliberation.

This was the third criminal trial against Weinstein in New York and the second consecutive deadlock on the same charges. Prosecutors have 30 days to decide whether to retry the case. Weinstein, who has denied wrongdoing, remains incarcerated on prior convictions in New York and California regardless of this outcome.

Someone Paid $9 Million to Have Lunch With Warren Buffett

Morning Brew

An anonymous bidder spent $9,000,100 at a charity eBay auction to dine with Buffett, Steph Curry, and Ayesha Curry in Omaha.

Proceeds benefit the Glide Foundation (homeless aid in San Francisco) and Curry’s Eat. Learn. Play. Foundation for Oakland students. Buffett will match the donation to each. The price has actually declined — the last time Buffett participated in 2022, the auction went for $19 million.
