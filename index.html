Breaking — Economy

Good Jobs, Bad Vibes: Labor Market Defies the Gloom

US employers added 115,000 jobs in April—roughly doubling expectations—yet consumer sentiment plunged to a record low as gas prices hit a four-year high.

The April jobs report exceeded forecasts, with the economy adding 115,000 jobs. Healthcare drove gains, while transportation, warehousing, and retail posted their best numbers since 2024. Combined with March's strong revisions, it marks the biggest two-month payroll increase since 2024.

But warning signs flashed beneath the surface. Wage growth came in at just 3.6%, below the 4.2% inflation rate economists predict tariffs and the Iran conflict will drive this year. The share of involuntary part-timers hit its highest since December, and labor force participation sank to its lowest since October 2021.

Consumer sentiment fell to another record low as surging gas prices compounded cost-of-living anxiety, per the University of Michigan. Next week's CPI data will be pivotal for the Fed's next rate decision.

Cybersecurity Morning Brew

ShinyHunters Claims Massive Canvas Breach Affecting 275 Million Users

The hacking group shut down the platform used by half of North American colleges—right during final exams.

Canvas, the learning management platform used by roughly half of all North American colleges, went down for hours on Thursday after parent company Instructure suffered a massive cyberattack. ShinyHunters claimed credit, asserting the breach affected 8,800 schools globally.

Instructure first noted a cybersecurity incident on May 1, when it discovered student IDs, names, emails, and inter-user messages had been breached. Hackers warned school officials they'd need to negotiate by May 12 or face a data leak. Some Harvard users reported seeing a hacker message on the Canvas login page during the outage.

ABC Accuses Trump Administration of Trying to Stifle Free Speech

The network's legal filing challenges FCC scrutiny of The View and the equal time rule.

In a fiery legal filing, ABC accused the FCC of threatening "to upend decades of settled law and practice and chill critical protected speech." At issue is whether The View is subject to the equal time rule when politicians appear for interviews. The FCC said it was reviewing ABC's claim that the program qualifies as an exempt news show. The filing comes amid the network's broader clash with the administration over comedian Jimmy Kimmel.
