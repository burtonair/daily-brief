Sunday Deep Dive • Entertainment • Business

Is Reality TV Washed?

After a decadeslong boom, the reality TV industry is downsizing hard — new unscripted shows fell 15% last year, now a third below 2022 levels. But audiences haven’t voted it off the island entirely. Morning Brew

The number of new unscripted shows in the US took a nosedive last year, falling 15%, according to data from Luminate reported by the New York Times. Powerhouse networks like HGTV released episodes of only 35 shows in 2025, compared to 78 in 2019. The Food Network produced just half as many shows as it did in 2019.

Streaming platforms and linear TV combined ordered 31% fewer reality series in 2025 than the previous year. Industry consolidation left fewer buyers, and the ratings of once-iconic brands like The Bachelor and The Real Housewives are on the decline, even as their budgets have swelled.

Still, Love Island USA was the most-watched original streaming show last year with 18 billion minutes streamed on Peacock. The genre isn’t dead — it’s just transforming.

Scandal • Industry

The Biggest Reality TV Drama Is Behind the Scenes Morning Brew

Forget who kissed who — in the industry that pushes its stars to produce drama, there’s been no shortage of real-life crises this year.

The Bachelorette: Three days before its March premiere, Disney indefinitely postponed the season starring Taylor Frankie Paul after TMZ released a video appearing to show her throwing metal bar stools at her then-boyfriend, one of which seemed to strike her daughter. The Secret Lives of Mormon Wives halted work on its fifth season amid an investigation.

Love is Blind: The show has faced exploitation allegations and lawsuits since 2022. Season 10 drew manosphere accusations after one male contestant compared himself to Andrew Tate, while women said producers cut conversations about political dealbreakers.

Documentary fallout: America’s Next Top Model is back in the limelight with a Netflix documentary featuring Tyra Banks, who’s now being criticized for a lack of accountability. A Biggest Loser documentary raised fresh allegations of physical and emotional harm.

A lawsuit by a former Real Housewives of New York City star accusing Bravo and Andy Cohen of a hostile work environment is proceeding in federal court.

Media • Platforms

How Reality TV Migrated to YouTube, Twitch, and Social Media Morning Brew

The drama and unpredictability that defined reality TV has moved to new platforms — and even into politics.

YouTube accounts for nearly 13% of viewing time in the US per Nielsen. MrBeast — basically a reality-TV host with 430M+ subscribers — has successfully taken a show to Amazon Prime Video.

Livestreaming has supplanted the more voyeuristic aspects of reality TV. Twitch has more than 21 million active streamers, and athlete and celebrity streams are up nearly 20% year over year. Social media and the influencer economy now carry on the spirit of reality TV in five-minute Instagram clips.

While the 2007 writers strike boosted reality TV’s nonunionized appeal, the 2023 Hollywood strike delivered no such windfall. Industry consolidation left fewer studios as buyers of content.
