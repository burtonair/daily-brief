Don't Ever Apologize for This

We've all felt the judging eyes. We've felt the embarrassment. We've asked in whispers or over text, "Is it ok?" "Do they mind?" "Are you sure?" We've held our breath, crossed our fingers, been desperately anxious, hoping that our kids wouldn't cry or misbehave or cause problems.

But you know what? Screw them. If you want to bring your kids, bring them. If people have a problem with that, let them.

Just recently, the Olympic skater Tara Lipinski had to deal with some of the above over her decision to bring her 2-year-old with her to the Milan winter Olympics. "Maybe I don't want to choose," she replied on social media. "I want my daughter to see a mother who chases her dreams and shows up fully for her."

No one should have to apologize for bringing their kids with them. They should be praised! It's called being an active and involved parent. There's nothing wrong with that. And there's definitely nothing wrong with letting your kids see you work, letting them see you at your best. Stop worrying what random strangers think. Worry what your kids will think. Think about what they can take from this. That's really the important thing.

Turn Any CSV Into an Excel Dashboard Using Claude

Learn four copy-paste prompts that turn any raw CSV into a clean, visual dashboard using Claude in Excel. Tested on World Bank GDP data and NBA player stats — works on any dataset.

Step-by-Step

  1. Understand your data: Import the CSV into Excel, open Claude, and prompt: "View the data in this workbook. Tell me what this data is, what each column represents, and flag any problems with the structure or formatting. List the issues."
  2. Clean the data: Prompt: "Fix every issue you identified. Remove any junk rows or metadata. Format numbers for human readability, and create proper tables with clear headers. Sort by the most important metric."
  3. Build the dashboard: Prompt: "Create a 'Dashboard' tab. Identify 3–5 most useful metrics from this data and build a summary table for each. Rank the entries. Highlight the top performers in green and the bottom performers in red."
  4. Add visualizations: Prompt: "Add 2–3 charts to the Dashboard tab that tell the story of this data. Pick the chart types that make the most sense. Make them clean, labeled."

Pro tip: Once created, prompt Claude to turn the dashboard into a reusable template with placeholder labels — so you can reuse it every month on fresh data.

AI Research — Education

OpenAI Publishes Framework to Track Whether ChatGPT Actually Helps Students Learn Rundown AI

OpenAI published a new framework built with Stanford and Estonia's University of Tartu to measure whether ChatGPT helps people retain knowledge over time — the company's first attempt at rigorous learning outcome measurement.

OAI's Learning Outcomes Measurement Suite tracks AI's impact on motivation, persistence, and recall. A 300+ student trial found microeconomics students scored 15% higher with ChatGPT's study mode, though results in other subjects weren't statistically significant. Estonia is running the framework's biggest test, tracking 20,000 high schoolers across the country for a full semester. The initiative comes as studies on both ends of the debate — from positive AI-first learning programs to concerns about "brainrot" impacts on critical thinking — have made the question of AI's net educational impact increasingly urgent.
