Finance & Tech Morning Brew

Nvidia Is Valued Like AI Never Happened

After a nearly $1 trillion loss in market cap over less than two months, Nvidia’s stock has retreated to 2019 valuation levels—even as its GPUs remain the undisputed engine of the AI boom.

From the end of 2022 through last year, Nvidia’s stock jumped over 1,100% as its GPUs became the clearest early winners of the AI era. It hit an all-time high in May. Then came the slide: down 16% since the peak, up just 5.6% for the year—far behind the S&P 500’s 9.6% gain and the Nasdaq 100’s 16% rise.

The culprit isn’t AI skepticism—it’s competition. Investors are now spreading bets across the entire semiconductor stack. The Philadelphia Stock Exchange Semiconductor Index has skyrocketed 74% this year. High-bandwidth memory chipmaker Micron is up 229% since January, after gaining 239% last year. Rivals like AMD and startup SambaNova are landing massive investments from investors hunting for the next Nvidia.

And even Nvidia’s own biggest customers are going around it. Alphabet and Amazon are developing their own in-house AI chips. Chinese AI company DeepSeek is building its own chip too, per Reuters. Nvidia still holds a commanding 97% share of the server GPU market, but the moat is being dug around.

Meanwhile, the AI memory story has a darker subplot: Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron—who make nearly all high-bandwidth memory—are facing a class action filed June 25 in California accusing them of cartel-like price coordination during the AI boom. The three have been here before—and last time, the government won.

What Does This Say About You?

You say that family is everything. You say your kids are your priority. You say that it’s all for them. But what does your calendar say? What does the way you spend your time say?

We talk a good game, we convince ourselves—but the facts don’t lie. Love is spelled one way: T-I-M-E. If the answer to “how do you spend your time?” is “constantly at the office” or “constantly on your phone,” well…

Of course, privilege comes into this discussion. The single parent working two minimum-wage jobs to keep the heat on is not worse than the semi-retired tech millionaire who has the time to walk their kids to school each morning. But parents of all stripes have the freedom to choose how they spend their free time, what they put first—after managing to provide for their children.

We say our kids are important—but our words don’t matter. Our choices are what matter. Our actions and our habits are what matter. Where are you spending your time? What are you demonstrating as your priority? That’s what counts.

We could spend more time with our kids. We could be there more than we are. We just have to put our priorities into place instead of just paying lip service to them.

Add CGI to Any Video with Higgsfield

Use ChatGPT to craft a tight VFX prompt, then drop it into Higgsfield with your source clip to generate AI-powered CGI additions—no editing software required.

  1. Start with a short video clip. Wide shots with a clear subject, simple motion, and visible background work better—they give the model room to work.
  2. Before opening Higgsfield, draft a strong CGI prompt with ChatGPT. Tell it to make the command more specific about what to add and what not to change.
  3. Open Gemini Omni Flash in Higgsfield under the video tab. Upload your source clip, paste in the refined prompt from ChatGPT, and click generate.
  4. Review the generated clip against the original. Revise in Higgsfield or start fresh with a new prompt.

Pro tip: Once you have a solid result, use Higgsfield to revise the generated video itself—removing unwanted effects without starting over. Full walkthrough here.

Tech & Privacy Morning Brew

Meta’s New AI App Can Manipulate Any Public Instagram Photo—Unless You Opt Out

Meta unveiled Muse, an AI image generator with a troubling feature: anyone can grab a photo from any public Instagram profile and use it to generate AI-altered images, just by tagging the account.

Originally designed for advertisers without an art department, Muse is also rolling out across Meta AI, Facebook, WhatsApp, and Instagram for everyone else. The backlash has been immediate, given Meta’s history: a $5 billion fine in 2019 over Cambridge Analytica data access, and a 2021 shutdown of its facial recognition system following biometric data lawsuits.

To opt out on Instagram: set your profile to private, or find the “sharing and reuse” tab in Settings and toggle off “allow people to use your content on Instagram and with AI features on Meta.” Some users may not have the toggle yet.

World & Travel Morning Brew

EU Rejects Calls to Suspend New Biometric Border System Causing Airport Chaos

The EU’s new Entry/Exit System (EES), launched in April, requires fingerprinting and facial recognition for all non-EU travelers—and it’s causing hours-long queues at vacation hot spots across Europe.

Rome’s airports have had to partially suspend biometric collection nearly every day this summer—the system can only be paused in six-hour windows. Spain and Italy have both seen missed connecting flights and mounting traveler frustration. Despite the chaos, EU officials defended the system: about 44,500 travelers have been turned away under EES, including many who were evading overstay limits using multiple passports.

The Memory Cartel: A Class Action Targets Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron

As AI demand has made high-bandwidth memory the most lucrative product the chip industry has ever sold, a California class action filed June 25 put a blunt word to the shortage: cartel.

The three firms who make nearly all AI memory—Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron—are posting record quarters. The commodity DRAM in laptops and phones has grown scarce as wafers migrate to the premium tier; Apple has raised its prices and blamed memory costs. The lawsuit alleges the three coordinated to fix prices. This isn’t their first time in this position—and last time, the government won.
