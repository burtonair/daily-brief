Economy

Jobs report disappoints: only 57,000 added, half of what analysts expected Brew

The US economy added just 57,000 jobs in June — about half of what analysts projected — as restaurant and hospitality payrolls shrank by 61,000. Wage growth of 3.5% continues to trail 4.2% annual inflation.

April and May numbers were also revised down sharply — to 148,000 and 129,000 respectively, from 179,000 and 172,000. Still, job creation is running ahead of last year’s lull: employers have added an average of 92,000 jobs per month this year, compared with losing an average of 8,000 each month in the second half of 2025.

Healthcare and social assistance continued to be a major employment engine, adding almost 47,000 jobs. Construction and manufacturing added 11,000 and 3,000, potentially reflecting the ongoing AI data center buildout. The restaurant and hotel sector shrank by 61,000, bucking World Cup boost predictions — some economists say this signals lower-income consumers pulling back on leisure spending.

Despite the hiring slowdown, unemployment actually ticked down to 4.2% from 4.3% in May, partly because the number of people working or looking for work declined by 720,000. The shrinking labor force may reflect harsher immigration policies and baby boomer retirements. Trading odds of a Fed rate hike this month dropped below 18% after the report. US markets are closed today for Independence Day.

It Won’t Last Much Longer

“Out, out, brief candle” — these childhood infatuations and passions do not last. They grow out of them. They stop working on them. They get too cool for them.

It’s a short period when the idea of sleeping on a pull-out couch in a hotel room is actually thrilling to them. It’s a short period when they get excited about going for a drive or going through a car wash. It’s a brief window when they want to play catch in the backyard or want you to tell them a story before bed. It’s not long that a $1 popsicle serves as motivation.

We are in a losing battle with cynicism and experience. We can only hold out for so long. Enjoy it while you have it. Let that joy be infectious. Blow their minds while you can. Take the trips while you can. Appreciate the leverage while you have it. Because you’re losing a little bit of it every day.

These moments don’t announce when they’re over. There’s no final exciting car wash. No bedtime story you recognize as the last. They just slip away. So catch them while you can.
