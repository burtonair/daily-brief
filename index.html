Amazon Opens Its Million-Robot Logistics Empire to All Businesses

Amazon launched Amazon Supply Chain Services, renting out its freight, distribution, fulfillment, and parcel network to any business willing to pay. FedEx dropped 10% and UPS fell 10.5%—their worst day in over a year.

The announcement sent shockwaves through the freight market. GXO Logistics fell 17.7% as the warehouse-for-hire firm competes most directly with what Amazon is now openly selling. Procter & Gamble signed up to move raw materials, 3M is routing products through the network, and Lands’ End is pooling inventory across sales channels.

Peter Larsen, the 18-year Amazon veteran running the business, told reporters Amazon was “doing for logistics what AWS had done for computing.” The substrate is staggering: one million robots across 300+ fulfillment centers, coordinated by DeepFleet—a generative AI foundation model trained on Amazon’s accumulated operations data that lifts fleet travel efficiency by roughly 10%.

Amazon has been building this for 14 years since the $775 million Kiva Systems acquisition in 2012. UPS, by comparison, runs around 1,400 warehouse robots company-wide—making Amazon’s operation roughly 700 times the size. 1% Better

Columnist Daily Dad

Show Them The Wow In The World

It is absurd, this thing called life—this world we live in. Not just that we exist (which is a miracle of miracles), but how everything that does exist works…and works together. The way certain trees lean towards the equator. That whales and birds migrate thousands of miles to return to some exact spot. The way X-rays or antibiotics work…or how a law is passed.

Perhaps you’ve heard of the podcast that the great Guy Raz does for kids, called “Wow in the World.” That title expresses it perfectly. That’s what our job as parents and teachers is: to help them see the “wow” in the world. To help them appreciate and understand the natural “wow” that’s out there and to be inspired by the created “wow”—inventions, ideas, art.

Because we want them to protect the natural “wow” and contribute to the created one. We want them to be curious. We want them to see the meaning and the power in all that’s out there. We want them to be excited and awestruck.

And we want them to believe—to know—that they can contribute to that beauty in life, that beauty in the world. Because they can. And if we, their parents, support them and encourage them, they will.

How to Replace Siri with a Free Local AI Model

Download a free AI model to your iPhone and bind it to your phone’s action button like Siri. The model runs locally—no internet required and your data stays private.

  1. Download Locally AI from the App Store and choose your model. Google’s open-source Gemma model works great as a starting point.
  2. Download the AI and keep the app open. Open Settings, search for Action Button, swipe to the Shortcut option, search “Locally AI,” and choose Voice Mode.
  3. Press the Action Button on your iPhone and wait for the chime. The app will ask you to download a speech-to-text model the first time.
  4. Try asking the model a question. They work best for explaining concepts, translation tasks, and math.

Pro tip: Download a bigger model and run the same prompt through it. Compare speed, storage size, and answer quality to get the perfect AI for your iPhone.

Economy Morning Brew

Birmingham Tops List of Best Cities for New Grads

ADP ranked 53 metro areas based on hiring momentum, wages, and affordability. Birmingham, AL earned the top spot due to strong job growth and a 16% jump in median annual wages.

Tampa–St. Petersburg followed in second due to hiring growth, while San Jose rose to third thanks to tech hiring at high salaries. San Francisco and New York City made the top 10 despite high costs. Raleigh, NC lost its two-year hold on first place, dropping to fourth. Unemployment for young degree holders jumped to 5.6%, up from 4.8% a year before.
