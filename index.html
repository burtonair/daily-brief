Owning a home now costs everything — and it’s only getting worse Brew

Emergency repairs up 175%, insurance up 72%, property taxes up 31% — the cost of homeownership has exploded since 2019, locking out the middle class and biting longtime owners alike.

An analysis conducted by the Wall Street Journal found that between 2019 and 2025, homeownership costs surged across every category: emergency repairs (+175%), home maintenance (+85%), insurance (+72%), interest (+35%), and property taxes (+31%). The combined effect has made it dramatically harder for middle-class Americans to enter the market.

According to Redfin, a buyer with a $2,500/month budget in 2019 could have purchased a $517,500 home with 20% down at the then-common 3% rate. Today that same budget only stretches to a $384,000 home at 6.5% — a 26% drop in purchasing power with the same monthly payment.

Longtime owners feel the squeeze too. The average US homeowner spent $9,000/year on improvement, maintenance, and emergency repairs in 2019. By 2025 that figure had climbed to $12,500. HOA fees are up 51% since 2021. US inflation is running at 4.2% — more than double the Fed’s 2% target — straining budgets on every front simultaneously.

Columnist — Daily Dad

How Much Longer Will They Want To Do This?

You’re tired. It’s been a long day or a long trip. It’s all the way across town. They haven’t mentioned it in a while. The tickets aren’t cheap.

There are a million reasons not to go: to pass on that children’s concert, to not stop at that roadside attraction, to not squeeze in the museum, to not go to the water park, to not take them to that store they love or that park with the ducks. But it’s worth asking, as you run through these excuses, how much longer will they want to do this? How long until the window closes?

Soon enough they’ll be too cool. Soon enough they won’t get excited like this anymore. Soon enough they’ll be too busy. Soon enough they’ll move on — and eventually move out. Almost all of these interests are phases. In any case, the opportunity to connect over them is fleeting.

Don’t let it go. Don’t pass up the chance. Even if it seems like a pain. Even if you don’t feel like doing it right now. You will regret not having done it with them while they were still interested. Now is the time.
