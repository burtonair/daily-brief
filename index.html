Breaking — World

Oil Shortage Fears Spread as Iran Refuses to Reopen Strait of Hormuz

As the war in the Middle East stretches into its fourth week, energy supply chains around the world are starting to buckle. Iran is reportedly not interested in discussing reopening the crucial passageway for oil and liquefied natural gas. Morning Brew

Southeast Asia is taking the brunt of looming shortages, importing nearly 80% of its crude oil from the Persian Gulf. Around 40% of gas stations in Laos and about a third in Cambodia have closed due to fuel shortages. The Philippines has instituted four-day workweeks to reduce energy use, and Pakistan has closed schools for two weeks.

Jet fuel stockpiles are particularly strained, with prices jumping as high as $200 per barrel compared to the prewar price of $85–$90. Airlines have canceled flights and warned of price increases — American Airlines expects $400 million in additional fuel costs this quarter alone.

In the US, gas prices have increased by a dollar on average to almost $4 per gallon since the war began, even as economists wave away recession fears.

You’re Not the Only One

“The pain you feel is a timeless pain, a common pain… You can find a way to connect with others, people who have been through this, too — or are going through it — because again, you’re not alone.” That’s the passage of the week from Daily Dad — a reminder that whatever struggle you’re facing as a parent, someone has walked that road before.

Unilever in Talks to Sell ~$33 Billion Food Business to McCormick

The deal would spin off Unilever’s food business — including Hellmann’s mayo — putting its focus entirely on household and personal care products.

Unilever’s food business is worth roughly $33 billion per Bloomberg Intelligence. McCormick, with a market value of about $14.8 billion, is much smaller than the $140 billion Unilever, but a successful deal would make it a Big Food player. The sale would be a major change for the nearly 100-year-old company.
