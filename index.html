Markets Surge on Iran Ceasefire, But Cracks Already Showing

Wall Street celebrated a two-week US–Iran ceasefire with the biggest one-day rally since April 2025, but Israel’s strikes on Beirut and confusion over the Strait of Hormuz are already testing the deal’s durability. Morning Brew

The S&P 500 closed up 2.5%, the Dow jumped 2.8%—its largest single-day percentage gain since last April—and the Nasdaq rose 2.8% following President Trump’s announcement of a “double sided CEASEFIRE.” Oil dropped well below $100/barrel and airlines soared, with United up 7.9% and Southwest up 6.7%.

But by yesterday afternoon, the optimism began cracking. Israel struck Beirut, killing at least 182 people, saying Lebanon was not part of the ceasefire deal. Pakistan, which brokered the agreement, disputes this. VP Vance called it a “legitimate misunderstanding.”

The Strait of Hormuz remains a flashpoint: Trump said Iran agreed to its “COMPLETE, IMMEDIATE, and SAFE OPENING,” but Iran disputed this after Israel’s attacks, and reports suggest new sea mines may have been placed in the waterway. Experts are warning the rally may have been premature. VP Vance will lead peace talks with Iran in Islamabad on Saturday.

Columnist Daily Dad

Are You Seeing the Beauty in It?

As parents, we have a choice about what we see. Just like everyone can view the cup as half-full or half-empty, we have significant power over the lens we bring to each parenting situation, crisis, and potential frustration.

In one of the most beautiful passages in Marcus Aurelius’ Meditations, Marcus deliberately identifies beauty in mundane things. He observes how loaves of bread crack open at the top in the oven. He notices wheat bending low under its own weight. He sees beauty even in the flecks of foam on a wild boar’s mouth—an otherwise grotesque vision.

As parents, we need to seek out this beauty—especially in our sleep-deprived, chaotic, and loud world. A disheveled room with toys tossed about should strike us as wonderful evidence of our kids’ happiness, not as just another mess to clean. We should hear our noisy house as a natural symphony and savor it while it lasts. Even in clipped fingernails and outgrown clothes, we can find the bittersweet evidence of growth, marking time’s precious passage.

Marcus Aurelius had the eye of a poet. It helped him find joy and gratitude amid the darkness of Rome. As parents, we must cultivate this same eye to appreciate every moment of this surreal—and yes, beautiful—journey we’re on.

Build an Automated Ad Generator with ElevenLabs Flows

Turn a product photo into a finished video ad using ElevenLabs Flows—a new workflow builder that bundles image, video, voice, and music generation in one place.

  1. Open ElevenLabs, click ElevenCreative > Flows, then hit + New Flow. Name it something like [product line] ad template so you can reuse it.
  2. Add an Image Generation node and upload 1–3 product shots. Prompt with a scene: “The product (see references) on a white pedestal, studio product shoot, soft morning light, photorealistic product shot”
  3. Add a Video Generation node, drag a line from the image node’s output into the video node’s start frame, and prompt: “Slow cinematic push-in on the product, soft morning light drifting across the scene, shallow depth of field”
  4. Click Run > Run till here to generate image and video in one go. Swap out prompts and quickly iterate on creatives.

Pro tip: For audio, add a Text-to-Speech or Music node and connect it to a Mix Audio node alongside the video. Duplicate the canvas and swap in new images for other products.

Artemis II Crew Hours From Most Dangerous Moment of Historic Moon Mission

Four NASA astronauts are about to reenter Earth’s atmosphere at 25,000 mph with temperatures hitting 5,000°F. 1% Better

The Orion capsule’s heat shield has been a source of concern—the original version partially failed during an uncrewed 2022 test, with chunks breaking off in over 100 locations. NASA redesigned it for future missions but flew Artemis II with the older version.

If the shield holds, 11 parachutes deploy in precise sequence to slow the capsule from 25,000 mph to under 20 mph before splashdown off San Diego at 8:07 PM ET. The USS John P. Murtha and Navy divers are already on site. Photos from the far side of the moon here.
