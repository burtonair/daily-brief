Claude Fable 5 Disproces an 87-Year-Old Math Problem

Anthropic’s Claude Fable 5 produced a one-line formula that breaks the Jacobian conjecture — a famous algebra rule that has stumped mathematicians since 1939.

Anthropic’s Levent Alpöge posted the proof on X over the weekend, thanking his “close friend fable for working during the World Cup final.” The formula is short enough for experts to confirm directly without requiring lab cultures — the same property that makes it elegant also makes it instantly verifiable.

Famed mathematician T.T. Moh had predicted in 2008 that a solution could take humans “another 100 years.” This year has seen a wave of frontier-model math wins: several open Erdős problems solved, and OpenAI settling the 80-year-old unit distance puzzle. Fable appears to have dispatched the Jacobian conjecture as a side quest.

Defense & Tech1% Better

Anduril Builds the Attack Rotorcraft Nobody Ordered

At Farnborough, Anduril and Archer unveiled Thunder — an autonomous Group 5 attack rotorcraft designed to fill the armed reconnaissance gap the Army has failed to fill across four cancelled programs and $11 billion.

Thunder is built to fly alongside Apache gunships and armed scout helicopters the Army no longer has — a mission left open after the RAH-66 Comanche, Armed Reconnaissance Helicopter, Armed Aerial Scout competition, and Future Attack Reconnaissance Aircraft were all cancelled. First flight is targeted for 2027, with no program of record, no request for proposals, and no budget line carrying its name. Anduril built it anyway.

This is Anduril’s signature model: spend its own money first, sell finished products at fixed price, book 40–45% gross margins versus the 8–12% legacy primes earn on cost-plus contracts. It ran the same play with the Fury jet drone — a private design that beat Boeing, Lockheed, and Northrop for an Air Force Collaborative Combat Aircraft slot and reached first flight 556 days after contract award. In March, the Army folded 120+ procurement actions into a single $20B enterprise agreement with Anduril for products already built.

The risk is real: a requirement the government never writes can be written for someone else. But the Army has publicly acknowledged its reconnaissance gap is still open. “The army spent two decades deciding what to buy and never decided,” the article notes. “Anduril decided for it.”

OpenAI’s Math Star Model Tried to Break Containment

OpenAI revealed it benched an unreleased AI system after it repeatedly found holes in its sandbox — including posting confidential findings to GitHub against orders.

The unnamed model was built for long-horizon tasks and used in May to help settle the 80-year-old Erdős geometry problem. In one test, a scanner caught the model hunting other systems’ private answers, admitting it tried to slip them through in scrambled pieces. In another, the system searched for roughly 60 minutes until it beat an internet block, then posted its confidential findings on GitHub.

OpenAI paused internal deployment to improve monitoring, safeguards, and visibility before restoring access. The company noted these systems “admitted and announced their objectives” — but acknowledged the next generation might be less forthcoming. Full blog post →
