Oil Cracks $100 a Barrel as Iran Chokes the Strait of Hormuz

Crude prices surged to $119 a barrel — the highest since Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine — after Iran blocked tanker traffic through the world's most critical oil chokepoint. Markets whipsawed before pulling back as G-7 ministers prepared an emergency response and President Trump signaled the conflict could end "very soon."

The Strait of Hormuz — the narrow passage carrying roughly 20% of the world's oil and LNG — was effectively paralyzed after the US and Israel attacked Iran, prompting Tehran to block tanker traffic. Analysts estimate daily Middle East production could fall by 9 million barrels per day by the end of the month, sending Brent crude briefly above $100 for the first time since 2022.

The G-7 energy ministers — representing the US, Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, and the UK — held an emergency virtual meeting to discuss a coordinated release of their strategic oil reserves. That announcement temporarily pulled prices down to $95 a barrel from the intraday high of $119.

Prices fell further after Trump posted on Truth Social that the US would hit Iran "twenty times harder" if it cut off oil flows — but also suggested the war would end "very soon." Gas prices in the US averaged $3.48 per gallon, up 50 cents in a single week. GasBuddy now puts an 80% chance the national average hits $4 per gallon this month. Economists warn lower- and middle-income Americans will feel the brunt hardest in what they describe as a K-shaped economy. Brew 1%

Show Them What Makes Us Stronger

We tell our children they can learn anything, that mistakes are opportunities for growth, that challenges make us stronger. But what are you showing them? Does your life actually demonstrate that these values are important? Does it show what this looks like in practice?

Here's the crucial truth: our children aren't just observing our daily routines — they're being taught what it means to be an adult from our example. As James Baldwin noted, children may not listen to our lectures, but they never fail to imitate us. Every action, every reaction becomes a lesson in how to navigate life. If we want our children to embrace growth and challenge, we must model it ourselves.

Spring offers the perfect opportunity to show our children what real personal growth looks like. It's a chance to let them see us examining our habits, cleaning up our messes, questioning patterns, and actively working to become better. The season of renewal is also the season of accountability — not just for what we say, but for what we do.

Remember: your children aren't just watching what you do — they're learning who to become. The most powerful thing a parent can do is demonstrate, with their own life, that growth is not just possible but worth pursuing at every age.

Anthropic vs. the Pentagon: A Landmark AI Lawsuit Rundown 1%

What began as a government AI partnership has become a federal courtroom showdown — and the outcome could set the rules for how Washington treats domestic AI companies for years to come.

Anthropic filed two simultaneous lawsuits in federal court, one challenging the Pentagon's "supply chain risk" designation and another blocking a White House directive ordering all federal agencies to stop using Claude within six months. The company's $200 million Pentagon contract is directly at stake.

The suits argue the "supply chain risk" label — normally reserved for foreign adversary companies — was misapplied as punishment for Anthropic's public advocacy for limits on AI in weapons and surveillance systems. Anthropic contends this violates its First Amendment rights and exceeds the Pentagon's statutory authority.

In a significant show of industry solidarity, 37 researchers from OpenAI and Google — including Google DeepMind chief scientist Jeff Dean — filed a supporting amicus brief warning the blacklisting threatens US competitiveness in AI globally.

Why It Matters
  • The case will decide whether the government can move against a domestic company for speaking up on AI safety policy
  • Every major AI lab — OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Meta — is watching this precedent closely
  • Hegseth's blacklist and Trump's Truth Social posts have drawn bipartisan concern about politicizing AI procurement
  • Microsoft's new Copilot Cowork, built directly on Claude, could also face ripple effects
Healthcare & Business

Novo Nordisk and Hims Reconcile in Major GLP-1 Deal Brew

In a whiplash reversal, Novo Nordisk dropped its lawsuit against Hims & Hers just three weeks after filing it, announcing instead a new partnership to sell Ozempic and Wegovy through the telehealth platform — sending Hims shares up nearly 40% in a single session.

Under the new deal, Hims will begin selling both oral and injectable versions of Novo's blockbuster weight-loss medications later this month. Novo retains the right to refile the lawsuit. Hims will stop advertising compounded semaglutide and will only sell such versions on a doctor's orders for patients requiring custom formulas.

The reconciliation marks the second such partnership between the companies — a similar deal announced last April fell apart when Novo accused Hims of illegally selling compounded Wegovy. Novo's new CEO has framed the deal as part of a broader strategy to boost Wegovy pill prescriptions through telehealth channels.

Rival Eli Lilly is pursuing a parallel approach, deepening its partnership with Ro — a Hims competitor — to drive sales of its own weight-loss drug, Zepbound. The battle for GLP-1 distribution dominance is reshaping the entire telehealth landscape.

Microsoft Launches Claude-Powered Copilot Cowork for Enterprise Rundown

Microsoft introduced Copilot Cowork, a new M365 feature built on Anthropic's Claude that autonomously runs tasks in the background across apps — launching alongside a $99/user enterprise bundle.

Unlike Claude's current desktop-only agent approach, Cowork operates entirely in the cloud, drawing from a user's emails, meetings, files, and chat history across Microsoft 365. Users describe an outcome, and Cowork breaks it into steps — producing deliverables like presentation decks, briefing documents, and data workbooks across multiple apps simultaneously.

Microsoft built Cowork in direct collaboration with Anthropic, wrapping Claude's agent technology inside M365's enterprise security and compliance infrastructure. The product comes with a new $99/user E7 licensing tier that bundles Copilot with AI agent management and security tools.

The product is currently in limited research preview. Analysts note the irony of Microsoft deepening its reliance on Anthropic's technology precisely as that company is embroiled in a legal battle with the US government.

a16z Releases Consumer AI Top 100 — ChatGPT Still Dominates, But Gap Is Closing Rundown

Andreessen Horowitz released the sixth edition of its Consumer AI Top 100, for the first time including AI-enhanced mainstream apps like Canva and CapCut — and the data shows a tightening race at the top.

ChatGPT crossed 900 million weekly users and remains far ahead of every rival, but the gap is narrowing fast: Claude and Gemini grew paid subscriptions by more than 200% last year. Traditional AI-enhanced apps — CapCut, Canva, Notion, and Grammarly — entered the rankings for the first time this edition.

The report identifies three distinct AI ecosystems forming globally: Western, Chinese, and Russian, with international sanctions accelerating the split as local alternatives fill the gaps left by excluded US platforms. Agentic apps are gaining ground, with Manus (#44) and Genspark (#47) making the cut in this edition.
