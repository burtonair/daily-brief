Gas Hits $4 a Gallon for the First Time Since 2022

The average price of gas in the US reached $4.02, surpassing $4 per gallon for the first time in four years as the Iran conflict buckles energy supply chains. Prices have jumped 35% since the war began on Feb. 28. Morning Brew

States along the West Coast saw the biggest increases — California hit $5.91 per gallon. Every 10% rise in fuel prices could push food costs up by 2-3%, according to the New York Times.

The Trump administration promised to release 172 million barrels from strategic reserves, nixed a maritime law to help ease supply chains, and removed sanctions on Russian oil. The EPA also loosened summertime gasoline blend regulations.

Stocks soared yesterday on hopes the Iran war will soon end, after Trump reportedly said he's open to ending the US operation without a full opening of the Strait of Hormuz.

Columnist Daily Dad

They Won't Hesitate. Neither Should You.

They'll cancel school over some ice on the roads. Or a weather report. They won't hesitate to announce a teacher development day. They'll stretch the holiday break to three weeks. They'll send your kid home for a cough.

And then here you are, stressed and guilty, for thinking about giving them an extra day with their grandparents. Or at the beach. Or bringing the family along on a work trip. Or to recover from a cold?

Coaches will cancel practice because they have a work conflict. Or because it's too windy or they'd have to share the court with another team. And you won't let them skip one to spend an afternoon hiking with you?

Commitments are important. Consistency matters. Learning matters. But your family is also a commitment. The whole world is a classroom. Trust your gut here. Value spending time together. You almost certainly will not look back and regret those days you skipped for family time, for adventures, for self-care. Don't hesitate to take it.

Upgrade Your AI Coding Workflow with Better Context

One of the fastest ways to fight context rot in AI coding workflows is to keep documentation in your repo, instead of letting it disappear in a chat thread. Here's how to build better context for any AI coding agent:

  1. Install Marksnip in Chrome and open the documentation page needed for your task — setup guides, SDK docs, API references.
  2. Open the Marksnip extension and click download. A markdown file downloads automatically.
  3. Drag that markdown file into your working repo and save it in a folder like agent-context/ or project-docs/.
  4. Tell your coding agent to read that folder: "Read the files in agent-context first and use them as the source of truth for this task."

Pro tip: Use Marksnip to quickly grab the "meat" of other media like tweets, articles, or even government sites for your AI agent.

McCormick Buys Unilever's Food Business for $45 Billion Morning Brew

The biggest news to hit the condiment industry: spice giant McCormick — owner of Old Bay, French's, and Frank's RedHot — acquires Unilever's food division.

About 70% of Unilever's food sales come from mayo brand Hellmann's and soup company Knorr, which will become part of McCormick's portfolio, subject to regulatory approval. Unilever's shedding of its food business comes as packaged food companies slim down in response to inflation and changing consumer habits.

AI & Tech

Claude Code's Source Code Accidentally Leaks to the World Rundown AI

Anthropic accidentally leaked its AI coding tool's source code to a public registry, exposing 1,900+ files, 500K+ lines of code, and unreleased features.

Developers found 44 feature flags and three unreleased projects, including persistent cross-session memory and a deep-planning system. A GitHub mirror hit 4K+ stars and 7K+ forks in hours. Internal codenames surfaced too — "Capybara" mapping to a Claude 4.6 variant already at v8, plus code that tracks when users swear at Claude. The code also revealed an unreleased AI terminal pet called BUDDY, with 18 species and rarity tiers.

Anthropic called it "human error, not a security breach" with no customer data exposed. Two major leaks in a week — coming days after a 'Mythos' model leak — are a rough stretch for the lab that prides itself on safety.

Business

Allbirds Agrees to Sell All Assets for $39 Million Morning Brew

From a $4 billion IPO valuation in 2021 to a $39 million fire sale. The trendy wool shoe company will sell to American Exchange Group, owner of Ed Hardy.

The eco-friendly shoemaker expanded to 60 stores globally by end of 2023. Now it will shutter all but two outlets. Along the way, it introduced products that didn't sell — including leggings discovered to be see-through after ordering tens of thousands. Sales fell from a high of $297.8M in 2022 to $189.8M last year, with every year as a public company ending in the red.
