Columnist | Daily Dad

It's Not Selfish. It's Essential.

A father's promise to take care of himself — not despite his kids, but because of them.

When you first become a parent, your whole life gets turned upside down. As you scramble to manage everything now on your plate, you tell yourself: When they're a little older, this will calm down. As soon as we get through [whatever phase], everything will go back to normal. It's this delusion — this lie, really — that we use to explain all the things we're putting off.

I'll start going to the gym once they're out of this sleep regression. I'll start eating better once they're less picky about food. My wife and I will get our relationship back on track once they're in school more. It was Seneca who said the one thing fools are always doing is "getting ready to live." Too many parents have that in common — always putting off taking care of themselves. This may come from a good place, but the results are good for no one.

You think your terrible eating habits aren't contributing to your temper? Of course you're grouchy — you feel like crap. You think you're doing your kids a favor by shortening your lifespan? You think you're making them feel safe when you're struggling to get up the stairs? You think you're being a good example when you're letting your relationship bleed out on the dining room table?

You have to take care of yourself. For them. For you. Because you'll be a better parent if you're healthy, happy, and wise. Don't put this off. It's not selfish. It's essential.

Turn Any ChatGPT Image Into an Editable Canva Design

Connect Canva to ChatGPT and move generated images into editable projects — no regenerating from scratch every time you need a new format.

  1. Open ChatGPT desktop, go to Settings > Apps > Browse Apps, and add Canva.
  2. Start a new thread and generate an image (social post, infographic, etc.).
  3. In that same thread, type @Canva then hit Enter. Use this prompt: "Now turn it into a new @Canva project."
  4. To resize for another format: "Okay, now resize that as a new @Canva project for a story post."
  5. Keep editing in ChatGPT — adjust layout, swap text, clean up spacing, or make more versions without starting from a fresh image.

Pro tip: Always mention @Canva explicitly first, or ChatGPT treats it as a text instruction instead of routing it to the app.

The White House ordered Anthropic to block all foreign access to its Mythos and Fable 5 models after Amazon's CEO raised jailbreak concerns with officials. Anthropic suspended global access to comply, potentially slowing a fall IPO and handing rivals an edge.

The Commerce Department issued an "export control directive" requiring Anthropic to remove access for all non-US citizens, even those inside the country. The move was traced to Amazon CEO Andy Jassy, who flagged the potential Fable vulnerability to Treasury officials — a striking move given Amazon's minority stake in the company. Semafor reports the action was partly tied to fears that a China-linked group had accessed Mythos.

Because the order would have also blocked foreign-national Anthropic employees from the models, the company suspended global access entirely. Anthropic later said "no testers have yet been able to find a universal jailbreak" for Fable.

The broader context: CEO Dario Amodei has pushed hard for AI regulation, and it's arriving more chaotically than he probably hoped.
