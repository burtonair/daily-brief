They Haven't Learned This Yet (And That's Wonderful)

We learned the hard way. We learned by experience. We picked up the social cues. We got the message. There's a reason we dress pretty much like everyone else. There's a reason we only sing in private. There's a reason we don't always say what we think. There's a reason we keep our feelings hidden away.

Because we've been hurt. We've been humiliated. We've been told, hey that's not cool and hey, that's not how we do things.

But our kids? They're still unafraid. They are not yet cynical. They have not been taught conformity or what's "cool." And that's part of what makes them so wonderful and fun, isn't it? They'll sing at the top of their lungs — regardless of talent. They have never even considered how they should dress, only what they like. They'll tell a stranger they love them.

We should protect that as long as possible — and if possible, try to learn (or unlearn, rather) from it, too.

Build an AI Case Study Generator

Learn how to use Claude to turn old project files — client emails, metrics exports, memos — into information-rich case studies you can use to win more proposals. Here's the step-by-step guide from The Rundown AI:

  1. Write project wrap memos when a project ends. Give an overview of results and key KPIs. If you're busy, even a quick voice memo is better than nothing.
  2. Create a "Case Study Generator" project in Claude's desktop app with instructions: You are a case study writer for [industry]. Your job is to take raw data and turn it into a case study using the challenge → solution → results framework.
  3. Upload your project memo and prompt Claude with: Generate a case study from these docs. Lead with strongest results in the headline. Use the challenge → solution → results framework. If any section is missing data, flag it with [NEEDS INPUT].
  4. Review and refine. Claude can generate a PDF case study, turn the memo into a slide deck, or format it as a social media carousel post.

Iran Escalates: Drones Strike US Air Defenses Across Six Countries 1% Better

Iran has struck radar and air defense systems across Qatar, the UAE, Jordan, Bahrain, Kuwait, and Saudi Arabia, targeting US and allied infrastructure using low-cost one-way Shahed-136 drones.

The most significant strike damaged a roughly $1 billion early-warning radar at Qatar's Al-Udeid Air Base. A Thaad radar in Jordan was also hit — the US operates only seven Thaad batteries worldwide, with two currently deployed in the region.

US officials report missile attacks have dropped 90% and drone attacks 83% since the conflict began, as American forces reinforce regional defenses with additional equipment and interceptors. Oil prices have spiked above $110 per barrel in response.

US Central Command also announced that a seventh US service member died from injuries sustained in the war on March 1. Iranian ships have been reported departing a Chinese port linked to military chemicals.
