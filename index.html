Columnist Daily Dad

You Never Know When They’ll Find Their Thing

It could happen when they’re very young. It could happen at school. It could happen when they get their first job, or in college, or after their second marriage when they’re rebuilding their life. It might never happen. Your job, obviously, is to try to make it happen as soon as possible. But you never know — you never know when they’re going to find their thing.

In his influential book Mastery, Robert Greene calls this a person’s “life’s task” — the calling that lights someone up, that makes them feel alive, that gives them purpose and meaning and hopefully a career. “At your birth a seed is planted,” he writes. “That seed is your uniqueness. It wants to grow, transform itself, and flower to its full potential. Your Life’s Task is to bring that seed to flower, to express your uniqueness through your work.”

Like so many worthwhile things, this isn’t something a parent can give their children — but we can help them discover it. We can be patient and supportive. We can nudge. We can encourage. We can model the importance of following it ourselves. And we can make sure that when they do find it, we don’t do what so many parents do — which is to criticize, stifle, or undermine it.

We never know when they’ll find it, but we have got to make sure that they don’t give up on it — and neither do we.

Demographics — Census

Miami, LA, and Other Big Cities Are Shrinking as U.S. Population Growth Stalls Morning Brew

Population growth across the U.S. slowed to one of the lowest rates in history, with the country adding just 1.8 million people between July 2024 and July 2025, driven by a rapid fall in international migration.

Of the 2,066 counties that saw growth between 2023 and 2024, 8 in 10 now face slower growth or population decline. Miami-Dade County had a dramatic flip, going from the second-largest population increase in 2024 to a population drop of about 10,000 people.

The Southeast is booming: more than 80% of counties in Tennessee, South Carolina, and Kentucky saw increases in domestic migration, with Georgia claiming three of the top 10 counties for net migration. Texas suburbs are exploding even as people leave Dallas and other major cities.

AI — Policy

Wikipedia Bans AI From Writing Its Articles The Rundown AI

Wikipedia’s volunteer editors voted nearly unanimously (40–2) to ban the use of AI for writing articles, calling it a “pushback against enshittification.”

Editors can still use AI for grammar fixes and translations with human review. The policy’s author said the change could “spark a broader change” and “empower communities on other platforms.” StackOverflow and German Wikipedia have enacted similar bans, while AI text reportedly surpassed human output for the first time in 2025.
