Rev. Jesse Jackson Dies at 84 Brew

Civil rights leader Rev. Jesse Jackson, known for his passionate oration and tireless advocacy, died peacefully yesterday. His family did not share a cause of death, though he was known to be fighting a rare neurological disorder.

Jackson took up the mantle of leader of the Civil Rights Movement after the assassination of Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. A tireless advocate for social change, he founded the Rainbow PUSH Coalition, seeking to create an inclusive force to address economic issues.

Born in 1941 in Greenville, South Carolina, Jackson participated in civil rights sit-ins, worked alongside Martin Luther King Jr., and founded Operation PUSH in 1971.

He made history in 1984 as the first Black man to run a nationwide major-party presidential campaign, later gaining front-runner status briefly in 1988. He ran for president twice and remained a prominent political figure for decades.

How Much Is Left?

Here you are, celebrating a birthday. Here you are, marking another milestone. Here you are, making another notch in the doorframe. As the ancients say, tempus fugit. Time flies.

It sneaks up on you...and even when you catch yourself noticing it, the full weight of the conclusion can escape us. By the time they're 6, most of the truly important things they're going to learn in life have already been learned (or not). At age 9, they're approximately halfway done living with you. By the time your kid is 12, you've already spent the vast majority—around 75%—of time you'll get to spend with them.

It's crazy. It's painful to think how little we have left, how little is left of their childhood, how little time there is left, period. You can let that thought depress you...or clarify. You can let it overwhelm you...or you can see and cherish every single moment.

Knowing these things—thinking about how quickly our time with them goes—can actually be a wonderful gift. It helps us realize how precious all of these years and weeks and minutes with them are, and helps us choose to make the most of them.

Crime Brew

Nine Arrested in $12 Million Louvre Ticketing Scam

Four months after a $100 million art heist, nine arrests were made over an alleged decade-long ticket scam that siphoned $12 million from the famed Paris museum.

Two Chinese tour guides were reusing tickets for tourists split into groups to avoid larger reservation fees. Each day over 10 years, guides moved up to 20 groups through the museum and bribed employees to stay quiet.

Some of the money was invested in real estate in France and Dubai, and more than $1 million in cash was seized.

More trouble: A pipe leak caused water damage to one painting last week, and in November, water damaged hundreds of works in the Egyptian wing.

Tech Rundown

Apple Fast-Tracking Three AI Wearables

Apple is reportedly fast-tracking three camera-equipped AI wearables: smart glasses, a pendant, and new AirPods—all designed to give Siri real-time visual awareness through the iPhone.

The glasses will feature dual cameras, Apple-designed frames, no display, with a production target of late this year ahead of a 2027 launch.

The pendant will act as an always-on camera and mic, internally dubbed the phone's "eyes and ears". Camera-equipped AirPods could ship as early as this year.

All three devices will tie into Apple's revamped Siri, expected to get a chatbot-style interface in iOS 27, powered by Google's Gemini.

Retail Brew

Tide Launches Waterless Detergent Squares Nationwide

Procter & Gamble's Tide is beginning a nationwide roll out of waterless detergent squares, an innovation it believes is the future of the $25 billion laundry care market.

Tide evo is about the size of your palm, with six different layers of woven fibers made from cleaning materials. The fibers dissolve in cold water and come in a recyclable box much smaller and cheaper to ship than other products.

A box of 42 tiles costs $19.99 on Amazon, compared with $12.97 for 42 Pods. The product took about a decade to develop.
