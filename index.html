Iran War Escalation: Houthis Enter, US Troops Arrive, Aluminum Under Siege

The Iran conflict expanded dramatically over the weekend as Yemen's Houthis launched missiles at Israel, 2,500 US Marines arrived in the Middle East, and Iranian strikes hit major aluminum producers in the Gulf. Morning Brew

Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps attacked two aluminum producers in US-allied nations — Emirates Global Aluminium in Abu Dhabi suffered "significant damage" and Aluminum Bahrain (home to the world's largest smelter) is still assessing damage. The Middle East produces about 9% of global aluminum supply, and a Qatar plant has already scaled back to 60% capacity due to gas shortages.

On the military front, Houthis from Yemen launched missiles at Israel, marking their first direct participation in the war. The US now has over 50,000 troops in the region — roughly 10,000 above baseline. President Trump told the Financial Times he wants to "take the oil" and is weighing a military operation to extract 1,000 pounds of uranium from Iran.

Columnist Daily Dad

This Is How They'll Remember

We know the world throws a lot at our kids. We know it's noisy out there and there are competing influences. We know they're pretty forgetful, too. These are kids that can barely remember where their backpack is — how are we supposed to communicate to them the most essential and important truths of life? How do we get them to remember what we tell them?

By simplifying. By being straightforward. By repeating powerful and key ideas time and time again — with both words and actions.

"To be good and to do good, is all we have to do," John Adams would say to his kids. This was also advice he would do his best to live by, and largely succeed.

Be good. Do good. That's the moral imperative of life. That's what we have to do as parents. That's what we have to teach as parents.
