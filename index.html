Iran Reopens the Strait of Hormuz — Markets Soar

Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz open to all commercial shipping after the US brokered a ceasefire pausing Israel's Lebanon campaign. The S&P 500 hit its third consecutive record high, oil futures plunged 13%, and airline stocks surged. Morning Brew

Wall Street cheered the prospect of sustained peace and the unlocking of the waterway that serves as a crucial conduit for fuel, fertilizer, and industrial goods. An estimated 135 million barrels of oil sit stranded in tankers in the Persian Gulf, per Bloomberg Intelligence. Oil futures dropped below $90/barrel for the first time in over a month, and the NYSE Arca Airline Index soared more than 6%.

But the situation remains fluid: Trump said he expects a peace deal "within a day or two" and told CBS that Iran had "agreed to everything," including giving up its enriched uranium. Iran did not confirm those claims, with its top negotiator saying Trump had made seven false claims in one hour. By evening, Iran threatened to close the strait again if the US blockade of Iran-affiliated traffic continued.

Chevron Wins Supreme Court Battle Over Louisiana Coastal Erosion Morning Brew

The Supreme Court ruled 8–0 that Chevron can move an environmental damage lawsuit from state to federal court — where the company is likely to find more sympathy.

The decision jeopardizes a $745 million state court judgment against Chevron to help restore damaged areas along Louisiana's coast. Numerous similar lawsuits pending against oil and gas companies will likely be impacted. "Chevron looks forward to litigating these cases in federal court, where they belong," the company said.

Goop Kitchen's Gluten-Free Empire Hits the East Coast Morning Brew

Gwyneth Paltrow's "certified clean" takeout chain is expanding to New York City after building a loyal following on the West Coast with 11 LA locations and 3 in the Bay Area.

Goop Kitchen offers bowls, wraps, salads, and pizzas made without gluten, seed oils, or processed ingredients — priced in the $15–$25 range. The company reports more than 40,000 customers per week, with half its locations averaging $20,000 per day. Year-over-year sales grew 48% in FY 2024 and 61% in FY 2025. Three Manhattan ghost kitchen locations open Monday, with Miami, Paris, and London potentially next.
