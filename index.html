Columnist Daily Dad Daily Dad

Are You Showing Them How to Be Bigger?

A 10-year-old Jim Lawson was walking down the street when a small child called him a racial slur. Stunned by the hate and the meanness of it, Lawson slapped the boy in the face. When his mother found out, she was understandably worried—but more than that, she wanted her son not to be defined or changed by the hate of the world around him.

"What good did that do, Jimmy?" his mother asked. "We all love you, Jimmy, and God loves you. We all believe in you and how good and intelligent you are. We have a good life and you are going to have a good life. With all that love, what harm does that stupid insult do? It's nothing, Jimmy, it's empty. Just ignorant words from an ignorant child who is gone from your life the moment it was said."

This was a life-changing exchange, as David Halberstam explains in his book The Children. It put Jim Lawson onto his world-changing path of non-violence. It helped him realize that he was above the horrible things that other people said and did.

Lawson's parents gave him the gift of teaching him that he was bigger than the small people who lived around him—that he could be the bigger person and do bigger things. Now, here today, can you do the same for yours?

Build Better Slide Decks with AI

Turn raw data into a presentation-ready slide deck. This works whether you're starting from a spreadsheet, a meeting transcript, a long document, or just bullet points.

  1. Grab the files you want to start with—a CSV export from Shopify or Google Analytics, a meeting transcript from Zoom, or a scope of work document.
  2. Launch Claude, attach the file/doc, and prompt: "I need you to turn these documents into a slide deck outline. Before you start, ask me 5 multiple-choice questions about the purpose, audience, tone, and structure."
  3. Answer the questions to get Claude's outline. Then go to Gamma → Create New → Generate from outline and paste it.
  4. Click Generate, and Gamma will create a full deck you can edit with AI and export as PowerPoint or PDF.

Pro tip: In preview, select "Preserve" and "Concise" options to get the best slide deck.
