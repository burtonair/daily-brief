US Government Bets $2 Billion on Quantum Computing

The Trump administration announced plans to invest $2 billion in nine quantum computing companies in exchange for equity stakes — the most significant government push yet for a technology that could reshape computing by 2035.

Federal dollars, drawn from the Biden-era CHIPS and Science Act, will flow to IBM, D-Wave, Rigetti Computing, Infleqtion, and five others. Their shares jumped as much as 31% on the news. IBM gets the lion's share — $1 billion to set up a dedicated quantum chip manufacturing company in Albany, NY. Quantum chipmaker GlobalFoundries receives $375 million. D-Wave, Rigetti, and Infleqtion each receive $100 million.

Unlike classical computers where bits are either 1 or 0, quantum computers use qubits that can hold both values simultaneously — potentially far more powerful for cybersecurity, drug development, and defense. McKinsey estimates quantum could add $2.7 trillion to the global economy by 2035. The catch: engineers are still fixing qubits' tendency to make errors, making them commercially impractical today. Jensen Huang says real-world applications are ~20 years out; Bill Gates thinks as few as three.

This expands the Trump administration's portfolio of federally-backed strategic bets — including a 10% stake in Intel last year and investments in critical minerals. Critics call it a risky gamble with taxpayer cash on unproven technology.

Columnist | Daily Dad

This Is All You Get

After every exhausting, interminable hour — after all those sleepless nights and terrible twos and tantrums and long afternoons — suddenly it changes.

Suddenly you get your kids for forty-five minutes in the morning. You get them for the fifteen-minute drive from school to practice and then five minutes home. And then, what seems like a whole long evening is actually just an hour or two before bed. You get the weekends, sure, but you share them with soccer games and friends' houses and birthday parties. Or you share them in a custody arrangement or with a boarding school.

The point is: It doesn't last long, this time when they are little. You get them all the time… and then suddenly this is all you get of them. And even this middle period is a way station until the time where you barely get to see them at all — because they move out and on.

So be grateful for what you have, as exhausting as it is. Enjoy it while you have it. Don't fritter it away. Don't waste it. Because soon enough, it will be all but gone. Tempus Fugit. Time flies. Make it a daily practice to ground yourself in the moment, to appreciate this fleeting time with your kids, to make the most of it.

Generate an Agent-Native CLI from Any Website

Learn how to use Printing Press to build agent-friendly tools for hard-to-use APIs like Google Flights or ESPN — giving your AI agent a CLI it can actually work with.

  1. Go to Printing Press and skim the examples or read the GitHub install notes. Install the starter kit: npx -y @mvanhorn/printing-press install starter-pack
  2. Tell your agent (Codex / Claude Code): "Inspect the starter kit. List the tools, read their help output, and try one safe read-only command from ESPN or flight-goat. Tell me which tool worked and which command you ran."
  3. Ask the agent to install the Printing Press binary: go install github.com/mvanhorn/cli-printing-press/v4/cmd/printing-press@latest
  4. Now the agent can build its own CLI tools. Start with a public-data-rich site: "Use Printing Press to generate read-only commands for [WEBSITE]. Propose the useful commands first, then generate and test them."

Pro tip: If your agent struggles with a specific MCP or integration, just tell it to build its own commands with Printing Press. You just need an API key.

Community workflow from Loïc R. in London: "I tore my ACL on both knees playing football. As a sprinter, I've never done long-distance running — but in one month I'm racing a 5-hour multi-sport adventure race. I asked ChatGPT to build a custom 4-week training program factoring in my past injuries and current strengths. After every running session, I use voice to capture how I did and how my body felt. ChatGPT adjusts my program based on time left and how to maximize it while minimizing injury risk. A personalized coach in my pocket for a very specific challenge."

OpenAI Drops Major Codex Upgrades: Appshots, Goal Mode & Locked Computer Use

OpenAI shipped another wave of Codex upgrades, including the ability to attach live app windows to threads, a new goal mode that runs for hours or days, and locked computer use that operates your Mac while the screen is off.

Appshots lets Mac users attach any open app window — screenshot, text, and content — to a Codex thread with a Command-Command press. Goal mode (now in the app, IDE extension, and CLI) lets users set a goal and let Codex work toward it autonomously. Locked computer use, triggered via a second device, lets Codex use desktop apps even after the Mac is locked. Advanced annotation mode lets you directly describe what you want changed on a web page and instantly preview the results.

OpenAI is betting these improvements will help it close the gap with Anthropic on developer mindshare, and maintain its lead over xAI and Google as competition for AI coding assistants intensifies.

California Becomes First State to Formally Study AI's Impact on Workers

Governor Newsom signed an executive order directing state agencies to study AI-driven job losses and develop worker protections — arriving one day after Meta laid off 8,000 employees to offset AI investments.

The order directs agencies to explore severance standards, worker ownership models, and universal basic capital. Within 90 days, California will launch a dashboard tracking AI's job impact. Within 180 days, agencies will pitch WARN Act updates for faster layoff alerts. By October 15, the state will review how unions are negotiating AI adoption and explore directing AI revenue toward public benefit.

The timing is hard to ignore: over 70,000 jobs have already vanished in 2026. California is home to 33 of the world's top 50 AI companies — so it's both the epicenter of the disruption and the logical place to study it.

Victoria's Secret Goes VSXY — A New Era of Sexy or Just a Ticker Change?

Victoria's Secret & Co is changing its NYSE ticker to "VSXY" on June 2, part of CEO Hillary Super's turnaround effort to reclaim the brand's position as the mall's dominant lingerie player.

At its 2019 peak, Victoria's Secret commanded nearly one-third of the bra market and $8.1B in revenue. By 2024, market share shrank to 18% and sales fell to $6.18B, as Savage X Fenty, Skims, and American Eagle captured customers with more inclusive branding. Super's rebound is showing traction — the company reported its third straight quarter of same-store sales growth and is projecting $6.85B in full-year sales. The old "VSCO" ticker retires June 2.
