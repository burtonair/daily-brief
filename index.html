AI & Tech

China’s AI is catching up while the US pumps the brakes on its own Brew Rundown

Researchers say China’s Zhipu AI can now match Anthropic’s powerful Mythos model on some tasks — the same model the US government forced offline earlier this month. The capability gap has narrowed to nearly nothing, and US businesses are already switching to cheaper Chinese alternatives.

The uncomfortable reality: while the US restricts its own frontier AI models, China hasn’t stopped building. Zhipu AI — which matches Mythos on certain security and coding benchmarks — is freely available to the public worldwide. Some industry watchers say banning US AI models while continuing to sell semiconductor chips to China only accelerates its ability to compete.

The policy bind is real. US businesses are turning to cheaper Chinese models from Zhipu and DeepSeek. Even Microsoft is reportedly exploring how to offer Chinese models to consumers on its platforms — a striking development given the national security framing around these restrictions.

On Friday, Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick partially restored Mythos access to “certain trusted partners.” The same day, OpenAI released GPT-5.6 to a small set of government-approved recipients. In a blog post, OpenAI said the gatekeeping should not “become the long-term default” as it keeps powerful tools from developers, enterprises, and cyber defenders who need them.

Coming soon: The Trump administration is expected to allow Anthropic to bring its Fable 5 model back online as soon as this week. Austria separately proposed that Anthropic relocate to the EU, citing “legal certainty, market access, capital, and a set of values that suits this company.”

It’s Not Only Happening to Everyone Else

You see that house down the street put up a sign about their graduating senior. Your daughter’s friend’s older brother just started medical school. You see a picture of some celebrity’s six- or seven-year-old… and realize you remember hearing the announcement of their pregnancy on a supermarket shelf.

They were kids, just recently! You remember when they were babies! But now they’re grown up. A handful of years — the years between kid and adult, toddler and teen — went by like that.

It’ll hit you sometimes when you see it happen to other families… as if the same thing wasn’t happening to your family. It’s just that you saw your kids every day — you got lost in the day-to-dayness. It’s only when reminded via someone else that it hits you: how much time has passed, how fast they are growing up, how little childhood is left.

It’s not only happening to everyone else. It’s happening to you and your family and your kids. Remember that — and do not let these precious remaining years of their childhood slip by.

Economy & Culture 1%

“Funflation” is busting household budgets this summer

Live entertainment has become a luxury item. One Virginia couple saved for a year and spent nearly $10,000 at the World Cup. World Cup resale tickets averaged $1,084; NBA Finals seats hit $4,100. Fans are taking on debt to attend.

Pandemic-era pent-up demand combined with heavy spending from wealthy fans has bid prices up across the board. Lenders like Affirm and Klarna are helping fans cover the tabs — raising questions about how sustainable this demand actually is.

Cracks are showing. Fans now track “blue dot fever” — the unsold face-value seats visible on venue maps. Some artists have canceled soft-selling shows. States won an antitrust verdict against Live Nation and want to break up Ticketmaster.

AI Economy Rundown

The AI economy banked $110B last year — growing 3x faster than the internet ever did

New Exponential View research shows generative AI hit $110B in revenues in 2025 and is on track to reach $175B. In 2023, it took 180 days to add $1B in total AI revenue. Now it takes two days.

Quarterly growth has held at ~35% for over a year. Every 10% token price drop drives usage up 12–18%. Global token volumes crossed 30 quadrillion per month — with agents consuming 1,200x more compute than chat interactions.

AI has restarted US electricity growth after 16 years of flat generation. Data centers are expected to account for ~55% of new electricity demand by 2030. Despite all this, most S&P 500 companies still haven’t reported measurable results from AI investment — a gap research author Azeem Azhar says mirrors how electricity made light 99.97% cheaper before it showed up in the numbers. AI still represents just 0.42% of GDP.
