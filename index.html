Midjourney’s wild medical hardware pivot Rundown

Midjourney — the AI image company — just unveiled a full-body scanner that lowers users through an ultrasound ring for a 60-second body map, wrapped in a spa experience. Possibly the most surprising AI product launch of the year.

The Midjourney Scanner uses underwater ultrasonic sensors to image the full body in 60 seconds. Founder David Holz says it rivals an MRI’s detail in a fraction of the time, built in partnership with ultrasound-chip maker Butterfly Network.

The first Midjourney Spa opens in 2027 in San Francisco’s Union Square, pairing around 10 scanners with saunas, cold plunges, and hot tubs. It’s a medical device tucked inside a wellness destination — which is either the future of preventive care or the most ambitious brand extension in tech history.

Why it matters: the startup’s other mysterious “TBA” products gain even more intrigue after this reveal. A company that turned text into surreal images is now trying to turn ultrasound into full-body health maps. Whatever Midjourney is building, it’s clearly not a single-product company anymore.

Columnist | Daily Dad

Your Worries Are Your Problem

Of course you worry. You care about them more than anything in the world. Our kids are, as Joan Didion famously said, our hostages to fortune. They make us vulnerable. They are our hearts running around outside our body.

So yeah, we worry. We want to make sure they get home safe. We want to make sure they’re feeling OK. We want to make sure they keep their grades up, don’t do dumb stuff, that they’re successful and happy and secure. We can’t stand the thought of anything happening to them.

But the worry — the anxiety — is our problem. It is our cross to bear. Not theirs. So we have to shut up about it. We have to keep it to ourselves (or to our spouses or our therapists or our own parents). We don’t get to saddle our kids with it. We definitely don’t get to bother them with it.

Let them live their life. Let them make mistakes. That’s the whole point. We’re trying to raise self-sufficient humans — kids that will eventually be independent, competent people. And that means we have to let them experience their lives, and learn to navigate the real world in their own way.

Save hours on meeting prep with Google Gemini

Use Google Workspace Studio to turn upcoming calendar meetings into automatic prep time — a Gemini-written brief lands in your calendar before the call starts.

  1. Go to Google Workspace Studio and create a new flow with Based on a meeting.
  2. Set the trigger to run 10–15 minutes before the meeting so the prep block lands before the call starts.
  3. Add an Ask Gemini step that creates a short prep brief: meeting context, open loops, suggested agenda, and questions to ask.
  4. Add Create a doc next. Use the meeting title variable + “Meeting Prep” for the doc name; Gemini output for the body.
  5. Add Block time last. Use the meeting title and start time variables, then place the doc link variable in the prep block description.
  6. Add a fake meeting to Google Calendar, click Test workflow, confirm the prep block lands before the meeting and the brief link works, then turn the flow on.

Going further: Start with one recurring meeting type, test the flow, then expand to client calls, hiring interviews, and exec reviews.

The talent war: OpenAI just landed Google’s transformer architect

Noam Shazeer started at Google in 2000 and co-authored “Attention Is All You Need” in 2017 — the paper that shaped nearly every modern chatbot. He left for Character AI, Google paid $2.7B to win him back, and now he’s gone to OpenAI.

Shazeer was a VP and co-lead on Google’s Gemini, his expertise helping bring the models near the frontier after falling behind ChatGPT. His announcement on X confirms he’s joining OpenAI.

The talent wars have been quieter than last summer’s Meta poaching spree, but this is a significant move. Where top AI researchers go is always a signal — and in 2026, the answer has consistently been Anthropic or OpenAI. Google is now down both its $2.7B investment and the researcher it was meant to retain.

Databricks unveils agentic data stack at Data + AI Summit

Databricks launched LTAP — a unified lake for running AI apps and analytics — plus CustomerLake, an AI-run customer data platform.
