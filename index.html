Columnist | Daily Dad

They Are the Perfect Child

Yes, they sometimes throw tantrums. They make messes. They break stuff. They hit their siblings. They refuse to eat what they just asked you to make — and then scream in hunger a few hours later. And yet, they are perfect.

They are behaving perfectly like children. Children throw tantrums. Children make messes. Children don't listen. Children are figuring out boundaries and needs and the volume of their voice. All of this is to be expected — the alarming thing would be if your child didn't do any of these things.

As the newsletter has said before, your kids are doing their job. They are doing what kids do and have always done. Your job is to be patient and understanding — to help guide them into better behaviors as they get older, as they begin to understand cause and effect, consequences and responsibilities.

Your job is not to take any of this personally, and it is definitely not to make them feel like there is something wrong with them for doing totally age-appropriate things. Got it?

AI Research Rundown AI

Stanford Study: AI Legal Tutors Beat Law Faculty 75% of the Time in Blind Test

Sixteen contract law professors judged anonymized answers from peers and from Google's AI systems — and chose the AI outputs three out of four times, across 2,918 matchups on real contract-law office-hours questions.

The test focused on settings requiring judgment and critical thinking, not one correct answer. Faculty chose Gemini 2.5 Pro and NotebookLM 75% of the time; only a single top professor stayed level with the models. An extended AI-judge ranking of nine more systems put Claude Opus 4.7 at the top — with all models beating the professors.

Early models like GPT-4 were already passing the bar exam. This study puts AI into tougher, more subjective territory — and the results weren't close.
