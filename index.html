GPT-5.2 Makes Original Physics Discovery Rundown

OpenAI's GPT-5.2 independently discovered a mathematical formula in theoretical physics and formally proved it was correct—marking what the company calls AI's first original contribution to theoretical physics.

The research tackles a problem in particle physics that was assumed solved. GPT-5.2 found the existing answer was wrong and proposed a correct one. A specialized research version autonomously wrote the math proof in 12 hours, verified by physicists from Harvard, Cambridge, and Princeton.

Harvard physicist Andrew Strominger, who verified the work, said the AI "chose a path no human would have tried." OpenAI's Kevin Weil is credited as a co-author on the paper.

Why it matters: The "can AI actually think?" debate isn't going away, but the real conversation is shifting from if AI can contribute to science to how fast it rewrites what we thought we already knew.

They Can Make A Difference

Today, as we mark George Washington's birthday in the U.S., it's worth thinking about why we celebrate important dates like these—from a parenting perspective.

Why do we tell our kids stories? Why do we tell them about history? Teach them about George Washington, Martin Luther King, Cincinnatus, Florence Nightingale, Jesus, Marcus Aurelius? Because it matters.

As Longfellow wrote: "Lives of great men all remind us / We can make our lives sublime, / And, departing, leave behind us / Footprints on the sands of time…"

We are trying to teach our kids the most important and pressing lesson of all: That they can make a difference. That they can change the world. There's a part of that which can feel a bit cheesy, even naive. But it matters. Because if they don't believe it, what hope is there of change? Of progress?

We tell our kids stories to inspire them. That's what bedtime reading is about—not just tiring them out so they'll go to sleep. The point of history class isn't to berate them with the evil deeds of old dead white guys. We want them to know: People can have an impact. People can lead wonderful lives. People can leave their footprints on the sands of time. Not just people, but your kids can do this. And in helping them realize that, you are making your own impact.

Launch an Outbound Calling Agent in 15 Minutes

Build an AI agent that makes real sales calls on your behalf. Create the agent, get a $1 phone number, and upload a list of contacts for it to call—with the whole setup taking about 15 minutes.

  1. Create a new AI agent in ElevenLabs. Choose a voice and add system instructions. Include details about your business, offer, and the goal of the call.
  2. Sign up on Twilio (free + $15 in credits) and buy a phone number for $1.20. Copy the number, Account SID, and Auth Token from the dashboard.
  3. Connect Twilio to ElevenLabs. Go to Phone Numbers in ElevenLabs, click Create New, and paste in your number, SID, and Auth Token.
  4. Click Outbound in ElevenLabs, create a "batch" with Telephony as the channel, and download a CSV template. Add in leads' numbers, then upload the CSV back. Start calling with the agent, test it, or schedule calls for later.

Pro tip: Toggle on "Transfer to Number" in your agent's Tools to have it patch hot leads through to your phone.

AI Brew

The Tech Industry Plunges Into a Crisis of Memory

Water and electricity are pleased to welcome memory chips to the growing list of resources that have become hotter and pricier than ever due to AI demand.

The Big Three memory-chip producers—Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron—can't meet demand for both AI data centers and consumer electronics. The shortage stems from bad timing and the feast-or-famine nature of the memory biz.

Rising action: Pandemic-era stockpiling drove memory prices higher. But buying dried up in 2022-2023, prompting production cuts of up to 50%. When demand rebounded in late 2023, producers couldn't shake the bad memory of getting burned on supply.

Big picture: Until solutions are found, most smartphones, PCs, gaming consoles, and routers will get more expensive. Some experts think AI PCs running operations locally might ease data center demand.

IBM Wants to Take Entry-Level Jobs to the Next Level

As AI improves, there's a fear it could take a wrecking ball to entry-level positions. But IBM wants to use that wrecking ball to its advantage, announcing plans to triple its entry-level hiring in the US this year.

Entry-leveling the playing field: Junior software developers will do less coding and spend more time with clients. HR staffers will let chatbots field questions, then step in when needed.

It's not just IBM: Cloud platform Dropbox is bulking up its internship and new-graduate programs by 25%. Chief People Officer Melanie Rosenwasser said younger workers are better at using AI than most, a skill the company wants to leverage.

Headwinds: Since early 2023, entry-level job postings in the US are down 35%, per analytics firm Revelio Labs.

Billionaire Moon Race: Musk vs. Bezos

Elon Musk announced this week that SpaceX is shifting focus from Mars to building a lunar city, directly competing with Jeff Bezos's Blue Origin.

Musk cited concerns about Mars colony sustainability, noting the moon could become self-sufficient in under 10 years versus Mars's 20-plus year timeline due to planetary alignment cycles.

SpaceX conducted 165 launches last year and plans to go public in 2026, while Blue Origin aims for its first lunar cargo flight this year. Bezos responded by posting a tortoise image on X, referencing Blue Origin's steady-progress approach.
