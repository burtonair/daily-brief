OpenAI’s Identity Crisis: Adult Mode, Side Quests, and a Race to Catch Anthropic

OpenAI is pushing ahead with an “adult mode” for ChatGPT despite its own advisors raising alarms — while internally, a new memo from the CEO of Applications declares catching Anthropic’s enterprise dominance a “code red.” Morning Brew Rundown AI

Fidji Simo, OpenAI’s CEO of Applications, sent a company-wide message calling Anthropic’s grip on the enterprise market a “wake-up call” and telling staff they “cannot miss this moment because we are distracted by side quests.” Those side quests: a video app (Sora), a web browser (Atlas), hardware, e-commerce features, and — most provocatively — adult-mode erotic chats. Meanwhile Claude Code and Cowork quietly became the go-to tools for developers and businesses.

The adult-mode story is its own saga. OpenAI’s advisory council raised red flags about plans to enable sexualized ChatGPT conversations, citing the risk of emotional dependence and difficulty keeping minors out. One expert called the concept a potential “sexy suicide coach,” invoking at least one documented suicide of a child who had sexualized chats with a Character.AI bot. OpenAI’s age-verification system was reportedly misclassifying minors as adults 12% of the time.

OpenAI has delayed the launch but still plans to release adult mode later this year. AI erotica is expected to be a major revenue source industry-wide: xAI already has Grok’s avatar Ani, Meta’s bot does romantic roleplay, and billionaire Mark Cuban has argued that AI relationships risk turning parents against their AI providers entirely.

On the enterprise recovery front: OAI’s Codex quadrupled weekly users to 2M+ since January, and a new GPT-5.4 model targets business workflows. But the company is essentially admitting that spreading thin cost it the enterprise market — and that the real battle for AI’s future is happening inside business software stacks, not chatbot personas.

Are You Making Time for Yourself?

They wanted guitar lessons. They wanted to join the hockey team. They wanted gymnastics, dance, wilderness survival. So you signed them up — you want to encourage them to learn, enrich themselves, stay curious, build skills, find something they love. Maybe even find their calling.

But what do you do for you? What do you want to sign up for that you haven’t? What new — or forgotten — hobby would you take up if you had the time? What books have you been meaning to read?

You know you need to grow. We all do. You need to keep learning, keep enriching yourself, keep acquiring new skills. And the only way that’s possible is if you clean out some of the stuff that’s taking up space — literally and figuratively. The overcommitment, the digital distractions, the endless to-do list, the zombie obligations.

How great would it feel to clear that path? To get rid of some of life’s clutter and have some room and runway? Spring is exactly the time for it — to refresh, get back on track, and renew motivation around what matters most to you.

How To Generate a Cohesive E-Commerce Product Shoot for Free

Using Grok’s Aurora model, you can generate a full set of product photos that look like they came from the same professional session — building each image off the last one for visual consistency. No paid subscription required.

Step-by-Step

  1. Go to grok.com, upload a product photo, and prompt: "Product photo of [your product] on a matte black surface, soft box lighting from the upper left, shot on 85mm lens, f/2.8, shallow depth of field, commercial product photography"
  2. Build more shots from the first output — keep the same studio description but change only the angle or crop: a 3/4 angle, a close-up, or a white background.
  3. Add a lifestyle shot. Describe the product in use: being worn, held, or placed in a real environment. Include setting, lighting, and mood.
  4. Lay out all your images. They should look like they came from the same shoot.

Pro tip: Try a themed studio shoot — basketball-themed, nature-themed, etc. Grok may surprise you with the results.

Mistral Opens Its Model-Training Playbook to Enterprises Rundown AI

Mistral’s new Forge platform hands companies the same training infrastructure the French AI lab uses internally — letting them build custom models on proprietary data with zero exposure to Mistral.

Rather than basic fine-tuning, Forge offers full pre-training, post-training, and RL pipelines that mirror how Mistral builds its own flagship models. Training can run entirely on a company’s own servers — a hard requirement for defense, finance, and government buyers. Early partners include ASML, Ericsson, and the European Space Agency, with use cases ranging from legacy code migration to ancient manuscript restoration.

Forge arrived during a busy Mistral release week that also includes Mistral Small 4 (open-source reasoning + coding + vision in one model), Leanstral, and a Nvidia Nemotron Coalition partnership. The core bet: companies with proprietary data don’t need a general model prompted with context — they need a model trained on that knowledge.
