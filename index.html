CEO Confidence Collapses — Economy Enters Red Zone

A new Conference Board survey of 141 Fortune 500 CEOs returned a Q2 confidence score of 47 — a 12-point drop from Q1 and below the 50 threshold that signals pessimism. It’s the clearest signal yet that the executive class has lost faith in the near-term outlook.

Only 8% of CEOs said conditions worsened in Q1. By Q2, that figure exploded to 47%. Forty percent expect things to get worse over the next six months — up from 13% in the prior survey. Consumer confidence also dropped in May, reinforcing a University of Michigan report last week showing consumer sentiment at an all-time low.

The worry list is long. The GDP for Q1 was revised down from 2% to 1.6% on “downward revisions to investment and consumer spending.” The 12-month inflation rate hit 3.8% — a three-year high, driven partly by high gas prices.

At the Fed, new Chair Kevin Warsh is finding his desired rate cut increasingly hard to justify. Per CME FedWatch, there is now a 37% chance of a rate hike in December, as central bankers grow skeptical that AI-fueled growth will cool inflation on its own.

Columnist | Daily Dad

They Won’t Love This Forever

Kids become obsessed — with dinosaurs and trash trucks, Disney characters and mermaids. For a period, our lives revolve around these infatuations. Then, one day, the obsession is simply gone.

We take them to airshows because we know seeing that many planes will blow their mind. We take them to train museums. We point out every cow, construction site, and fire engine. We put the song on repeat. We sing it on command. Because it makes them happy, because it keeps their interest, because even though it sometimes drives us crazy, we get into it too.

But we can forget that this is a temporary madness. One day they will not even look up when we tell them we hear a helicopter. They will not respond to the song. They will shrug their shoulders when you tell them Mickey is on TV.

None of it lasts, and soon enough you will miss it. So embrace the insanity. Enjoy the madness while it’s here.

How To | The Rundown AI

Use Codex to Build a Functional Game in One Prompt

In this guide, you’ll learn how to use Codex /goal to build a small browser game without nudging the agent every few minutes. The game is the demo — the real move is giving Codex a finish line it can work toward autonomously.

  1. Open Terminal and enable goals: codex features enable goals. Think of a short, simple game idea with rules you can test objectively.
  2. If the idea feels fuzzy, ask ChatGPT to rewrite it in 100 words or fewer with objective tests. If it can’t, the scope is too big for one /goal.
  3. Paste the description after /goal and follow the checklist Codex creates. For a simple game, expect about 5–6 minutes of building, testing, and fixing.
  4. Give testing feedback as another /goal. Be specific: “Add distinct animations for every action the user can take.”

Pro tip: This works beyond games. List three business processes, define a metric that proves each works, then ask Codex to improve the process against that metric.

Community workflow from Gabriela in Austin, TX: “I uploaded several selfies to ChatGPT and asked it to perform a color palette analysis, then moved to hairstyles and outfits. I had it turn the results into custom instructions for a Project within ChatGPT just for style. Now I use that Project for feedback on daily outfits, haircuts, and new clothes. I feel more confident and more myself.”

Meta Launches Paid Subscriptions for Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp

Fresh off a 10% layoff, Meta is officially rolling out paid tiers across its apps to diversify beyond its $55+ billion advertising business.

Facebook/Instagram Plus: $3.99/month — see story rewatch counts, extend stories beyond 24 hours, search story viewers. Business plans run up to $49.99/month for access to human customer service agents. WhatsApp Plus: $2.99/month — custom themes, pin more conversations, premium stickers.

Meta also introduced Meta AI subscription tiers at $7.99 and $19.99/month for more compute power; the company didn’t specify limits for non-paying users. Meta’s non-advertising revenue was $1.29B last quarter — a long way from $55B+ in ad sales, but the company needs to monetize its $600B AI infrastructure investment.

Apple’s AI Siri Finally Takes Shape — Rebuilt on Gemini, Lives in Dynamic Island

Bloomberg got a glimpse of Apple’s long-overdue AI Siri: a dedicated ChatGPT-style app inside Dynamic Island, rebuilt on Google Gemini, with support for third-party AI agents.

Siri will run AI-powered web searches similar to Perplexity, with answers surfacing as rich cards in Dynamic Island. Swiping deeper opens a full Siri app where users can route queries to external AI models. The revamped Siri also lands in the Camera app with advanced AI-based photo editing.
