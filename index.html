Housing & AI Economy Morning Brew

AI Money Is Reshaping the Housing Market — SF Median Hits $2.15M Record

Luxury home sales are surging in San Francisco and the Hamptons on the back of free-flowing AI industry wealth, while the rest of the housing market stalls. A new Redfin report shows luxury prices up 3.6% year-over-year — nearly triple the gain for non-luxury homes.

The median US luxury home sale price jumped to $1.39 million from February to April 2026 — a 3.6% year-over-year increase compared to just 1.4% for non-luxury properties. The epicenter is the San Francisco Bay Area: luxury prices there have risen 13.4% since ChatGPT launched in November 2022, while less-expensive properties in the same market have actually fallen 3.8%.

In March, the median San Francisco house price hit a record $2.15 million. The rental market is just as punishing — the median one-bedroom jumped over 21% in a single year to above $4,000, the biggest jump in the US. Two-bedrooms average $5,500/month, tying New York for the most expensive rent in the country.

On the other coast: the Hamptons hit a record median of $2.34 million in Q4 2025. Realtors say deep-pocketed AI workers are paying all cash and shelling out $50,000 to ensure fiber internet is top-tier. Luxury sales are also running hot in Tampa, West Palm Beach, and Miami. Meanwhile, AI itself doesn’t expect mortgage rates to drop below 5% in the next five years.

Columnist | Daily Dad

If Money Could Talk, This Is What It Would Say

You sweated for it. You worked for it. You saved it. You invested it. But if your money could talk, it would say one thing: spend me on your kids — and do it now, not later.

This is not the same as “rack up credit card debt” or “spoil them rotten.” It’s saying that right now your kids are young, right now is for certain — so don’t put things off. If you were ever going to move to the neighborhood with better schools, take that family trip to Europe, get them that new bike, or help them with their bills — now is the time. Not when you’re older. Not when things settle down. Not someday. Now.

The book Die With Zero makes this case powerfully: the shockingly late age at which people inherit money from parents or grandparents is often when they’re already financially secure — long past the time they needed the help most.

So if you’re in a position to spend, to support, to give — well, now is the time. Not later. Now.

How To | The Rundown AI

Teach Your AI Agent to Edit Like You

In this guide, you’ll learn how to teach Codex or Claude your editing style by turning finished edits into reusable writing rules. The real move is the loop: draft, snapshot, edit, compare, and improve.

  1. Create a project folder with emails/, rules/, and skills/. Inside emails/, add draft/ and final/ so the agent can tell what’s active and what’s approved.
  2. Prompt Codex/Claude Code: “Interview me about the editorial rules I use for marketing emails. Ask about audience, tone, subject lines, claims to avoid, CTA style, and examples I like. Turn my answers into rules/editorial-rules.md.”
  3. Have the agent draft one email using the rules and save two copies: a working draft in emails/draft/ and an identical snapshot that never changes.
  4. Tell it to turn the draft-and-snapshot process into a skill. Edit the draft into the approved final and ask the agent to compare and update the editorial rules.

Pro tip: Set an automation that scans approved emails once a day, compares draft with final, updates the rules, and changes the status so drafts don’t run twice.

Community workflow from Michel in Solana Beach, CA: “I run a wine import firm with an archaic backend. Salespeople had to call me to verify inventory on specific SKUs — slowing everything down. Using Claude, Cursor, and Claude Code, I built a web-based one-click inventory verification app. Now, with a single button tap, salespeople get instant up-to-date inventory levels. It’s given the team a renewed sense of control and sped up sales conversions.”

Robinhood to Let AI Make Trades and Buy Things for You

Robinhood announced Agentic Trading and an Agentic Credit Card, letting users connect their AI agents of choice to execute investment and spending plans with limited human interaction.

OpenAI Foundation Commits $250M to Help Workers Navigate AI Disruption

The nonprofit arm of OpenAI is funding grants, partnerships, and direct programs to support workers, communities, and economies as AI reshapes the labor market.

The Foundation is exploring systems to track how AI value flows beyond wages — what people can actually do and access. Continual AI The Rundown AI

Trajectory Launches With $15M to Build AI That Keeps Learning on the Job

The ex-DeepMind and Apple startup captures user corrections and retries to continuously post-train models — targeting the holy grail of AI that compounds in quality from real-world use.

Trajectory captures the corrections, retries, and edits that users make and uses them to post-train models on a weekly basis — targeting hourly updates or even per-interaction eventually. The $15M seed was led by Conviction and Bessemer; the team comes from DeepMind, OpenAI, Apple, Meta SuperIntelligence Lab, and Scale AI.

Early customers include Clay, Harvey, Decagon, and Rogo. If Trajectory cracks continual learning at scale, companies will end up with AI tools that get measurably better almost instantly after user feedback — much like humans do.
