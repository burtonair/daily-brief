Trump Gets Down to Business in Beijing

Morning Brew

President Trump arrived in Beijing for a two-day summit with Xi Jinping — the first sitting US president to visit China in nearly a decade — bringing Tesla’s Elon Musk, Nvidia’s Jensen Huang, and a cohort of Wall Street and tech executives on Air Force One.

The summit could be the first of potentially four meetups between Trump and Xi this year, with the agenda covering AI, Iran, and trade. In bilateral talks to kick things off, Trump said his business delegation “look forward to trade and doing business, and it’s going to be totally reciprocal.” Xi responded: “We should be partners, not rivals.”

The biggest US wins are expected in what Sen. Steve Daines called “Boeing, beef, and beans” — US soybean farmers and beef producers have been locked out of the Chinese market for a year. In a hopeful early sign, China renewed import licenses for hundreds of US beef plants today.

Xi is expected to press Trump on the US’ $14 billion proposed weapons sale to Taiwan — a move Xi warned could put “the entire US-China relationship in an extremely dangerous situation.” China also wants more access to US advanced chips, while the US wants rare earth minerals and metals access restored.

The trade war truce from last October will likely be extended, and officials have hinted that a new “Board of Trade” will be established. Read the full agenda breakdown.

You’d Give It All For This

It wasn’t that long ago — and you didn’t fully appreciate it while you were in it — but then it hits you. You see someone carrying a sleeping kid in their arms. You see a gaggle of little ones trailing their parents like ducks. You find some clothes that don’t fit them anymore. That wave of nostalgia hits you: where did that kid go?

Comedian Bert Kreischer was talking with Pete Holmes and got emotional. He had walked into Holmes’s bathroom and seen two little stools with names on them — the kind toddlers use to reach the sink. He hadn’t seen those in his own home in twelve years. “I would give it all,” he said. He’d give it all to be back in a house like that, with kids that young.

Those years feel exhausting when you’re in them. Loud. Sticky. Chaotic. You count down the minutes to bedtime. You look forward to it being quiet. And then one day, it is. The house gets quiet. Those little stools disappear. The chaos organizes itself into independence. Now, you miss what you just rushed through.

You’re living in moments right now that your future self will ache for. You can’t slow it down or freeze this season of life. But you can be here now. Put the phone down. Stay a little longer at bedtime. Say yes to one more story. Because one day you’ll walk into a quiet house, see something small that used to matter, and think the same thing Bert did: “I would give it all.”

Create Content with Claude Code and Higgsfield

Learn how to connect Higgsfield to Claude Code with the Higgsfield CLI, then use Claude Code to send one image prompt to several AI image models at once for side-by-side comparison.

Step-by-step:

  1. Create a new project folder, install the Higgsfield CLI (npm install -g @higgsfield/cli), authenticate (higgsfield auth login), and add the Higgsfield skill (npx skills add higgsfield-ai/skills) for Claude Code.
  2. Open Claude Code in the same folder and ask it to inspect higgsfield.ai/cli, verify the installation, and list available image models.
  3. Give Claude Code an image prompt and tell it to run it across six models, save outputs into a higgsfield-model-test folder, and generate a comparison.md file with notes for each result.
  4. Pick the best direction, then ask Claude Code to refine the winning prompt or run one more comparison round.

Pro tip: If setup gets confusing, ask Claude Code to check Node/npm and give a full walkthrough. Higgsfield also supports video, so you can try this for short clips.

Morning Brew

AI & Environment

Utah Is Not Jazzed About a $100B AI Data Center

Morning Brew

County commissioners approved plans for a 40,000-acre AI data center called Stratos in northwest Utah, despite nearly 4,000 resident complaints about environmental damage and rising energy costs.

The project — helmed by Kevin O’Leary — would require 9 gigawatts of power, more than the entire state currently uses. Environmentalists warn it could destroy the Great Salt Lake ecosystem and raise local temperatures by up to 28°F at night. A group called the Box Elder Accountability Referendum is collecting 5,422 signatures to trigger a November vote to reverse the decision.

AutoScientist Automates What Only a Few Thousand People Know How to Do

The Rundown AI

Ex-Cohere VP Sara Hooker’s startup Adaption introduced AutoScientist, a system that automatically customizes AI models for specific domains by iterating on both training data and learning settings.

In internal tests, AutoScientist outperformed expert-tuned models by 35% on average, with success rates jumping from 48% to 64% across finance, legal, and medical domains. Nearly all people who know how to properly fine-tune a frontier model work at a handful of labs — if AutoScientist can automate that expertise, custom models may finally become practical for individual businesses.
