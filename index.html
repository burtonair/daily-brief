US Exports Record Oil, but Gas Prices Keep Climbing

The US exported 5.2 million barrels of crude per day in April—a new record and 30% jump since before the Iran war—but Americans are paying more at the pump than ever. Brent crude is up 89% year-to-date.

US refineries are set up to handle heavy, sour crude, but domestic fields pump mostly light crude. That oil gets exported to countries better equipped to refine it. Gasoline prices are set by the global market, so achieving net exporter status “has essentially no impact on the prices Americans pay at the pump,” according to an analyst at the Institute for Energy Economics and Financial Analysis.

The US has overtaken Saudi Arabia as the world’s top crude exporter, largely because Asian markets are buying “whatever they can get their hands on” while the Strait of Hormuz remains blocked. President Trump announced a plan to guide ships through the strait, but Iran called the proposal “delirium” and warned any US armed forces approaching would “be subjected to attack.”

Columnist Daily Dad

Worry Is Not Love

You worry because you love them. That’s what’s so hard about being a parent—it’s like having your heart running around outside your body. So yeah, you’re anxious a lot. You’re anxious that they could get hurt, that they could go down the wrong path, that they might need something.

It’s reasonable that you worry. It’s good that you worry. But it’s worth saying here today that worry is not love. Or rather, it does not count as a loving action.

Love is encouragement. Love is support. Love is appreciation. Love is presence. Love is connection. Love is sitting down and eating dinner. Love is not stressing them out. Love is not holding them so close that nothing bad can ever happen to them. Love is not guilt or biting your nails.

Your worry is your problem. It is not a weight you get to put on them to make yourself feel better. What they need from you is not your worry. It’s your love and all actual loving actions.

Golden Tempo Makes Derby History as First Woman Trains a Winner

Long-shot Golden Tempo charged from last to first at Churchill Downs on Saturday, paying out $48.24 at 23–1 odds. Trainer Cherie DeVaux became the first woman to ever saddle a Derby winner in the race’s 152-year history.

The colt edged co-favorite Renegade by a neck before a crowd of 150,415, with longshot Ocelli finishing third at 70–1. Sibling jockeys José and Irad Ortiz rode the top two finishers—marking the first sibling 1–2 finish in Derby history. It was José’s 11th appearance and first win, coming one day after taking the Kentucky Oaks.

Spirit Airlines Collapsed. Now Comes the Baggage.

After two bankruptcies, 18 months in Chapter 11, and a failed bailout deal, Spirit’s flights were abruptly canceled Saturday morning. Doubling jet fuel prices from the Iran war crushed its restructuring plan.

Spirit’s fares were so low they helped set a price floor for the industry. With that tether gone, other airlines are free to raise prices. But Spirit had already shrunk dramatically: from 3.4% of domestic flights in May 2024 to just 1.1% this month.

American Airlines is capping economy fares on overlapping routes, and United is preparing to absorb stranded passengers and employees. Low-cost competitors like JetBlue could benefit but face the same fuel-cost headwinds. A trade group representing several budget airlines asked the government for $2.5 billion in relief.

Pentagon Announces New AI Partners, Excludes Anthropic

The Pentagon added 8 AI companies to its classified networks—SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Nvidia, Reflection, Microsoft, AWS, and Oracle—while continuing to exclude Anthropic.

The Department of War said the deals will “accelerate the transformation toward establishing the US military as an AI-first fighting force.” DoD CTO Emil Michael told CNBC that Anthropic’s supply-chain risk label still stands, but called its Mythos model a “separate national security moment.”

Notable on the list: Reflection, which raised $2B from 1789 Capital, a Donald Trump Jr.-backed fund. The White House seemingly wants priority access to Anthropic’s Mythos model despite the blacklist.
