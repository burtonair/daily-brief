Google's Deep Think Crushes Reasoning Benchmarks, Introduces Math Research Agent Rundown

Google just reminded everyone why it's still the biggest powerhouse in the AI race. The upgraded Gemini 3 Deep Think reasoning mode is obliterating benchmarks across math, coding, and science—while a new research agent autonomously solves open problems.

Why it matters: After Google dominated benchmarks and headlines to close 2025, the focus has been more on Anthropic and OpenAI in 2026—but don't forget about the tech giant. Deep Think's scores are wild, and the frontier for math and science is quickly moving into uncharted territory.

Isn't This When You Feel Love The Most?

Of course, we try to tell our children we love them. Sometimes they receive it. Sometimes they roll their eyes. Sometimes—depending on how we grew up and what we saw from our parents—even just saying "I love you" can be difficult or a stretch for us.

That's what makes the line in The Boy, the Mole, the Fox, and the Horse so beautiful and perfect. "Sometimes I want to say 'I love you all'," said the mole, "but I find it difficult." "Do you?" said the boy. "Yes. So I say something like 'I'm glad we are all here.'"

Isn't that when you feel the love the most? When you're all there? When you're surrounded by your kids at the crowded table? Whether it's for some special occasion or just some ordinary bit of garbage time where everyone is hanging out, it doesn't matter. This is the best, you think. This is what makes it all worth it.

But here's the thing. It's not that saying "I'm glad we are all here" is better because it's easier to say than "I love you." Getting everyone together is saying I love you. Being together is love. Making time is love. Being present—and not just literally, but figuratively, spiritually—that is love. It's a statement of priorities. It's a gift of the most precious resource and thing there is.

How To Rundown

Generate a TV Commercial with AI

In this guide, learn to generate a 20-second ad in the style of a professional TV commercial—taking the guesswork out of outputs without needing to click and pray.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Think of a commercial idea and ask Gemini to plan out two 5-second scenes. Once done, ask it to write prompts for the start and end frames of both scenes.
  2. Log in to Higgsfield, a free AI tool for video generation, and go to Image > Flux 1.1 to generate the frames from each prompt.
  3. In Higgsfield, go to Video > Kling 3.0, upload your frames with the short scene prompt, and hit generate.
  4. After this, stitch the videos in a free editor.

Pro tip: Ask Gemini to use photography terms like "Hero shot" when generating scene prompts. You can also generate music for the ad with Suno + Eleven Labs.

AI Rundown

MiniMax's Open-Source M2.5 Hits Frontier Coding Levels

Chinese AI lab MiniMax launched M2.5, an open-source model that rivals Opus 4.6 and GPT-5 on agentic coding benchmarks—but at a fraction of the cost.

M2.5 shows especially strong coding performance, scoring roughly even with Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.2 across key development benchmarks. Two APIs are available: M2.5-Lightning ($2.40/M output) and standard M2.5 ($1.20/M output), both priced much lower than Opus ($25/M).

MiniMax revealed that M2.5 now handles 30% of daily company tasks across R&D, product, sales, HR, and finance, as well as 80% of new code commits.

Why it matters: Every few months, a Chinese lab drops a model that changes the cost math for the entire industry. M2.5's frontier-level coding at this price makes "intelligence too cheap to meter" feel closer than ever.

Real Estate Brew

Homes Are Not Selling Like Hotcakes

Sales of preowned US homes decreased by 8.4% in January from December, amounting to the worst month for home closings in more than two years.

Despite mortgage rates falling throughout 2025, home sales were down 4.4% from a year ago. Dwellings sat unsold for a median of 46 days last month, compared with 41 days a year prior.

Analysts attribute tepid homebuying to economic uncertainty, on top of low inventory that's keeping prices high. In a silver lining: the median price of homes crept up by 0.9% to $396,800 over the year—but they've become more affordable, thanks to wage growth and lower mortgage rates.
