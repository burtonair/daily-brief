Create High-Converting Landing Pages in Claude Design

Anthropic’s new Claude Design tool can generate four mockup variations of your website’s landing page from a single prompt. Here’s how:

  1. Go to claude.ai/design, select the wireframe option, click create, and describe: who the page is for, what you’re selling, and the action a visitor should take.
  2. Find and screenshot a landing page you like. Try searching “top landing pages in [your niche]” and the checkout pane of a high-traffic site like Amazon, eBay, or PayPal.
  3. Add the screenshots to your brief and tell Claude to create four variations. Wait 2–5 minutes for results.
  4. Click any element and leave a note like “rewrite this CTA to be outcome-specific” or “add a testimonial.” Claude applies changes to refine outputs.

Pro tip: Click Share → Handoff to Claude Code → Send to Claude Code Web to get Claude Code to build and deploy the final website for you.

FBI Director Kash Patel Sues The Atlantic for $250 Million Morning Brew

Patel alleges defamation over an article claiming he drinks excessively and that his “erratic” behavior has officials worried about national security.

The lawsuit targets journalist Sarah Fitzpatrick, who authored the article based on over two dozen anonymous sources. Patel’s lawyers say the article falsely portrays him as “a habitual drunk, unable to perform the duties of his office” who is “unreachable in emergencies.” The Atlantic called the suit “meritless” and vowed to fight.

Establishing defamation is especially difficult for public figures, who must prove “actual malice”—that a publication knowingly made false claims or recklessly disregarded the truth. A judge recently dismissed Trump’s $10 billion defamation lawsuit against the WSJ publisher.

Science • Health

Breakthrough in mRNA Cancer Treatment: Pancreatic Cancer Patients Alive Six Years Later Morning Brew

New clinical trial data shows 7 of 16 patients given personalized mRNA vaccines responded to treatment—and are still alive six years later. Pancreatic cancer typically has just a 13% five-year survival rate.

The vaccine uses mRNA technology (the same behind Covid vaccines) to train a patient’s immune system to fight off lingering cancer cells after surgical tumor removal. A larger Phase 2 trial is in progress.

Separately, Revolution Medicines reported that a pill called daraxonrasib has nearly doubled life expectancy compared with chemotherapy. However, HHS Secretary RFK Jr. has questioned mRNA technology, putting research dollars in limbo.
