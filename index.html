Rundown AI How To — AI Training

Transcribe Any Video for Free with This Local AI

In this guide from The Rundown AI, you'll learn how to transcribe and translate any video file for free by running OpenAI's Whisper model locally on your computer — no sketchy online upload required.

Step-by-Step Setup

  1. Open your terminal and run brew install ffmpeg, then pip3 install -U openai-whisper. Not on Mac? Find your platform's commands at the openai/whisper GitHub repo.
  2. ffmpeg is an open-source tool for editing videos from the terminal; openai-whisper is the OpenAI model that does the actual transcribing — running entirely on your machine.
  3. Point Whisper at any video file: python3 -m whisper your-video.mp4 --model base. It outputs both a .txt file and an .srt file with timestamps.
  4. A 15-minute video takes about 2 minutes to transcribe. For translation, add language and translation flags to the command (full details in the full guide).

Pro tip: Whisper can also translate foreign-language videos to English — just add the appropriate flags to your command.

Community AI Workflow — Video Editing with Claude

From reader Sam S. in New York, NY: "I am a video editor, and I was tasked with creating a 2-minute trailer out of 6+ hours of interview footage of emotionally challenging subject material. I used Claude to transcribe the audio of all 6 interviews and asked it to pull the most impactful soundbites and create a time-coded 2-minute script.

After generating the script, Claude then drafted an Edit Decision List that I could import into Premiere and open as a timeline sequence, complete with Claude's edits and soundbites. This saved me hours worth of reviewing interview footage and helped with the emotional stress of watching the difficult material. The result was an impactful, dramatic trailer with a story arc as good as anything I could've scripted myself."

Workflow: Transcribe interviews with Whisper or Claude → ask Claude to identify impactful soundbites + create a timed script → have Claude generate an Edit Decision List → import directly into Premiere Pro as an editable timeline.
