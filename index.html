Breaking: Trade

Supreme Court Strikes Down Trump's Global Tariffs Brew

In a 6–3 decision, the Supreme Court struck down President Trump's sweeping global tariffs, ruling he exceeded his authority by invoking IEEPA without congressional approval.

The ruling applies to the "reciprocal" tariffs on nearly every US trading partner and additional drug-trafficking-related tariffs on China, Mexico, and Canada. Markets rose following the decision, with e-commerce companies like Amazon, Wayfair, eBay, and Etsy getting a particular boost.

Trump isn't backing down—he announced a new 10% global tariff under the Trade Act of 1974, which has a 150-day limit before congressional approval and a 15% maximum rate. The US could owe $175 billion in refunds to businesses from the jettisoned tariffs, per Penn-Wharton economists.
