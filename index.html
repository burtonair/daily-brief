Columnist Daily Dad Ryan Holiday

Enjoy It Now While You Can

It's kind of crazy how crazy things are right now. All that you have going on. All the stress that raising a family entails. The places you have to get to, the appointments you have to make, the illnesses you have to treat, the problems you have to solve. One thing after another, everything all at once. You are exhausted. You are burned out. You are in a perpetually bad mood.

And yet it's not hard to imagine that you will look back on this very period — whether your kids are 3 or 13 — as an absolutely wonderful time. You'll miss it. You'll forget the fights. You'll forget the throw up. You'll forget the anxiety and sense of inadequacy. You'll just remember how cute they were. You'll remember strapping them into their car seat. You'll remember taking them school shopping. You'll remember movies and baseball games and dance recitals.

Tempus fugit — time flies. Not long from now, you'll be jealous of people in the very position you are in. You will be nostalgic for something that you did not enjoy enough while you were in it.

It's not too late to change that. It's not too late to be a little easier on yourself. It's perfectly possible to look around and remind yourself of all that you have and how fleeting it is — and to feel good about this right now. You deserve that. Your kids deserve that. Because it is good. Because it is wonderful. The sooner you realize that, the better.

Use Claude Cowork + Obsidian to Triple Your Output

In this guide from The Rundown AI, you'll learn a simple system to plan and manage your workdays by setting up the Obsidian notes app so that Claude automatically creates daily plans for you — helping you triple your output.

Step-by-Step Setup

  1. Get Obsidian, create a vault with daily-notes/, templates/, projects/ folders, and add my-workflow.md to root with your schedule, work priorities, and projects.
  2. Create daily-notes-template.md in templates/ with "Plan," "Notes," and "End of Day" sections. Create a project-template.md in projects/ with "Goals," "Tasks," and "Notes."
  3. In Claude Cowork, create a scheduled morning planning task with this prompt: "Read my vault. Check yesterday's note, my project files, and my-workflow.md. Create today's daily note with prioritized tasks. Flag anything overdue."
  4. Create an end-of-day scheduled task with: "Read today's daily note. Summarize what got done, what's carrying over. Update project files with any completed tasks."

Pro tip: Ask Claude to interview you about each project upfront to create clear goals and deadlines in your project files.

Technology — Infrastructure

Data Centers' Hidden Threat: Mass Disconnections Could Crash the Power Grid 1% Better

Virginia data centers posed an unexpected threat to power grid stability in two separate incidents. In July 2024, roughly 70 facilities simultaneously disconnected; in February 2025, about 40 more followed — forcing grid operators to scramble.

Data centers automatically switch to backup power during grid disturbances, causing sudden demand drops. Losing under 2,000 megawatts was manageable both times, but officials warn losses of 3,000 to 5,000 megawatts could trigger cascading failures across the region. Data centers may consume 17% of US electricity by 2030, and Texas grid operator ERCOT estimates losses exceeding 2,600 megawatts could crash its system entirely.

NERC and Dominion Energy are actively working with tech companies to prevent automatic disconnections during grid disturbances. The issue underscores the growing tension between the AI industry's insatiable power appetite and the reliability of the aging US electrical grid. Tech companies meeting with Trump on Wednesday are expected to address this directly with their energy pledge.
