Streaming & Media Morning Brew

Netflix Is Reinventing the Thing It Killed: Cable TV

Netflix is reportedly weighing the addition of live genre channels and streaming bundles to combat sagging engagement — edging toward the very cable model it spent a decade disrupting.

Executives are discussing live channels that run shows and movies on a loop by genre (think: a 24/7 Golden Girls feed), plus the ability to bundle third-party streaming services directly inside Netflix — similar to what Amazon and Apple already do. The moves are aimed at reversing a decline in how often viewers stick with content, which can signal coming cancellations.

The numbers tell the story: Netflix generated ~$1.5 billion in ad revenue last year and projects to double that this year. Live channels would expand ad inventory. Shares are meanwhile down 40% over the past 12 months, and TV viewership in April hit its lowest level since May 2025, per Nielsen.

On a separate front: Netflix is reportedly in the running to purchase Letterboxd, the movie-log app with 30 million global members. Sony, Paramount, and Reddit co-founder Alexis Ohanian are also reportedly interested. Investment bank LionTree is floating a $250 million valuation.

The irony is complete: the company that once marketed itself as the antidote to channel-surfing is now seriously considering adding channels.

Space & Technology 1% Better

China Catches a Rocket in a Giant Net at Sea — A World First

China successfully launched and recovered its Long March-10B rocket from Hainan province, becoming the second country ever to retrieve an orbital-class booster — and the first to do it from a sea platform.

The method is a world first: instead of landing legs, the descending booster deployed hooks that snagged a giant tensioned net mounted on a sea platform. SpaceX first landed an orbital booster in 2015; two Chinese recovery attempts failed last December.

Reusability radically cuts launch costs — key to China’s plans for massive satellite constellations and landing astronauts on the moon by 2030. The 200-foot rocket carries 16 metric tons to orbit. China expects to refly this booster by year’s end.
