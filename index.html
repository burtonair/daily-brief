Deadly Hantavirus Outbreak on Atlantic Cruise Ship Triggers Global Contact Tracing

Three passengers dead and five ill aboard the MV Hondius as health authorities across four continents race to track dozens who disembarked before the virus was confirmed.

An Atlantic cruise ship carrying nearly 150 passengers is suffering from an outbreak of hantavirus, a respiratory disease with a mortality rate of up to 50%. Since the Dutch-flagged Hondius departed Argentina last month for Cape Verde, three passengers have died—including a Dutch couple and a German national—and five others have fallen ill, with at least five cases confirmed via lab testing.

The first death occurred April 11, but hantavirus was not confirmed until May 2 in a British passenger now in intensive care in South Africa. Roughly 30 passengers from 12 countries disembarked at Saint Helena and flew home before cases were confirmed. Singapore, Switzerland, and St. Helena are now monitoring contacts, with some told to isolate for 45 days.

Health experts assure the risk of a pandemic is low—hantavirus doesn't spread easily between humans. However, some passengers tested positive for the rare Andes variant, the only strain known to allow human-to-human transmission through close physical contact or shared utensils. The WHO says public risk remains low. The Hondius is now being diverted to the Canary Islands.

Columnist Daily Dad

Mothers Are the Quiet Heroes of History

Every morning at Daily Stoic, we reference the great Stoics: Seneca, Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, Zeno. They were all men. But is that somehow representative of what it means to be a Stoic? Far from it.

In Book 1 of Meditations, Marcus Aurelius writes about his mother, Domitia Lucilla. He thanks her for "her reverence for the divine, her generosity, her inability not only to do wrong but even to conceive of doing it. And the simple way she lived—not in the least like the rich." One of Marcus' biographers confirms she was positively unusual—an imperial Roman who distanced herself from her class's dominant ethos of conspicuous consumption.

Yet how many people even know her name? Or the names of the countless women throughout history who raised, supported, taught, encouraged, and sacrificed for their families? Think of what they put up with, the indignities they tolerated, and the sacrifices they were willing to make—all while knowing they might die giving birth.

As we celebrate Mother's Day this weekend, let us think about just how much mothers matter. The fact that they did all this bravely and patiently for so long is proof that they are true Stoics. They have a thing or two to teach everyone about what focusing only on what you can control really looks like.

Food Morning Brew

Italian Food Brand Accused of 'Tomato Fraud' Over San Marzano Claims

A California lawsuit accuses Cento Fine Foods of deceiving customers about the legitimacy of its canned San Marzano tomatoes.

Like authentic Champagne and Parmesan, official San Marzano tomatoes can only come from one specific region in Italy. The premium fruits have a thicker tomato wall, fewer seeds, and lower acidity. Sales are regulated by Il Consorzio di Tutela del Pomodoro San Marzano DOP, but Cento uses a third-party agency called Agri-Cert. The lawsuit claims Cento was actually removed by the official group for counterfeit labeling. Cento called the allegations meritless, noting it defeated a similar suit in 2019.
