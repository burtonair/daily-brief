Work & Society Brew

Mom and dad both work full time now — a first in American history

A new Pew Research study finds 52% of two-parent US households have both parents working full time — the first time a majority has hit that mark.

The figure has climbed steadily: 31% in 1975, 46% a decade ago, and now a majority for the first time. Women earning more college degrees than men is a key driver. Pew surveyed 2,242 working parents (excluding same-sex couples due to sample-size limitations).

The balancing act is real: 70% say parenting tasks bleed into work hours; 59% say work bleeds into parenting time; and 54% call the balance genuinely difficult. But 83% still view dual incomes as a positive. Only 24% have meaningful telework flexibility — a gap most say they’d close immediately if they could.

Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham won a special election and entered Parliament — setting up a potential challenge to unpopular PM Keir Starmer for the Labour leadership.

No formal challenge has begun yet, but Burnham’s parliamentary win puts him in position to try to unseat Starmer as both party leader and Prime Minister. Starmer responded by saying, “I don’t think that is a good thing for the country, to plunge us into chaos.”
