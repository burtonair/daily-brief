Labor • AI

Meta Sends Termination Emails to 8,000 — Morale at “All-Time Low”

Morning Brew

Starting Wednesday morning, waves of emails began landing in roughly 8,000 Meta employees’ personal inboxes: their jobs are gone. Workers had been dreading the mass culling since The Information first reported it in March. The mood at Menlo Park is reportedly rancid.

The layoffs affect about 10% of Meta’s ~78,000-person workforce. In a memo earlier this week, the company also announced it would move 7,000 employees to AI-related initiatives and close 6,000 open roles. CEO Mark Zuckerberg framed this round differently from 2022’s overhiring correction: this is about freeing up funds for AI, with the company pledging as much as $145 billion in AI spending this year.

Morale had been deteriorating for weeks. Some employees are reportedly begging for the virtual pink slip and the 16 weeks minimum severance. One decade-long veteran told the SF Standard: “I tend to cry in the shower.” Large, unexplained empty boxes began appearing at Menlo Park offices days before the cuts. In April, Meta had already rolled out a program tracking employees’ every computer move, framing the data as training material for AI models. Workers pushed back with a petition; UK employees began attempting to unionize. All while the company posted record profits.

The timing lands as Google’s I/O event Tuesday made clear the entire tech industry is repricing labor against AI productivity. Google simultaneously announced AI coding systems that can build an OS core in 12 hours, and its new Gemini 3.5 Flash benchmarks near rivals at half the price. The message from every major lab Wednesday: the engineers who remain need to build faster, and the ones who can’t keep pace will be next.

Columnist • Daily Dad

Let These Guide You

There is no Rosetta Stone for parenting. But the Stoics give us something equally ancient — a compass that works for most every situation a parent will face.

These are the Cardinal Virtues, which Zeno laid down in the 3rd Century B.C.: Courage. Discipline. Justice. Wisdom. Like the cardinal points on a compass — named in a nod to the virtues — these ideals guide us through the sleepless nights and the arguments, through uncomfortable conversations and big decisions. When tempers are tested. When values are tested. When things don’t go according to plan.

We need these virtues and we need to teach them to our children. Their life — and the future — hinges on it. Memorize them. Act on them. Live them. Parent by them.

As Marcus Aurelius urged: “Be a boxer, not a fencer” — because the fencer has a weapon they must pick up, but a boxer’s weapons are already part of him. These four virtues are already yours. You only need to develop them.

Build Automated Business Reports with AI

Connect Composio to Codex or Claude Code and use simple prompts to pull Google Analytics data, generate a Markdown report, and schedule it to run weekly — no custom backend required.

  1. Create a Composio account and connect the Google Analytics toolkit. Open Codex in Terminal and paste your custom install script from the Composio dashboard.
  2. In Codex, prompt: “Use the Composio CLI tool to inspect our Google Analytics setup. Identify available accounts and properties, find the property that tracks [YOUR SITE], and tell me which dimensions and metrics you’d use for a useful traffic report. Do not create the report yet.”
  3. Once reviewed: “Use Composio + GA to identify the three most important trends over the last 90 days. Return a markdown report with an executive summary, tables, source notes, and recommended actions.”
  4. Ask Codex to visualize it as a PDF or website, then turn the steps into reusable skills with an automation to run daily/weekly/monthly.

Pro tip: Ask Codex to audit the Composio toolkit for useful connections. The same Explore, Analyze, Visualize framework works on YouTube, Shopify, and other connectors.

Google I/O: The Price War Is the Product

The Rundown AI 1% Better

Google launched Gemini 3.5 Flash, Omni, Spark, Antigravity 2.0, and a Search redesign at I/O — but the real news is DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis saying Flash benchmarks near Claude Code and Codex at half the price.

What launched: Gemini Omni converts any input (text, image, audio, video) into video. Gemini Spark is a 24/7 personal agent running on Cloud VMs that reaches Gmail, Maps, Docs, and Chrome. Search gets cross-modal inputs, 24/7 info agents, and generative UI layouts. Antigravity 2.0 built an OS core in 12 hours. The Gemini app now has 900 million monthly active users, double from a year ago. Gemini Pro (bigger model) arrives in June.

The price play: Vector’s Grant Janich frames it cleanly: Google has 10–15% share in professional coding vs. OpenAI and Anthropic’s ~40% each. Flash comparable at half the price is a direct shot at their revenue. Google also dropped its Ultra subscription from $250 to $200 and added a $100 entry tier. Coding baked into Search reaches a billion users for free. The $190B in 2026 capex is the financing line on a pricing campaign funded by ad and cloud earnings the rivals don’t have. Alphabet is up 130% over 12 months. The market is paying Google to run this play.

Smart glasses: Google’s Intelligent Eyewear — with Warby Parker and Gentle Monster — ships this fall with voice-first Gemini: navigation, messaging, photos, live translation. A display-equipped follow-up (Project Aura with XReal) comes later. Google Glass is back. Timing is better this time.

Karpathy Joins Anthropic to Help AI Build Itself

Morning Brew The Rundown AI

Andrej Karpathy announced he is joining Anthropic’s pre-training team under Nick Joseph and leading an internal effort to apply Claude to Anthropic’s own AI training pipeline — essentially using AI to build better AI.

Karpathy co-founded OpenAI in 2015, led Tesla’s Autopilot until 2022, returned briefly to OAI, then left in 2024 to start an AI education startup (which he plans to resume “in time”). He wrote on X: “The next few years at the frontier of LLMs will be especially formative.” It’s Anthropic’s second high-profile OAI/xAI poach this month, following a founding member of Musk’s xAI. Separately, new reporting revealed that Demis Hassabis was an early angel investor in Anthropic, with Google also separately investing billions into the rival lab.

Tech • Consumer

Amazon Kills Support for Pre-2013 Kindles — 2M Users Left in the Cold

Morning Brew

Today, Amazon ended support for Kindle e-readers released before 2013, meaning owners of the ~2 million older devices still in use can no longer buy new books from the Kindle Store.

Affected models include some Kindle Fire tablets, the Kindle Paperwhite 1st Generation, and the Kindle Touch. Books already loaded are safe — new purchases are blocked. Amazon is offering a 20% discount on new Kindles and a $20 e-book credit for qualifying trade-ins. Many users are sideloading titles from non-Amazon apps; others are jailbreaking devices to install alternative reading apps. Users are accusing Amazon of planned obsolescence. Amazon controls 72% of the e-reader market.
