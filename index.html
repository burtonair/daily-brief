Nvidia Posts Record $68B Quarter — Investors Shrug

Nvidia blew past Wall Street's expectations with $68.1 billion in Q4 sales — up 73% year-over-year — and a $43 billion profit. Yet the stock barely budged, as investors fret about whether hyperscaler AI spending can sustain this pace.

Nvidia forecast approximately $78 billion in revenue for its fiscal first quarter, not even counting data center revenue in China where it recently received US regulatory approval to sell certain chips. Despite the record numbers, Nvidia's stock has a history of falling post-earnings even when beating expectations. Shares briefly spiked when results dropped, then pared gains during the earnings call and flattened out by end of after-hours trading.

Take This When You Can

It's a video game. It's a YouTuber who makes unwatchable videos about video games. It's drama at school. It's an argument about politics you don't agree with. It's a TikTok dance their friends are doing. It's a new boy band that sounds awful.

But you know what it also is? It's interest. Your kids are interested in something and for some reason they're interested in talking to you about it. Or watching something with you. Or they're interested in going somewhere with you.

So take it. This is what you say you want: to connect with them. To do stuff with them. To talk with them. To spend quality or "garbage time" together. Don't turn it away because it doesn't match your taste. Or because you're in a bad mood. Or because you're tired. Or because you can "see where this is going." Take the opportunity. Encourage the interest. Show them that you're interested in what interests them.

None of us get enough of these chances — is there even such a thing as too many? — so take it when and where you can.

Turn Your Forgotten Bookmarks Into Something Useful with Comet

All those articles you saved for "later" can be turned into an automated usefulness engine. Here's how to use Perplexity's Comet browser to read through bookmarks, score them by value, and log the best finds into a Google Sheet — on a daily schedule.

  1. Get Comet and create a Space with the instruction: "Read X threads and articles, and rate findings by usefulness, estimated implementation time, and cost."
  2. Create a Google Sheet with headers: Date, Title, Link, Rating, User Rating, Time, Cost. Add it to Comet's custom instructions via the Google Drive connector.
  3. On X, set up a bookmark folder to save tweets to. Then prompt the Comet space to open it, check bookmarks, and rate anything new into the connected sheet based on usefulness. Add your own ratings too.
  4. Go to Scheduled Tasks, create a daily one, and prompt it to find the most interesting new use cases about [topic of choice] from the last 24 hours.

Pro tip: Make sure "Control Browser" is enabled in the dropdown and Web and Social are selected as sources when creating the scheduled task.

Perplexity Launches 'Computer' — A 19-Model AI Agent System Rundown AI

Perplexity just introduced Perplexity Computer, a multi-model orchestration system that dispatches tasks to 19 separate AI models — positioning itself as one of the first platforms to make model flexibility a core product feature.

Users describe an outcome, and the system spins up sub-agents that can browse, code, connect to apps, and autonomously handle tasks. Each job runs in its own sandbox and freely mixes rival models across tasks — including models from competing labs — claiming to run actively for months at a time.

Ayahuasca Compound DMT Shows Promise Against Depression in Clinical Trial 1% Better

A phase II clinical trial published in Nature found DMT, a psychoactive compound in the Amazonian ritual drink ayahuasca, reduced depressive symptoms more effectively than placebo.

Seventeen participants received DMT injections alongside psychotherapeutic support, with measurable improvements appearing within two weeks. Researchers at Imperial College London developed a synthetic DMT formulation producing an intense 30-minute experience — without ayahuasca's traditional nausea. All participants received therapist support before, during, and after treatment.

The trial was small and results remain preliminary. Researchers noted DMT functions more as a catalyst for deeper personal work than a standalone cure. FDA regulatory pathways could also complicate wider clinical adoption.
