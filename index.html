Economy — Breaking

Inflation Erases Wage Gains for First Time in Three Years

Morning Brew

Surging energy prices drove US inflation to a three-year high in April, with annual CPI hitting 3.8% — outpacing the 3.6% average wage growth for the first time since 2023.

The BLS report showed more than 40% of April’s month-to-month inflation increase came from energy prices, up ~18% year-over-year. Food prices also surged — tomato prices rose 15% for the second straight month due in part to tariffs on Mexico, and fresh produce hit its highest monthly increase since 2010.

Even stripping out volatile categories, core CPI hit 2.8% — well above the Fed’s 2% target. Higher airfares, Netflix raising prices, and a one-time rental cost adjustment from last year’s government shutdown data blackout all contributed.

“Inflation is eating up all wage gains,” a Navy Federal Credit Union chief economist told CNBC. A recent CNN poll found nearly three-quarters of respondents say economic conditions are poor, with “uncertainty” and “stress” topping the list of words Americans use for their financial future.

The data made traders more bullish on a rate hike by year-end, even as incoming Fed chair Kevin Warsh had previously called for lower rates.

Columnist Daily Dad

You Have the Perfect Teenager

With all the sneaking out and the lying and the moodiness and the aimlessness and the challenging and the whining and the ignoring, it might not feel like you have a perfect teenager — but you do. Because this is all perfectly normal teenage behavior.

Emily Wilson’s translation of The Odyssey recently noted how timeless Homer’s descriptions of teenage Telemachus were. He was moody and emotional, insecure and insolent — just like your teenager, and just like all teenagers. He was doing his job. And so is yours.

Your job is to understand this and be patient. Your job is to model what mature emotional regulation looks like, to guide them — not to make them feel like there’s something wrong with them, because there isn’t. Your job is not to take any of this personally, because it isn’t personal.

Your job is to wait this out and help them become an adult — which means first letting them be a perfectly difficult teenager.

Turn Claude Code into Your Personal Wall Street Analyst

Add Anthropic’s financial services plugin marketplace to Claude Code, install market research skills, and use them to generate equity analysis, earnings reviews, and financial reports.

Step-by-step:

  1. Open a terminal and start Claude Code. Type /marketplace and select the plugin marketplace option.
  2. Go to the Marketplaces tab, select Add Marketplace, and paste the GitHub link to add the claude-for-financial-services marketplace.
  3. Browse and install plugins. The market-researcher, earnings-reviewer, equity-research, and financial-analysis skills work well.
  4. Use installed skills inside Claude Code to create sourced reports, compare companies, review earnings, and generate financial analysis.

Pro tip: Treat this as research support, not financial advice. Ask Claude to cite sources and verify anything important before making decisions.

AI & Tech

Google Launches Googlebooks and Gemini Intelligence for Android

The Rundown AI

Google unveiled AI-native Googlebook laptops and a new Gemini Intelligence cross-device platform at its Android Show event — a major hardware and software push ahead of Google I/O next week.

Googlebooks ship this fall as Gemini-native laptops built with Dell, HP, Lenovo, Acer, and Asus, featuring a “Magic Pointer” AI cursor shown in a new demo. The laptops will run Android phone apps and files, blending ChromeOS, Android, Google Play, and Gemini into one platform.

Gemini Intelligence acts as Android’s cross-device AI platform, carrying out agentic tasks within apps and operating with on-screen context. Other releases include a Create My Widget tool, a Rambler dictation tool that strips filler words, and Gemini auto-browse in Chrome on-device.

While the world awaits Apple’s Siri AI revival, Gemini is being woven directly into Android instead of bolted on as an afterthought. An “intelligence system” across devices is a clear path to making AI actually useful day-to-day.

Google and SpaceX Explore Putting Data Centers in Orbit

The Rundown AI 1% Better

Google is reportedly in talks with SpaceX for a rocket-launch deal to build orbital data centers — a moonshot that could reshape AI infrastructure well ahead of SpaceX’s record summer IPO.

Google already holds a 6.1% stake in SpaceX from a $900M 2015 investment, with VP Don Harrison occupying a SpaceX board seat. Google’s own Project Suncatcher aims to launch prototype satellites by 2027.

Anthropic also finalized a compute deal with SpaceX last week and expressed interest in “multiple GW of orbital AI compute capacity.” Sam Altman called the concept “ridiculous” at a New Delhi event, saying it won’t “matter at scale this decade.”

SpaceX has filed for approval for up to 1 million satellites — making orbital compute a centerpiece of its pre-IPO pitch. Betting against big ideas from both Google and Elon Musk hasn’t historically been a winning trade.

Finance & Law

Yale Lawyer Charged in Decade-Long $3M Insider Trading Ring

1% Better

Federal prosecutors arrested Boston M&A lawyer Nicolo Nourafchan for allegedly running a scheme that funneled merger tips to traders in Florida, New York, Russia, and Israel for a decade.

The 43-year-old Yale Law grad allegedly accessed deal files at Goodwin Procter, Latham & Watkins, and Sidley Austin, then recruited lawyers at Wachtell Lipton and Weil Gotshal to share their own. Tips covered deals involving Amazon, Johnson & Johnson, and Burger King — windfalls ranging from $2,000 to $3 million per trade.

The case names 29 other defendants including Nourafchan’s brother, his Santa Monica hair stylist, and former college classmates. Nine have already pleaded guilty.

Economy & Business

MBA Fire Sale: Business Schools Slashing Tuition by Up to 50%

1% Better

Purdue, Washington University, UC Irvine, and Johns Hopkins are rolling out steep tuition discounts as MBA applications slide — with AI displacement as both the cause and the sales pitch.

Washington University’s Olin Business School is offering a $10,000 scholarship specifically for professionals whose jobs changed due to AI adoption. Purdue cut 40% off out-of-state tuition (from $60,000 to $36,000). UC Irvine trimmed up to 38%.

Financial aid for business grad students hit 62% in 2025, up from 48% a decade earlier. Schools are pivoting toward shorter, AI-focused degrees for workers worried about displacement. Applications are also down as international students face a more unwelcoming US environment.
