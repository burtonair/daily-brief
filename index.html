Columnist  |  Daily Dad

Getting There Is Part Of The Trip

Of course, getting there is important. You paid for the hotel room. The theme park tickets. You have to make the flight in order to get to the beach.

But do you have to make the flight miserable? It’s strange — most of us look back on our own childhoods as needlessly stressful, see that our parents were awfully hard on us, yet the second we start traveling, we seem to turn into them.

The trip, we need to understand, is part of the trip. Getting there is not separate from the vacation. If the point is for our family to be together, to have fun, to relax together — we need to start much earlier. Planning the trip should not be a nightmare. Packing up should not be a nightmare. Being on the road should not be a nightmare.

The worst part about all this is the unfair expectations it puts on the trip itself. Now it has to be worth all the headache and tension. Instead of just being what it can be: fun. Time together. Getting from one place to another. Relax — that’s the whole point of the vacation, isn’t it?

Anthropic Ends Fable 5 Access Drama — With a Compromise

After postponing the removal of Fable 5 from subscriptions three times in five weeks, Anthropic has landed on a permanent answer: the model stays — at half the usage caps.

Fable 5 will remain inside Max and Team Premium plans at 50% of each plan’s limits. Lower-tier plans get a one-time $100 credit before moving to pay-per-use. Anthropic admitted demand was “challenging to predict” and the usage situation “frustrating,” pledging investment in more compute.

OpenAI CFO Proposes “Useful Intelligence Per Dollar” as the New AI Budget Metric

As companies hunt for cheaper AI options, OpenAI CFO Sarah Friar published a framework for grading AI spend that — conveniently — favors pricier frontier models.

Friar’s scorecard tracks reliability first, then useful output, scale, and total expense — replacing raw per-token pricing as the standard. Using the DeepSWE coding benchmark, she showed Sol scored 2.8 points above Fable 5 with a 36.2% lower estimated API bill.

Her bottom-line question: “whether the value of the work AI completes grows faster than the cost of producing it.” The industry has quickly shifted from token-volume mania to reining in AI budgets — but critics note this is a scoreboard built by the companies selling the expensive models.
