Musk v. Altman: The Trial That Could Reshape AI Begins Monday

A high-stakes California trial pitting Elon Musk against OpenAI CEO Sam Altman is set to begin with jury selection Monday — the culmination of a yearslong friends-to-enemies arc that could reconfigure the AI landscape. Morning Brew

Musk, an OpenAI co-founder and original funder, accuses Altman and OpenAI President Greg Brockman of breaking their vow to keep the ChatGPT maker as a nonprofit. He claims they "assiduously manipulated" him out of millions in donations by promising to develop AI more safely than "profit-driven tech giants" like Google and Microsoft.

OpenAI calls Musk's accusations "baseless" and "motivated by jealousy, regret for walking away from OpenAI, and a desire to derail a competing AI company." Musk left OpenAI's board in 2018 after failing to convince other founders to fold the startup into Tesla, then started xAI in 2023.

Musk demands $100+ billion in damages, the removal of Altman and Brockman, and the reversal of OpenAI's recent for-profit restructuring. If he prevails, OpenAI could be kneecapped right as it's racing xAI to an IPO.

The $100 Landline Kids Can't Stop Asking For Morning Brew

A screenless, landline-inspired device called Tin Can has a monthslong waitlist and has already sold hundreds of thousands of units in its first year.

The wi-fi-enabled phone costs $100 and offers free calls between Tin Cans and to emergency services. Creator Chet Kittleson, a father of three, felt exhausted by the choice between giving his kids a smartphone or managing all their playdates. The company has raised $15.5 million total, with a $12M seed round led by Greylock Partners.

Schools are the fastest-growing segment — Nativity Parish in Kansas delivered free Tin Cans to 200+ families. The device has attracted parents amid growing backlash to kids on social media; Meta and YouTube recently lost a huge social media addiction trial.
