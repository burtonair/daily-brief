Anthropic's Claude Mythos Strikes Fear Into Banks

An impromptu meeting of bank CEOs and federal officials was held in Washington this week over the destructive capabilities of Anthropic's latest AI model, which can detect cybersecurity flaws with exponentially greater efficiency than human hackers.

Fed Chair Jerome Powell and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent gathered the heads of Bank of America, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and Wells Fargo on Tuesday after the limited rollout of Mythos. The emergency meeting signals that the Trump administration believes the model — while intended to protect companies from hackers — could be weaponized against the US financial system.

Anthropic distributed Mythos to only ~40 organizations because it's too powerful for a full public release. Officials believe it can debilitate Fortune 100 companies, infiltrate national defense systems, and take down huge chunks of the internet. A security expert told Business Insider that while a human team discovers about 100 critical flaws per year, Mythos can find "thousands."

The concerns come as Anthropic is fighting the Trump administration over being designated a supply chain risk by the Pentagon after limiting the use of its AI tech in war. Morning Brew
