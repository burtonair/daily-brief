Tech & Economy

Apple hikes MacBook and iPad prices as AI chip inflation hits consumers Brew

Apple announced price hikes across laptops and tablets, blaming an unprecedented surge in memory and storage chip costs driven by Silicon Valley’s AI data center boom. The company said it has “never seen a component price increase this much, this quickly.”

The new prices: the MacBook Neo goes to $699 (up from $599), a MacBook Air 512GB climbs to $1,299 (up from $1,099), and an iPad Air 128GB rises to $749 (up from $599). iPhones are spared for now, but analysts expect Apple to charge up to $200 more for iPhone 18 models this fall. Apple’s stock dipped more than 6% on the news.

The culprit: AI data centers have pulled factories away from consumer electronics, handing chip suppliers like Micron enormous pricing power. Memory chip costs have spiked “more than fourfold” since Q4 last year. Micron itself crushed earnings estimates this week, its stock surging 15.8%. Government data shows computers and electronics prices rose around 6% from December to May.

Apple isn’t alone: Microsoft also raised Xbox console prices this week, citing the same memory and storage cost pressures. Fed Governor Lisa Cook noted that AI spending on data centers could keep consumer electronics prices elevated — strengthening the case to hold interest rates higher for longer.

Suddenly, They Won’t Care

It just becomes a part of your life. Pointing out the trash truck. Or the helicopter. Or the bunnies — because they love bunnies.

You know it as almost a kind of incantation, a magical remedy that can relieve tears or boredom. You know you just have to point it out and they’ll get excited. It’s a wonderful part of childhood, their obsessions with these things that otherwise seem ordinary to us. Boats, cows, dinosaurs, the color pink. They’ll race to the front window to see a dump truck go by. They’ll watch in awe as airplanes fly overhead. And you’ll get excited, too — almost taking for granted the power to bring joy and laughter to them just by noticing something.

…Until the day when they suddenly don’t care. It just goes away. They won’t get up to see the garbage get picked up. They roll their eyes at the cows you’re pointing out on the road trip. They don’t care that there’s a new exhibit of fossils at the museum.

In that moment, your powers fade. The happiness tap dries up. But that’s just how this goes. So enjoy it while you still have it — because soon, you’ll have to work harder and harder to find new things, new ways to connect with them. They’re growing up before your eyes. Make sure you see it.

Tech & AI Brew Rundown

Anthropic accuses Alibaba of the “largest known” AI distillation attack — 28.8 million Claude exchanges in 45 days

Anthropic sent a letter to US officials alleging that Alibaba ran nearly 25,000 fraudulent accounts to harvest Claude’s most advanced capabilities — targeting agentic reasoning, coding, and long-horizon task execution at an unprecedented scale.

Distillation attacks work by querying a frontier model at massive scale and feeding the responses to a weaker model — effectively stealing capabilities without building them. Anthropic says this one totaled 28.8M exchanges across 45 days, dwarfing prior incidents. In February, Anthropic had already disclosed attacks from DeepSeek, Moonshot, and MiniMax totaling 16M+ exchanges; OpenAI raised similar concerns.

Anthropic is now calling for antitrust clarity to allow AI labs to share threat intelligence, stronger chip export controls, and sanctions against Chinese labs behind distillation. The accusations land as Anthropic tries to repair a rocky relationship with the federal government — and as the White House is already moving to restrict frontier model releases.

AI companies fund $500M+ initiative to retrain workers their own tools may displace

A bipartisan coalition called RAISE US, led by former Commerce Secretary Gina Raimondo and former Indiana Governor Eric Holcomb, launched with over $500M raised toward a $1B goal — backed by OpenAI, Anthropic, Amazon, Microsoft, and Eli Lilly.

The coalition aims to build AI retraining programs, reform unemployment insurance, and reward employers who retrain staff rather than replace them. It launches first in Arkansas, Maryland, Utah, and Connecticut. The timing is pointed: MBA median starting pay is already projected to drop to $120K as AI absorbs the entry-level analyst roles MBAs were traditionally hired to fill.

Sports Brew

NCAA limits eligibility to 5 years — but lawsuits are already filed

Starting in 2027–28, college athletes will have a maximum five-year eligibility window. The clock starts at enrollment or the year after a player turns 19, whichever comes first — ending an era of players competing well into their mid-to-late twenties.

A lawsuit was filed immediately, with more expected. Athletes who enrolled in 2022 say the rule is retroactively unfair — they competed against older players their whole careers and now face a shortened window to earn NIL money, some of which reaches seven figures annually. A hearing on a preliminary injunction is set for Wednesday.
