Supreme Court Strikes Down Trump Tariffs, Opening $175B in Potential Refunds Brew

On Friday, the Supreme Court ruled that President Trump lacked authority to impose tariffs under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act without congressional approval. Now businesses that paid $175 billion in tariffs face an uphill climb to get refunds.

The Court stopped short of specifying how refunds should be processed, leaving it to the US Court of International Trade to figure out. President Trump suggested refunds won't be easy, saying "We'll end up being in court for the next five years."

Two big US firms have already filed hundreds of tariff refund cases for companies including J. Crew, Illumina, Dole, Costco, and Revlon. Trade lawyer Richard O'Neill advised: "The time to file was yesterday. The next best time is today." US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer said Trump's tariff policy hasn't changed and the president will enact tariffs by other means.

You Need to Sync Your Timelines

It doesn't matter that you're on the other side of the world. It doesn't matter that you're in the middle of some crazy period at work. It doesn't matter that you don't have custody this week. Just because you're not physically with your family doesn't mean you can't still be with them.

You have to stay on the same timeline as them. You can't lose track of the rhythms of their life just because you hopped on a plane, just because they might not be living with you at the moment, just because you're running from meeting to meeting.

Know, as you're going to bed at some terribly late hour, where they are in that moment. Know, in the middle of that meeting, that they're heading to practice—and send them a message. Be ready, even if you're far away, for the chance that they might need you.

We stay on their timeline. We stay rooted in who we are and what is happening at home because that is the center of the world. That's what matters. That's what we're tracking.

Self-Host an n8n Automation Server in Minutes

Run thousands of automations per month on n8n with just a $5 virtual server. You can set it up in under 10 minutes.

  1. Go to railway.com/deploy/n8n, sign in with GitHub, and click Deploy Now. You don't need to configure any variables.
  2. After your automation server is deployed, click the n8n module, go to your custom link, and set up your email and password login.
  3. Create a new automation by clicking Create workflow in your n8n dashboard—no need to set it up node-by-node.
  4. Ask Claude or ChatGPT to map out the automation you want as a JSON file. Then in the new workflow, click the three dots and import from file.

Pro tip: You can invite users via email to your n8n server, making it great for client- or team-specific projects. You can even save API keys in the server for others to use.

AI Chips Rundown

Taalas' Custom Chip Gives AI a 10x Speed Boost

AI chip startup Taalas emerged with HC1, a custom chip built to run a single AI model—delivering responses roughly 100x faster than today's standard hardware.

The chip permanently embeds Meta's Llama 3.1 8B model into the hardware rather than running it as software. The result is near-instantaneous AI responses in under 100 milliseconds at a fraction of the power and cost.

While Llama 3.1 is far from the frontier, Taalas says it can retool chips for new models in months—with a top-tier option planned by winter. The startup pulled in $169M in new funding, bringing its total above $200M.
