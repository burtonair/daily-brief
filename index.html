Tech & AI

OpenAI builds its first custom chip — “Jalapeño” promises the fastest ASIC cycle ever Rundown

Nine months after announcing the project, OpenAI and Broadcom have a working inference chip in the lab — and OpenAI’s own AI helped design it. The company says it delivers “substantially better performance per watt than current state-of-the-art.”

Jalapeño is an ASIC built for inference — running finished models for users — not the GPU-heavy training workloads Nvidia still dominates. It went from design kickoff to factory-ready in nine months, with OpenAI calling it “the fastest ASIC development cycle ever achieved.”

OpenAI’s own models contributed to design and optimization — a recursive flex that signals how quickly the feedback loop will tighten. The company is pushing to power 10 GW of compute with custom chips by 2029, with Nvidia still anchoring model training while Jalapeño chips away at the inference cost stack.

Why it matters: owning silicon, model, and product simultaneously lets OpenAI tune every layer for the others. If the efficiency claims hold at scale, this could meaningfully shift the economics of running ChatGPT, Codex, and future agents — and reduce the per-query cost that has long been the AI industry’s dirty secret.

Columnist | Daily Dad Daily Dad

This Is How You Brighten A Place Up

You might think your house is a mess. You might think it’s looking a little dated. You might be a little embarrassed to have people over.

But you shouldn’t be. Your house looks that way because you have kids. You’ve got better things to do. You’ve got a whole different sensibility you’re expressing.

A journalist covering Theodore Roosevelt’s administration noted how the introduction of kids back to the residence had “done more to brighten and cheer the White House than a whole army of decorators,” and just how lovely it was to hear “the merry prattle of children echoing through the corridors.” And so it goes with our own homes.

Our remodel is the fort in the living room. Our transition spaces are piles of hockey gear and cleats. Our landscaping is bikes on their side and things written in chalk on the sidewalk. We pull the bathroom together with a little stool so they can reach the sink. Our appliances are covered in art and school pictures.

That’s how we freshen our place up. That’s how we make our house a home.
