Breaking — Markets

Tech Stocks Plunge as Nasdaq Enters Correction Territory Amid War Fears

The Nasdaq fell 2.15% to close in correction territory — down 11% from its October peak — as investors grapple with rising oil prices and the prospect of a prolonged Iran conflict. The Dow also entered correction territory after dropping nearly 800 points. Morning Brew

For three years, tech behemoths fueled a bull market on the promise that AI advancements would increase efficiency and profitability. But investor skepticism and Middle East instability have pushed the Nasdaq to its worst week in nearly a year, with 10 weekly losses over the past 11 weeks.

Two of the biggest drains since late October are two of the biggest AI spenders — Microsoft (down 34%) and Meta (down 29%). Even Nvidia is down nearly 20% from its October high. As a group, Magnificent Seven shares are down 8%.

Microsoft is having its worst quarter since the 2008 financial crisis, down 25% as shareholders recoil from continued AI infrastructure spending. There are also fears that startups like OpenAI and Anthropic will create agents that can replace Microsoft products.

The so-called TACO trade (Trump Always Chickens Out) is losing its effectiveness — crude prices soared to $110 per barrel, stoking inflation fears, even after Trump extended the Iran peace deadline by 10 days. Israel threatened escalation and Iran has resisted peace talks. However, Big Tech stocks could be viewed as a relative bargain at current levels.
