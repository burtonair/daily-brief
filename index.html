Iran War Snarls Global Supply Chains as Conflict Enters Sixth Day

The nearly week-old Iran war raged on Thursday, expanding to more countries and further imperiling the seas and skies across the Middle East — with oil markets, food supply chains, and consumer goods all feeling the ripple effects. Brew 1%

The US and Israel continued bombing targets in Iran on Thursday, while Iran retaliated with strikes across the region. An Iranian drone injured four at an airport in neighboring Azerbaijan. Iran's foreign minister, Abbas Araghchi, said the country isn't asking for a ceasefire and sees no reason to negotiate.

In the US, a legislative effort to restrict President Trump's ability to conduct military action in Iran failed after Republicans in the House followed the Senate in voting it down. Trump also told Axios that he wants to be involved in choosing Iran's new supreme leader to replace Ayatollah Khamenei, who was killed in an Israeli airstrike over the weekend.

Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz — which carries 20% of the world's oil — has slowed traffic to a trickle through the vital trade route. Farmers have been hit by soaring nitrogen fertilizer costs, as much of the supply comes from the Middle East. Experts say this could undermine global agricultural production and push up grocery prices.

Victoria's Secret said the conflict has caused shipping delays, while Abercrombie expects "sales headwinds" due to sourcing hitches in the region. Treacherous skies above Middle Eastern air hubs also threaten supply chains for pharmaceuticals and electronics transported by plane. See the latest updates here.

According to 1% Better, a senior Iranian cleric broadcast a call for violence against President Trump and Israeli leadership on state television. The conflict has killed over 1,000 in Iran, 70 in Lebanon, and roughly a dozen in Israel, while disrupting global oil supply and international shipping. How the Iran Conflict Is Widening, in Maps.

Make The Most Of This

Spring breathes freshness and renewal into our world with warmer air, sun-filled days, and bursts of new foliage and colors. And after a long winter, we can't wait to rush outside and enjoy it all.

But in our eagerness, in all those spring activities — from baseball games to camping trips — we may miss the gentle reminder inherent in the changing seasons. Just as the flowers bloom and trees grow, our children are growing, too. As each season passes, so does time, marching us closer to the day our kids will go out on their own and leave home.

Meditating on this isn't meant to cast a shadow over the joy and lightness of spring; rather, it's nature's way of asking us to reflect. Are we making the most of our precious time with them? What do we have to show for the season that just passed? Are we wasting time we could be spending with our family on things that drain us? Maybe we fell into a rut this winter — but that's also the beauty of the spring, right? That we can, as the Philip Larkin poem goes, "begin afresh, afresh, afresh."

Don't let yourself ignore spring's reminder of time's passing. Don't settle into old patterns and busy schedules that you'll end up regretting years from now. This season is an invitation to clear out the mental and physical clutter holding you back from spending your time and energy on the people you love most.

Turn an Investment Memo into a Polished Slide Deck in 5 Minutes

Learn how to use Manus to turn a structured investment memo into a professional, ready-to-present slide deck in Google Slides. You can try this with any type of memo with structured, formulaic sections.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with a memo PDF that has sections like: market analysis, team background, business diligence, traction, SWOT analysis, and deal dynamics. You can use Sequoia's 2014 DoorDash memo as a test case.
  2. Go to manus.im and sign up. Click New task > Create slides, attach your PDF, and enter the prompt: "Turn this investment memo into a slide deck"
  3. That's it. Manus will create the deck for you — it usually one-shots the layout. You can edit or present the deck directly in Manus, or ask it to update specific slides.
  4. When done, download your slides as PPTX, PDF, or click "Convert to Google Slides" to save the deck perfectly formatted in Google Slides.
Pro Tip:

Attach an example deck with your memo and tell Manus to style your new deck in the same way. This gives you precise control over visual design and branding with zero extra effort.

OpenAI's GPT-5.4 Clears Human Bar on Desktop Work Rundown

OpenAI shipped GPT-5.4 just two days after rolling out 5.3 Instant, calling it "our best model ever" — with VP of Science Kevin Weil pointing to record scores across math, science, coding, and real-world desktop navigation.

The model scored 75% on OSWorld-V, a benchmark testing real desktop navigation — 3 points above the human baseline of 72.4% and twice what GPT-5.2 managed. GPT-5.4 also supports up to 1 million tokens of context and a new "x-high reasoning effort" setting, letting agents plan and execute across longer tasks that can take hours.

  • Available now as GPT-5.4 Thinking for Plus, Team, and Pro users
  • Won or matched professionals 83% of the time on GDPval (a 44-job knowledge-work benchmark) — up from 71% for GPT-5.2
  • OAI researcher Noam Brown's take: "We see no wall."

The launch comes after a rough week of public sentiment for OpenAI, and signals a meaningful step forward for agentic AI, particularly for desktop use cases. Read OpenAI's announcement.

AI & Labor

Anthropic's Early-Warning System for AI Job Loss Rundown Brew

Anthropic published a study on AI's job impact, finding that while mass layoffs haven't hit broadly, the youngest workers are already getting squeezed out of the most AI-exposed fields.

The study uses "observed exposure," a metric gauging AI job displacement by comparing tasks AI can do to what they are already automating. Computer programmers top the exposure list at 75% task coverage, followed by customer service reps and data entry workers at 67%. About a third of the U.S. workforce sits at zero AI exposure — largely in hands-on roles like cooks, bartenders, and lifeguards.

  • No broad unemployment spike since ChatGPT's 2022 launch — but hiring into exposed fields for 22-to-25-year-olds fell 14% in that time
  • Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei has warned publicly about looming disruption on the jobs front

Separately, the Pentagon officially labeled Anthropic a "supply chain risk," which the company plans to challenge in court, even as reports suggest both sides have resumed deal talks.

Entertainment & AI

Netflix Acquires Ben Affleck's AI Filmmaking Startup InterPositive Rundown Brew

Netflix acquired InterPositive, a stealth AI filmmaking company Affleck started in 2022 — retaining all 16 staffers and bringing Affleck aboard as a senior adviser in a rare acquisition for the streaming giant.

InterPositive's technology trains models on a production's own footage, then handles post-production work like relighting, swapping backgrounds, and fixing continuity errors — it does not generate video from scratch. Affleck said he was "shocked" by how much engineering talent was pouring into AI video without any "artistic, filmmaking information whatsoever."

"We also need to preserve what makes storytelling human, which is judgment," Affleck said in a statement. The acquisition marks a rare move for Netflix, which deserted its bid for Warner Bros. Discovery last week. Read more via Variety.

Victoria's Secret Is Rebounding — Projecting Its Best Year in Half a Decade Brew

Victoria's Secret announced it's projecting at least $6.85 billion in full-year sales — a seven-year high — following a turnaround credited to CEO Hillary Super, who took over in 2024.

The Victoria's Secret Fashion Show, which took a backlash-fueled hiatus, has returned featuring a more inclusive roster of models. The retailer posted its third straight quarter of same-store sales growth — its longest such stretch in at least four years.

Shares fell 12% Thursday due to multimillion-dollar expenses related to a past acquisition, but the stock is up more than 200% since an April 2025 low point. The company did note that the Iran conflict has caused shipping delays affecting its supply chain.

United Airlines to Boot Passengers Who Play Audio Without Headphones Brew

United Airlines updated its contract of carriage last Friday, adding a rule that passengers who listen to audio or video without headphones can be kicked off the flight and potentially banned from the airline.

The change rolls out as United expands Starlink internet service across its fleet. Other carriers — including Frontier, Delta, and Southwest — suggest using headphones, but United appears to be the first to implement actual consequences for speaker use. The policy is seen as a customer-friendly move as airlines attempt to rebuild goodwill after years of adding fees and cutting perks.
