Deep Dive: Finance

Is Private Equity Past Its Prime? Brew

Private equity funds now own a record number of US businesses—from dentists' offices to carwashes and national bookstore chains—but declining returns have made investors skeptical about the industry's future.

Over the last 15 years, the number of US companies under PE ownership more than doubled, from just over 6,000 in 2010 to almost 13,000 as of the end of 2025, accounting for about 7% of US GDP. But rising interest rates have depressed valuations and made it harder to find buyers.

From 2022 to Q3 2025, annual returns from an index of PE funds were 5.8%—only about half of the S&P 500's 11.6% during the same period. President Trump recently signed an executive order to make it easier for Americans' 401(k)s to invest in PE funds, but critics say the industry needs Americans' retirement piggy banks more than they need PE.

AI Security Rundown

The Biggest AI Security Risk: Agent Compromise

As enterprises deploy agentic systems that access data, invoke tools, and make decisions independently, those agents become a new attack surface.

Cisco's DJ Sampath warns: "Agents can be hijacked, impersonated, or manipulated to exfiltrate data or execute unauthorized commands at machine speed." The first thing to harden is the agent infrastructure—the protocols that link agents to tools, data, and each other. Standards like Model Context Protocol scaled faster than the security around them.

The right model isn't "human-out-of-the-loop"—it's "AI-in-the-loop." Let agents handle routine tasks at speed, but keep humans as the authority where impact is high.

Sports Brew

Private Equity's Growing Stake in Professional Sports

The New England Patriots made history this year as the first team backed by private equity to reach the Super Bowl.

There are now 74 major professional US sports teams with ties to private equity. The NBA leads with 20 of 30 teams having PE connections, while the NFL and NHL each have 10 of 32. PE made its first venture into college sports in December with University of Utah.

AI Strategy Rundown

Why "Intelligence Should Be Owned, Not Rented"

Cisco's DJ Sampath argues that adding a generative API to an existing product isn't a strategy—it's a feature. Companies that are "a thin shim on top of a model—their days are numbered."

Sustainable advantage comes when intelligence is embedded into the product itself, trained on contextual enterprise data that improves continuously. "I don't believe the future belongs to a handful of centralized models controlled by a few providers. Intelligence should be owned by enterprises—and ultimately, by individuals."
