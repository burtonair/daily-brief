The Iran War’s Economic Scars May Be Irreversible, IMF Warns

The US–Iran ceasefire is holding, but the economic fallout—from a toll-gated Strait of Hormuz to fertilizer shortages—will outlast any peace deal. The IMF says it will lower its 2026 global growth forecast to account for permanent losses. Morning Brew

The precarious two-week ceasefire held yesterday, but its durability hinges on talks in Pakistan this weekend, where VP JD Vance will negotiate a longer-term accord. Israel has also agreed to talk directly with Lebanon to end its campaign against Hezbollah, which Iran insists was a condition of its ceasefire.

Iran released a safe-transit map for the mine-laden Strait of Hormuz but is charging ships $2 million to transit—an illegal tolling system that violates international law. Traffic remains below 10% of prewar levels, and analysts expect oil prices to stay elevated through year-end.

Natural gas disruptions are driving up nitrogen and phosphate fertilizer prices—a third of the world’s supply passes through Hormuz. Goldman Sachs warns the disruptions could last for a while, impacting electricity costs globally.

Columnist Daily Dad

Fretting Is Not a Service

You mean well. You do it because you care. You do it because you want them to be comfortable, to be safe, because you want to connect. But all the fretting? It’s not doing anything for them. It’s just you trying to soothe yourself. It’s about your own guilt and anxiety.

They don’t need you to call and ask about every little detail. They don’t need you to check fifty times if they have their lunch or remembered their school project. They don’t need you to hover. They don’t need you to control every outcome.

Fretting is not a substitute for true connection. It’s not a substitute for real conversation. There are so many things you could do instead: actually be curious about what’s going on with them, respect their space and boundaries, listen without trying to fix everything.

Stop bothering them. Stop making your worries their problem. If you want to serve them, be steady. Be present. Be someone they can talk to.

Automate Your Business with Custom Notion Agents

Build Notion Custom Agents that run your company’s recurring work on a schedule—automating inbound leads, campaigns, accounting, or any recurring job without adding complexity.

  1. In Notion, create two databases—Tasks (Name, Source, Priority, Status, Assigned To) for every to-do, and Reports so agents can log their work.
  2. Open Notion AI, click +Create custom agent, and prompt: “Create a Weekly Planner agent that reads my last 7 days of emails every Monday morning, adds action items into the Tasks database, and writes a summary into Reports.”
  3. Notion drafts the agent with a name, trigger, instructions, and data sources. Review, connect Gmail, confirm the schedule, and save.
  4. Run it on demand to test. Tighten the instructions if the output is off, then leave it running. Notion shows the next scheduled run, and the work is off your plate.

Pro tip: Clone the pattern for every recurring job—same two databases, different agent, with every run traceable in one place.

US Fertility Rate Drops to Another Record Low Morning Brew

The number of births per 1,000 women of reproductive age fell by about 1% last year—an all-time low, and down nearly 20% from two decades ago.

Despite a growing pronatalist movement, American women are waiting later to have children, while some are deciding not to due to concerns about the economy, climate change, and healthcare. Experts warn that a declining birth rate can be an economic “drag” on the US, reducing demand and the labor force.

Tech & AI

Oxford AI Catches Heart Failure Five Years Early Rundown AI

Researchers at Oxford introduced an AI system that detects invisible changes in heart fat from routine CT scans, flagging high-risk patients with 86% accuracy across 72,000 patients.

Fat around the heart shifts texture when the muscle beneath is inflamed—patterns invisible to doctors on any current scan. In the highest-risk group, 1 in 4 patients developed heart failure within five years, a 20x gap versus those the AI flagged as safe. Oxford is already working with regulators to bring the tool to NHS hospitals.
