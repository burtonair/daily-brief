Markets — Breaking

AI Chip Stocks Continue Their Historic Tear

Intel up 239%. Sandisk up 558%. Samsung past $1 trillion. The global chip frenzy is rewriting market records—and drawing dot-com comparisons.

Even if you housed a Party Size bag of Tostitos Scoops every day for the rest of the year, you couldn’t approximate the bewildering chip frenzy playing out in global markets. Intel, which sold a 9.9% equity stake to the government last year, is up 239% this year. Sandisk is up 558% YTD. South Korea’s Samsung surpassed a market cap of $1 trillion last Wednesday.

South Korea’s Kospi index is up 78% this year (the S&P 500 is up 8%), making it the seventh-largest stock market on the planet. But Samsung and SK Hynix account for more than 40% of the index, raising concentration risk.

Some market-watchers note eerie parallels with the dot-com bubble—last week was the best for the PHLX Semiconductor index since March 10, 2000. But unlike the dot-com era, today’s chip winners have real earnings: Micron is expected to finish the year with $77 billion in operating profit, and Samsung reported an 8x increase in Q1 operating profits.

Columnist Daily Dad

Do You Know Where They Are?

You’re on the other side of the planet. You are absolutely wrecked by the time zone. You are at the most important business meeting of your life, in the middle of some work crisis, or on the cusp of some incredible opportunity.

Do you know where your kids are? They’re not with you—they can’t be—but you should still know where they are. Are they at school right now? Are they just waking up? Is today the day their favorite game drops a new mod and they’re eagerly playing it? Are they excited about the sleepover this weekend or missing their best friend this week?

You can’t just check out. You can’t just leave that to your spouse or the babysitter. You can’t just get busy with your own stuff and forget. It’s hard to be gone, and you’re busy, but you have to stay connected. Squeeze in a call before dropoff. Send them a text to let them know you’re thinking about them before their game. Don’t lose track of the rhythms of their life—of your life—just because you’re away.

Because no matter how important your work is or where you are, the most important thing happening today is still happening at home, in their world.

Automate Any Manual Task with Codex

Learn how to let Codex click through any annoying, repetitive work using Computer Use on Mac or Windows.

  1. Open Codex, go to Plugins, find and enable the Computer Use plugin, and start a new task.
  2. Open the permissions menu and switch from Default permissions to Full access, then confirm any prompts.
  3. Give Codex something real to do—for example: “Open Chrome and debug this web page UI I’m developing. Click through, reproduce the bug I describe, then tell me what you think is causing it.”

Pro tip: Codex can automate repetitive workflows in local apps too—try it for Photoshop exports, Adobe Premiere cleanup, file renaming, or any other tool.

World Morning Brew

India’s Diet Coke Fans Are in Dire Straits

The Strait of Hormuz closure has created an aluminum can shortage in India, and since Diet Coke is only sold in cans there, fans are desperate.

Trade logistics in Asia depend heavily on tight supply chains and fossil fuels from the Middle East, and the Persian Gulf is responsible for about 9% of global aluminum production. Indian entrepreneurs have capitalized by throwing Diet Coke-themed parties, charging a cover and raffling off cans.

Nearly 10% of India’s adult population is diabetic, making the sugar-free drink a staple for health-focused consumers and Gen Zers alike.

AI Finds 100+ New Exoplanets Hidden in NASA Data

University of Warwick astronomers confirmed over 100 exoplanets using an AI system called RAVEN that scanned 4 years of NASA TESS data covering 2.2 million stars.

RAVEN handles detection, vetting, and confirmation in one shot, finding 31 exoplanets never before spotted, plus strange worlds that orbit their stars in under a day. Hundreds were found in the “Neptunian Desert,” a region so close to a star that Neptune-sized planets shouldn’t survive the heat. The system measures planet frequency at 10x the precision of previous systems—from smarter AI alone, not new hardware.
