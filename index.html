Shooter Targets White House Correspondents’ Dinner; Trump Evacuated

A 31-year-old California man armed with a shotgun, handgun, and knives rushed a security checkpoint outside Saturday’s White House Correspondents’ Dinner. A Secret Service agent was shot but survived thanks to a bulletproof vest. President Trump was evacuated from the ballroom. Morning Brew 1% Better

The suspected gunman was swiftly apprehended by law enforcement. Attorney General Todd Blanche said Trump and his Cabinet were likely the intended targets. The suspect left behind a manifesto denouncing the administration’s policies.

The suspect was identified as Cole Tomas Allen of Torrance, CA — a Caltech mechanical engineering graduate with a master’s in computer science. He traveled by train from Los Angeles to Washington and checked into the hotel the day before the event. He is expected to be charged in federal court today.

The shooting has raised questions about hotel security — the same venue where President Reagan survived an assassination attempt in 1981. Trump said the incident demonstrates why the $400 million White House ballroom he’s seeking to build is needed, though a private event like the dinner is unlikely to be held there.

Columnist Daily Dad

Don’t Weigh Them Down

You’ve seen a lot. You’ve been through a lot. You know what’s up. As a parent, you have to make sure that the insight you’ve gleaned from these experiences does not turn into cynicism — and if it does from time to time, that you shield your kids from it. Because any disillusionment you might feel about people or the world is not their fault and it does not serve them well.

The “poorest way to face life is with a sneer,” Theodore Roosevelt reminds us. What he’s talking about isn’t so much disillusion as it is contempt — cynical detachment and superiority, the kind that voids out creativity, collaboration, and connection. It is so easy to fall into the clutches of these forces, to let them affect your behavior. Little comments under your breath; making fun of stuff that makes you uncomfortable; rationalizing your avoidance of things you’d rather not do.

Your kids are a fresh start. They came into the world with fresh eyes, a full heart, and so much energy. Don’t deprive them of these forces for good. Don’t weigh them down with your baggage.

Lift them up. Encourage them. Let them be earnest. Let them care and try. Better yet, let them infect you with their sincerity and the clarity of their passion. Whatever you do, don’t let your cynicism infect them.

Anthropic’s AI Agents Broker Trades in ‘Project Deal’ Rundown AI
