The Big Business of Little Sweet Treats Brew

TikTok has transformed how Americans discover candy, from Swedish gummy shops to viral Dubai chocolate. The candy industry has never been more varied—or more challenged.

CandyTok is obsessed with the sugary heritage of Sweden, where kids pick-and-mix candy every Saturday in a tradition called lördagsgodis. Swedish candy chain BonBon has opened four new locations in New York City since 2023.

Freeze-dried candy has popped off—Mars Wrigley released astronaut Skittles, and the market is projected to more than double from $1.3 billion in 2024 to $3.1 billion in 2034. Meanwhile, pistachio paste-filled "Dubai chocolate" pushed nut prices up 35%.

But there's a bitter side: GLP-1 medications are rewiring cravings, a cocoa shortage inflated prices, and at least five states have restricted SNAP purchases of candy this year.

Social Media Brew

When the Sugar Rush Comes from TikTok

TikTok has introduced new vehicles for sweetness, from bougie Dubai chocolate to lip-shaped Swedish gummies. The algorithm rewards the most vibrantly colored, funkily shaped confections.

Trending now: Freeze-dried candy (astronaut Skittles, Jolly Ranchers), the world's hottest gummy bear, and chamoy-seasoned pickles stuffed with hot candy strips.

Nostalgia sells: Americans are reliving childhood with TikTok-viral classics like Ring Pops and Retro Sours, made to mimic discontinued Altoid Sours from the 1990s.

Corporate Brew

How Mars Went From Candy to Pet Care Superpower

Thanks to a savvy pivot nearly a century ago, Mars has become a $137 billion business that sells both Snickers and Pedigree.

Mars bought UK dog food company Chappie in 1935, then expanded with Whiskas and Kal Kan in the 1960s. In 2007, they took over Banfield Pet Hospital, followed by VCA's 800 animal hospitals for $9.1 billion.

Bottom line: Mars now owns 3,000+ veterinary clinics worldwide and runs neck-and-neck with Purina as the world's biggest pet care company, both reaching ~$22 billion revenue annually.

History Brew

The Holidays Belong to Candy

Nearly every holiday involves candy—but Halloween, Valentine's Day, and Easter stand out on the candy calendar.

Modern trick-or-treating didn't take off until the late 1940s. Richard Cadbury popularized heart-shaped chocolate boxes in the 1860s, and candy hearts that said "Be Mine" entered the conversation in 1902.

Valentine's Day 2026: 56% of US consumers were projected to buy candy, outpacing greeting cards and flowers.

Health Brew

Slinging Candy in a Wellness-Focused World

The practice of getting a sweet treat appears to be falling off in the US, with convenience store candy trips down 7.5% in the year leading up to last fall.

GLP-1 effect: About 1 in 8 US adults is taking a GLP-1 as of November 2025, and many users report the medication has rewired their cravings.

Dye concerns: Health Secretary RFK Jr. has been urging companies to phase out artificial dyes, and 25% of US adults now avoid artificial colors. Natural dyes are sourced from seasonal crops or even bugs.
