Musk Unveils $25B ‘Terafab’ to Build 50x the World’s AI Compute

Elon Musk unveiled Terafab, a joint Tesla-SpaceX-xAI chip facility aiming to produce a terawatt of AI compute per year — roughly 50x what the entire world outputs today — calling it “the most epic chip building exercise in history by far.” The Rundown AI

The Austin facility would handle logic, memory, packaging, and testing — a vertical integration Musk said exists nowhere else. Two chip types are planned: one for Tesla vehicles and Optimus robots, another space-grade chip for solar-powered AI satellites launched via Starship.

Musk said “no one wants AI computing centers in their backyard,” expecting space-based compute to undercut ground costs within 2–3 years. He pitched the Terafab as the first step toward a “galactic civilization” with a post-scarcity economy providing “abundance for everyone.”

Building a fab on this scale from scratch is an enormous bet, but the need for AI chip scaling is real — and Musk has made a career out of ignoring what the industry says can’t be done.

Columnist Daily Dad

Let Them Have Fewer Choices

The amount of choices we have today is not normal. Certainly, it’s quite new. When you were a kid — which was not that long ago — there were how many channels on TV? Even cable was nothing compared to streaming. There was no infinite scroll. And this is only discussing what we watch — there is essentially an unlimited choice in what we listen to, what we read, what we eat, where we travel.

It’s an unimaginable abundance… and ungodly stressful for your kids. It has to be. It’s stressful for you, isn’t it? Filmmaker Casey Neistat shared how his family has taken to watching movies on a big TV in their family room because the iPads were overwhelming and causing problems. They turn on a movie like “Moana” and watch it — no changing, no switching — it’s out of their hands. You could do the same thing turning on the radio in your car, handing over control to the DJ.

The bigness, the infiniteness of the world is wonderful, but oppressive if you’re too young. We can protect our kids from that. We can take some of the power out of their hands. In the future, they will have many decisions to make and many options. But for now? Let them have a taste of the smaller, more intimate, less overwhelming childhood that you got to experience. Shrink the amount of choices they have to make. Keep things simple.

Use Google Stitch to Redesign Your Entire Website for Free

In this guide, learn how to use Google Stitch to redesign an existing web page by uploading screenshots — a fast way to fix weak layouts, generate better variations, and create something you can hand off to a developer.

  1. Pick a single page on your website that needs improvement. Take a screenshot and think about what needs to change.
  2. Go to Google Stitch, add the screenshot in chat, and prompt: “Improve the layout of this page so the user sees more content and perceives more value. Make the content more accessible and reduce the dead space.”
  3. Select the generated page → Generate → Variations → Generate Variations to get four new page concepts. Choose the best one and go to More → Export.
  4. Export the design to Figma or download the new code, then rinse and repeat with as many pages as you like.

Pro tip: Click Export → AI Studio to build a live prototype with Google’s vibe coding tool.

Business — Retail

Walmart’s Digital Price Labels Could Change How You Shop Morning Brew

The retail giant plans to switch all US stores to digital shelf labels by year’s end, while new patents lay groundwork for AI-powered pricing.

Digital shelf labels are easier for employees to update than paper tags, but that also means it’s easier to raise prices — stoking concerns that stores could quickly adjust prices based on demand, like airline surge pricing.

Walmart recently secured patents for predicting demand and recommending prices, and for “dynamically and automatically” updating prices for markdowns. The company insists DSLs won’t be used for surge pricing. Legislation has been introduced in Congress and several states requiring paper shelf-pricing for large grocery stores.

World — Ukraine

Ukraine Retakes 150 Square Miles After SpaceX Cuts Russia’s Starlink 1% Better

SpaceX cut Russian forces’ Starlink internet access in February, limiting their battlefield communications and drone coordination.

Ukraine subsequently retook roughly 150 square miles in the Zaporizhzhia and Dnipropetrovsk regions, marking Kyiv’s largest domestic territorial gains in over two years. Russia had been illegally obtaining Starlink terminals through middlemen.

Losing access forced Russian commanders to rely on interceptable radio communications, giving Ukrainian forces advance intelligence on troop movements. Ukrainian troops estimate Russian coordination has recovered to about 60% of pre-cutoff levels.

Tech — AgTech

Halter’s AI ‘Cowgorithm’ Nears $2B Valuation The Rundown AI

New Zealand’s Halter is nearing a new round led by Peter Thiel’s Founders Fund that would value the AI cattle-collar startup at $2 billion.

Halter’s solar-powered collars create virtual fences and let ranchers herd cattle remotely via app using vibration and audio cues. The collars send 6,000+ data points per minute to what the company calls the “Cowgorithm” — tracking health and optimizing grazing patterns.

Founded on a 300-cow New Zealand dairy farm, Halter has collected 7 billion+ hours of animal behavior data and is now expanding into the US market.
