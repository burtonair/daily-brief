The Fight Against AI Data Centers Escalates After Shooting at Councilor’s Home

Thirteen bullets were fired at the home of an Indianapolis city councilor who voted in favor of a data center project, as states across the country move to ban or restrict the controversial buildings powering the AI boom. Morning Brew

Councilor Ron Gibson was targeted with gunfire days after voting to approve a data center in his district. A note left at the scene read “No Data Centers.” No one was physically harmed.

Maine is expected to place a moratorium on data centers consuming more than 20 megawatts this spring. Statewide bans are on the table in nine states, with Ohio activists collecting signatures for a November ballot measure. Port Washington, WI, voted yesterday on stopping future data center development.

A new study shows data centers create heat islands within a 6-mile radius that can cause pollution and deaths — but they’re also job creators. The response in Maine could serve as a “canary in the coal mine” for officials nationwide.

Columnist Daily Dad

They Could Need You — That’s Why

You’re traveling. You’re far away. The kids are in good hands. This is your little break. But do you still need to be on top of it? On top of them? Yes. Because they could need you.

Flea, the bassist from the Red Hot Chili Peppers, once explained that the reason he quit doing drugs — even though he only did them recreationally when he was on the road — was the thought that his daughter could call him in the middle of the night, could need him emotionally, and he would be unavailable. It occurred to him that even spiritually to be out of it — to be checked out — was wrong.

When we’re away, we have to stay on their timeline, still know where they are and what they’re doing. We have to be checked in and connected. Be reachable. Be clear-headed. Be present — even from a distance.

Because they might need us. And we should always be available for them.

How To — AI Training Rundown AI

Get to Inbox Zero with This Claude Prompt

Run email triage once in Claude, then have it write a recurring task prompt for your inbox. You’ll end up with a daily cleanup workflow based on your real rules.

  1. Open Claude with Gmail access and run one triage session on your unread inbox to show Claude what matters before automating.
  2. Prompt: “Generate an interactive email triage report for the last 24 hours. Sort each email into: Needs response, Needs attention, Archive, Archive and unsubscribe.”
  3. Review, correct misfires, then prompt: “Turn this workflow into a recurring task prompt for my inbox, with my common senders, archive, and unsubscribe rules.”
  4. Save the prompt as a Claude Cowork scheduled task so it runs every morning automatically.

Pro tip: Set up Gmail rules around the labels Claude adds — “Needs Response” and “Needs Attention” can be auto-starred, and Archive emails can be auto-archived.

Economy

Upper Middle Class Is Now America’s Largest Income Group

The ranks of Americans earning $133k–$400k have swelled from 10% in 1979 to 31% in 2024, while near-poverty households declined. Morning Brew

According to an American Enterprise Institute analysis, upper middle class families are often white-collar professionals in dual-income households who have benefitted from rising wages in fields like software engineering. Many are boomers raking in sizable pension payments from stock market gains.

This group’s spending habits are behind the rise of premium goods like first-class plane tickets — a major driver of U.S. economic growth. But economic inequality has also grown, with the poorest 5% seeing their earnings decline.
