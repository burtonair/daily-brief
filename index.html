Oil Soars Past $100 as Houthis Attack Saudi Tankers in Key Strait

Iran-backed Houthi militants struck two Saudi oil tankers in the Bab el-Mandeb Strait, pushing crude above $100 per barrel for the first time since May — dragging down stocks, spiking bond yields, and adding $1.6 billion in expected fuel costs for American Airlines alone.

The Houthi militia, which controls part of Yemen, attacked two Saudi tankers Thursday traversing the Bab el-Mandeb Strait — the critical artery through which Saudi Arabia ships almost two-thirds of oil that would otherwise transit the blockaded Strait of Hormuz. Oil jumped from $72 a barrel earlier this month to above $100 per barrel, spooking markets. The Nasdaq fell 2.15%, S&P 1.21%, Dow 0.97%. Tesla added a separate bruise, tumbling 14.5% on AI spending fears. Morning Brew

The escalation compounds an already dire energy picture. US gas prices climbed past $4 per gallon this week, up from $3.94 a month ago and $2.94 in February when the conflict began. American Airlines revised its yearly earnings estimate down for the second time in three months, citing an extra $1.6 billion in expected fuel costs. Higher oil has also pushed bond yields up, increasing odds of the Federal Reserve holding rates elevated. The US emergency oil reserve recently dropped to its lowest level since 1983.

Saudi oil could still reach global markets via the Suez Canal, but at longer delivery times and higher costs. President Trump said he will inflict “major military punishment” on the Houthis and Iran for any future attacks. Analysts warn that if the conflict continues to choke Gulf shipments — or if attacks hit energy production infrastructure — prices could push even higher still.

Columnist  |  Daily Dad

Everybody Has Their Uphill

It’s been tough. The parent-teacher conferences. The doctor visits. The appointments with specialists. Sleepless nights. Arguments with your spouse. Stressing about how you’re going to afford it all. Worrying you won’t be able to get your kids what they need. And we feel bad — for them, for ourselves — that they have to deal with this, that we have to deal with it.

But then you talk with another parent and you realize: man, I got off easy.

Bert Kreischer once talked about thinking he had it bad because his daughter had dyslexia. Then another parent opened up about what their child was going through — something even heavier. From that conversation he learned: “When you’re a parent, everybody’s got their uphill. Some people’s uphill is worse than other people’s uphill, but everyone’s uphill is an uphill.”

Every family is climbing something, even as we’re climbing our own. We can’t clearly see or know or imagine what anyone else is going through. But we can be kind. We can be understanding. We can have empathy for other parents on this journey.

OpenAI and Anthropic Shipped Dueling Voice Upgrades Within Minutes of Each Other

OpenAI moved ChatGPT Voice into its desktop app and into Codex, while Anthropic upgraded Claude’s voice to its smarter Opus and Sonnet models and added Gmail and Slack integrations. Both dropped at nearly the exact same time Thursday.

OpenAI’s GPT-Live voice is now in macOS and Windows desktop apps for paid plans, letting users talk to agents in Codex and ChatGPT Work via speech. A custom hotkey calls it over any app; Bloomberg reports the tech may power OpenAI’s upcoming home speaker hardware.

Claude’s voice mode previously ran only on the small Haiku model; it now runs on Opus and Sonnet and can connect to Gmail and Slack. Anthropic still lags on turn-by-turn speaking — GPT-Live listens, thinks, and replies simultaneously, while Claude still takes turns. Speech is increasingly the primary way people work with AI, and Anthropic has ground to make up.

Peacock Turned a Profit for the First Time Ever in Q2

NBCUniversal’s streaming service posted its first quarterly profit ever — $189 million EBITDA — driven by the NBA playoffs, World Cup, and Love Island USA. It’s taken nearly six years to get here.
