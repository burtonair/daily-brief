This Is What Matters

Nobody wants to be like their parents — the clueless ones, the uncool ones, the ones who forgot what it was like to be a kid. We'd like to think we've succeeded. Because we're younger in spirit, more aware, more informed. We know what an influencer is, after all.

But here's the cold hard truth: you're not different. You're like all the other parents that ever were. The poet Audre Lorde's children had to break this news. "You think just because you're lesbians you're so different from the rest of them but you're not," they told their moms. "You're just like all the other parents." Audre fancied herself a revolutionary — but where it mattered, she was as ordinary and out-of-touch as everybody else who ever procreated. And so are you.

This isn't something to be ashamed of. It's just the reality of bringing life into this world — of being responsible for young, vulnerable souls who can't fully take care of themselves yet. What matters isn't how you see yourself. What matters is that you're present. What matters is that you love your kids. What matters is that you be what, and who, they need you to be. Accept it. Embrace it. Love it.

Use GPT-Live to Plan Any Trip — Just Talk It Through

GPT-Live lets you have a live voice conversation with ChatGPT while it simultaneously researches in the background. For trip planning, this is the most natural workflow yet: no typing, no itinerary templates, just a conversation that turns into a usable plan.

  1. On desktop, open ChatGPT.com in a browser (not the desktop app) and start a Voice chat.
  2. Lead with the outcome: "Plan a full [CITY] itinerary for [DATES]. Decide the hotels, restaurants, and things to do."
  3. Interrupt as the plan takes shape — change the budget, add a longer work block, ask for a weather backup. GPT-Live keeps researching as you redirect it.
  4. Close Voice when satisfied. Ask ChatGPT to output the final research as a shareable memo you can save or forward.
