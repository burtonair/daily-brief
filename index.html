US Blockades Strait of Hormuz as Iran Peace Talks Collapse

President Trump ordered a naval blockade of the vital waterway after weekend negotiations in Pakistan fell apart over nuclear demands. Oil surged past $104 per barrel. Morning Brew 1% Better

After more than 21 hours of face-to-face negotiations in Islamabad, the US and Iran failed to reach a deal to end six weeks of war. Tehran demanded the release of $6 billion in frozen assets, guarantees around its nuclear program, and the right to charge transit fees through the strait. The US called nuclear disarmament non-negotiable.

Trump announced on Truth Social that the US Navy will blockade the vital waterway to ensure Iran can't ship its oil. He also warned the US will "interdict" any foreign ships that paid Iran for passage through an illegal tolling system where vessels reportedly paid $2 million to cross.

US Central Command confirmed the blockade would begin at 10am ET today. Oil markets responded immediately: US crude surged 8% to over $104 per barrel and Brent jumped 7%. Energy analysts warn elevated oil prices may persist even after a ceasefire resolution. Despite the talks failing, US officials said negotiations were cordial and an Iranian official said future talks were possible.

Columnist Daily Dad

You Need to Back Them Up

Young Gloria Johnson didn't want to stand up for the Pledge of Allegiance. She didn't want to say the school prayer, either. This would have been an act of ordinary teenage rebellion — except Gloria was Black, living in the American South, and a promising student with a path to college when few young Black girls were afforded such an opportunity.

Naturally, her mother was worried. Where was this defiance coming from? What would this cost her daughter? But instead of assuming — or shutting it down — her mother decided to ask. As David Halberstam details in The Children, she asked, "How seriously do you feel about this?" When Gloria replied she couldn't believe in it more and was prepared to give up college if necessary, her mother told her she would back her up all the way.

Imagine how difficult that must have been. All she wanted was for her daughter to have a better life than she had. She herself had to put up with so much she didn't believe in, follow so many customs just to survive in a racist world, and now her daughter was risking it all. Yet she put those doubts aside, went to the school principal, and convinced him that fighting Gloria on this would be a costly and embarrassing battle.

The fight did cost Gloria — she was shunned and snubbed for awards at school. But with her mom's backing, she rode it out, made it to college, and ultimately helped usher in the fall of segregation as a pioneer of the sit-in movements. Stories like this are why we have to back our kids up, why we can't crush their rebellions. We want to encourage them to question, to stand on principle, to believe in themselves. A better future depends on it.

Generate Editable Infographics in 15 Minutes with AI

Turn raw research into an editable infographic using Perplexity, Gemini, and Canva Magic Layers — going from a data-heavy topic to a graphic you can fix and ship instead of rebuilding manually.

  1. Use your preferred AI to research the infographic data, prompting: "Do deep research on [topic]. Look for outliers and narrative angles. Our end goal is four infographic concepts recapping the most interesting data trends"
  2. Turn the concepts into image prompts: "Give me a prompt for each concept that I can paste into an image generator. Make data easy to copy and paste"
  3. Give those prompts to Gemini, generate some options, and download the best PNG. Then, upload it to Canva, click Edit, open Magic Studio, and Magic Layers
  4. Canva will make the image editable, letting you clean up the layers, fix text, swap incorrect images, and tighten the layout for near-perfect infographics

Pro tip: Skip the research step when you already have a memo, case study, or report. Feed it into Perplexity or Gemini and turn the strongest data into an infographic prompt.

Mississippi Runs Out of Liquor After Warehouse System Meltdown

The state's only wholesale alcohol warehouse nuked its own computer system, leaving stores out of business during their most profitable months.

Mississippi is one of 17 Alcoholic Beverage Control states where the state handles alcohol distribution. Its single wholesale liquor warehouse, run by Ruan Transport Corporation, closed for two weeks in January for inventory — but also tore out old conveyor belts in favor of a new packing system that relied on humans. Then Ruan allegedly didn't hire humans.

By the time temporary workers arrived, there were 200,000 backorders. Many liquor stores' most profitable month of the year turned into Dry January. The state is planning a new warehouse, but it won't open until 2027.

America's Restaurant Dishwasher Crisis

Dishwasher jobs rank among the hardest-to-fill positions in the US restaurant industry, squeezed by tougher immigration enforcement and declining interest from younger workers.

Foreign-born workers account for roughly 20% of restaurant industry jobs. With federal enforcement tightening, major chain operators including First Watch and Darden Restaurants have named dishwasher staffing their single biggest employment concern heading into 2026.

Dishwashers average around $32,500 a year, placing them in the bottom third of restaurant pay. Replacing one hourly worker now costs an estimated $2,700, up from $2,300 the year prior. One sushi chain has begun importing $15,000 robotic dishwashers from Japan to fill the gap.

AI Finds GLP-1 Side Effects That Clinical Trials Missed

Penn researchers fed over 400K Reddit posts about Ozempic and Mounjaro into AI models, pulling out side effects that clinical trials hadn't caught.

The team used GPT and Gemini to map posts by 67K users to standardized medical terms in a study published in Nature, covering 5+ years of real discussions. Nearly half the sample reported at least one side effect, flagging menstrual irregularities, chills, and hot flashes not reflected in current drug labels.

Fatigue ranked as the second most common complaint despite barely showing up in clinical trial reporting. AI is compressing parts of drug discovery, but it's also increasing the novelty of what's entering the market — making real-world monitoring like this increasingly critical.
