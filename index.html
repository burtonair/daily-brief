Artemis II Crew Sends Back Stunning Photos on Way to the Moon

The four astronauts aboard NASA's Orion capsule are on their way to becoming the first humans to reach the moon since 1972, snapping breathtaking photos of Earth along the way. Morning Brew

After Wednesday's launch and a 5-minute, 50-second engine burn on Thursday, the crew has left Earth's orbit and is on their test flight path to the moon. The mission is historic for many reasons: the crew will travel the farthest distance humans have ever gone in space.

It's also the first launch with a toilet aboard the spacecraft — a $23 million, six-year development that replaced the Apollo-era bag-and-tube system (which once produced a rogue floating turd during Apollo 10). The extremely loud space toilet was briefly offline early in the mission but fully operational by bedtime. Scientists hope this fly-by leads to a moon landing in 2028.

Politics Morning Brew

Inside Trump's Military-Heavy, $2.2 Trillion Budget Proposal

The White House wants a 42% boost in military spending — the biggest single-year increase since WWII — while slashing domestic programs deemed "woke, weaponized, and wasteful."

The proposed $1.5 trillion defense budget would fund more munitions and ships, higher servicemember salaries, and a missile defense system inspired by Israel's Iron Dome. Trump is also seeking $28.5 billion more for immigration enforcement, $10 billion for DC beautification, and $150 million to rebuild Alcatraz as a working prison.

On the chopping block: nonmilitary programs would be cut by 10% ($73 billion). The SBA would lose 67% of its funding, the EPA 52%. NASA faces a 23% cut, and $15 billion in clean energy funding would be redirected to fossil fuels and AI supercomputers.

Fiscal watchdogs say the plan could tack trillions onto the $39 trillion federal debt. Note: This is just a proposal — only Congress can set the annual budget, and lawmakers largely rebuffed Trump's domestic cuts last time around.
