Breaking — Aviation

Two Pilots Killed in LaGuardia Runway Crash Between Air Canada Jet and Fire Truck

An Air Canada regional jet carrying 72 passengers from Montreal collided with a Port Authority fire truck while landing at LaGuardia Airport late Sunday night, killing both pilots and hospitalizing roughly 40 people. 1% Better Morning Brew

The fire truck had received clearance to cross the runway when the collision occurred. Audio captured an air traffic controller urgently warning the truck to stop before impact. The crash severed the cockpit and ejected a flight attendant, who survived.

LaGuardia was closed Monday morning and reopened in the afternoon, but about 600 flights were canceled and delays are expected through Tuesday morning. The NTSB is leading the investigation alongside Canadian authorities.

Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy noted LaGuardia currently operates below its target of 37 certified controllers. A minute-by-minute breakdown of the crash is available from the Wall Street Journal.

Columnist Daily Dad

Take The Opportunity. Always.

They want to go swimming, but it’s a little cold. They’re suddenly asking about their rollerblades, which you just put away in the garage. They’re asking about playing in the snow, but it’s a pain to get all that winter gear on. They asked to go to the park, even though you don’t have much time.

In short, they’ve asked to go spend time outside… and you have a problem with that? This is what you say you want! This is what you say the iPad is interfering with! This is what you say that kids these days don’t do anymore!

So don’t get in the way. Facilitate. Don’t complain. Encourage. Support it. Make it memorable. Help build the habit. Take the opportunity. Do what you’ve got to do. Always. Every time.

Being cold and wet. Being bored. Being tired. Being quiet. Screwing up. These are things our kids need to experience. Pick up Steven Rinella’s Outdoor Kids in an Inside World — a great resource for the perennial struggle against screens and comfort. Nobody wants an inside kid who can’t step away from their screen. Shrink the amount of choices they have to make. Keep things simple.

How To — The Rundown AI

Use Claude to Find What’s Actually Eating Space on Your Computer

If you use AI to code, you’re likely eating up a ton of storage space without knowing it. Here’s how to use Claude as a storage-cleanup copilot:

  1. Start Claude or Claude Code and prompt: “I need to free up Mac storage, but I don’t want to delete anything important. Ask me about my tools, find storage culprits, and help me investigate before suggesting any cleanup commands.”
  2. Answer the questions and ask Claude to rank the culprits by size and risk. It should give you terminal commands to check the size of top storage-eaters.
  3. Work through each category. Have Claude explain what the files are, why they get large, what is safe to remove, and what should be reviewed manually.
  4. Prompt: “Start with the lowest-risk cleanup wins first. For each one, explain what would be removed, how much space it might save, and anything I should double-check before deleting it.”

Pro tip: Ask Claude to turn this into a reusable skill file that you can feed into any AI.

Business — Gambling

Prediction Markets Are Booming — and That’s Why Congress Wants Them Gone Morning Brew

Kalshi reported 90% of its trading volume during the NFL season was on sports. March Madness drove $2.67 billion in weekly trades on Kalshi alone.

Prediction markets exploit a loophole: the CFTC monitors them federally as “swaps,” not bets, letting platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket operate in all 50 states — including where sports betting is illegal. Nevada won a temporary ban last week.

Despite the regulatory risk, Polymarket just became an MLB licensing partner and Kalshi recently raised $1 billion at a $22 billion valuation.

Nobody Wants Ads on Their Samsung Fridge Morning Brew

A pilot program introducing banner ads on $1,800+ Samsung smart fridges has triggered fierce customer backlash.

Owners can turn off banner ads for Tide and Samsung water filters, but doing so also disables the news, weather, and calendar widget. Full-screen browser ads can’t be disabled at all. “I will never buy a Samsung appliance again,” one owner told the WSJ.

Samsung’s “Screens Everywhere” campaign includes washers, dryers, and ovens. LG, Whirlpool, and GE have said they won’t put ads on their smart home screens — for now.

Tech — AI

Luma AI’s Uni-1 Thinks Before It Creates The Rundown AI

Luma AI rolled out Uni-1, an image model that processes text and visuals through the same pipeline — reasoning about what it’s asked before and while it generates.

Uni-1 runs on the same architecture type as GPT Image 1.5 and Nano Banana Pro, processing text and images in a single pipeline instead of diffusion. At ~$0.09 per image at 2K resolution, it undercuts Nano Banana Pro’s $0.134 rate by roughly a third.

If the same system extends into video, voice, and interactive worlds as Luma is teasing, Uni-1 could set the foundation for one model that handles all creative work.
