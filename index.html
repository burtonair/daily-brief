The World's First Trillionaire: Musk Crosses $1.1T After SpaceX Soars 19% on Debut

SpaceX's first day of trading sent its stock surging from $135 to nearly $161 — making Elon Musk richer than the GDP of most countries and the first human ever to hold a trillion-dollar fortune.

After the biggest IPO in history, SpaceX closed Friday up 19% at $160.95 with a valuation of $2.1 trillion — the sixth most valuable public company in the US, bigger than Tesla. Musk, who owns nearly half of available shares, saw his net worth catapult to $1.1 trillion according to Forbes, a level of wealth with no precedent in human history.

To put it in perspective: the founders of Google, Amazon, Oracle, and Meta combined have roughly $1.3 trillion. Only 21 countries have GDPs over $1 trillion. If you laid out a trillion dollar bills end to end, they would cover 200 round trips to the moon. With $1 trillion, you could give every person on Earth $122 — or buy every piece of property in Houston with $121 billion to spare.

Musk's inner circle of early SpaceX investors also made a killing, minting new billionaires, while early employees with equity scored millions. The broader market rose with SpaceX's debut, with all three major indices closing in the green.

Don't Fritter It Away

"These are not choices you're going to be proud of. When you look back and wonder where the time went, where their childhood went, it's going to break your heart to have to account for how much of it you wasted."

This week's Daily Dad returns to a theme that doesn't get easier no matter how many times it lands: the slow, daily frittering of time with your kids. Not the dramatic failures — the quiet ones. The evenings that dissolved into screens. The weekends that vanished into logistics. The summers that felt like they should've been more.

On the flip side, there's this: with summer break upon us, the small things done consistently become the traditions that bond and define a family. Not the big trips — the after-dinner walks, the bad movies on a Friday, the ritual of nothing in particular. It's these moments, repeated across years, that your kids will carry with them.

The podcast puts it a different way this week: letting kids be bored isn't neglect — it's the gift of developing the ability to be alone, to entertain themselves, to not need constant input. The most AI-proof skill there is. Ryan Holiday's reading this week is Kate Bowler's Everything Happens for a Reason: "I keep having the same unkind thought: I am preparing for death and everyone else is on Instagram."

Iran Deal Inches Closer: Both Sides Agree on Wording, Mediators Still Finalizing

Pakistan's PM says the US and Iran have agreed to the text of a peace deal — a significant step forward after months of escalation, near-strikes, and drone incidents in the Strait of Hormuz.

Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif posted on X Friday that "peace has never been this close," saying the agreed-upon text includes Iran opening the Strait of Hormuz and getting economic incentives in exchange for meeting additional US demands. The status of Iran's nuclear program is reportedly left for future negotiations.

Officials from both countries appeared to confirm a deal was near while stressing nothing had been signed. The announcement comes after last week's whiplash — Trump posted the US would strike Iran "VERY HARD TONIGHT," then pivoted hours later to claiming a deal announcement was "within days." Two Iranian drones targeting commercial ships were shot down by the US military in the Strait of Hormuz during that tense period.

DOJ Clears Paramount's $111 Billion Takeover of Warner Bros. Discovery — No Strings Attached

After months of drama and opposition from Hollywood heavyweights, the Justice Department approved the Paramount Skydance / WBD merger without requiring any divestitures — a rare clean sweep in a mega-deal review.

The Justice Department's Antitrust Division approved the deal with no concessions required, according to Politico — a significant win for a merger that has faced persistent opposition from major movie industry figures. The combined entity would create a media giant spanning Paramount Pictures, CBS, CNN, HBO, Max, and Warner Bros.

It's not quite a Hollywood ending yet: the deal could still face legal challenges from states including California, where the attorney general has been reviewing it. But federal clearance removes the biggest hurdle and puts the merger on track to close.

Anthropic Disables Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Globally — US Gov Order Bars Foreign National Access

In an unprecedented government intervention in the AI industry, Anthropic says it has been ordered to bar foreign nationals from accessing its most advanced models due to national security concerns.

Anthropic said it was disabling all access to its advanced Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models to comply with a US government order barring their use by foreign nationals. The company did not disclose which agency issued the order or the specific national security rationale, but the directive affects any non-US-citizen trying to access the two flagship model families.

The news follows a difficult week for Anthropic's Fable launch: the model was already under fire after scientists discovered it was silently downgrading responses on AI research topics without disclosure. Anthropic apologized and added on-screen alerts. The compounded controversy gives OpenAI an opening ahead of its expected GPT-5.6 release later this month.
