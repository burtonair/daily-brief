Trump Admin Plans to Loosen Federal Weed Restrictions

The White House is on the verge of reclassifying marijuana from Schedule I to Schedule III—the biggest shift in federal cannabis policy in decades—opening the door to more research and tax relief for the cannabis industry. Morning Brew

Multiple outlets reported that Trump administration officials are on the verge of downgrading marijuana’s danger designation, moving it from Schedule I (alongside heroin, ecstasy, and LSD) to Schedule III (next to prescription painkillers, anabolic steroids, and ketamine).

The reclassification wouldn’t make weed federally legal, but it could encourage more research into the drug’s benefits and risks, boost cannabis-based pharmaceutical development, and allow dispensaries and farms to deduct expenses from federal taxes. Shares of weed stocks popped, with AdvisorShares Pure US Cannabis ETF spiking 19%.

President Trump signed an executive order in December directing agencies to loosen restrictions, continuing a similar Biden-era effort. The Washington Post reported Trump has been frustrated with how long the rescheduling process takes. More than half of Americans say marijuana should be legal, and about 1 in 5 Americans aged 12+ use the drug at least once a year. ICYMI: Trump also signed an executive order on Saturday to expedite research into psychedelics like ibogaine for treating mental disorders.

Columnist Daily Dad Daily Dad

Time Is Racing Toward Us

We think we have until later. We think about things as existing in the deep future: when our kids grow up, when we retire, when we get old. But this is wrong, the Stoics said, especially when it comes to mortality. Seneca wrote that death wasn’t something we move slowly towards, but instead something that is rushing at us. We are dying every minute, every day.

So it goes with our children, literally and figuratively. We are losing our 2-year-old and 12-year-old and 20-year-old the second they become those things. They will not one day grow up—they are growing up. There is no later. There is only the evaporating present.

We can’t defer. We can’t delay. We can’t tell ourselves that we have time. We have to take the time now. We have to be present for what this is—the moments big and small. Because we have them right now, and soon enough they (and we) will be gone.

Rundown AI How To • AI Training

Use This Two-Step Dictation Strategy to Write Better Docs

A voice dictation system that immediately makes you write better and improves itself over time. Here’s how to set it up with Typeless and a coding agent:

  1. Install Typeless for dictation, open a Codex/Claude Code session, and dictate: “Draft an outline for a short internal memo about [topic].”
  2. Tell the agent: “Save this as the initial draft and do not edit it. Now create a separate working draft that we can revise.”
  3. Read it and use Typeless to add comments—pointing out inaccurate things, then things that are missing, and finally phrases that sound generic or too AI.
  4. Prompt: “Rewrite the draft using the comments I left. Write it in my tone. Use my verbiage. No em dashes. Preserve the core points, but cut anything that sounds generic.” Review and do one more pass.

Pro tip: Have an agent compare the untouched initial draft against the final drafts every week. It will extract the edits you make and update editorial rules for the future.

Meta told US employees it’s installing mandatory keystroke-tracking software on their computers to help train the company’s AI agents, Reuters reported.

The top-rated internal comment was “this makes me super uncomfortable. How do we opt out?” CTO Andrew Bosworth responded: “There is no option to opt out,” drawing a wave of crying, shocked, and angry emoji reactions.
