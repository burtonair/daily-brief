Legal • Agriculture

SCOTUS Weighs Roundup Cancer Case That Could Reshape Farming and the Midterms

The Supreme Court heard arguments over whether Monsanto can be sued for failing to put a cancer warning on Roundup, its blockbuster weedkiller. A ruling expected by June could decide the future of thousands of lawsuits, a $7.25 billion settlement, and a growing rift in the MAHA movement. Morning Brew 1% Better

The case centers on whether federal pesticide law preempts state-level cancer warning requirements. The EPA has never classified glyphosate as cancer-causing, but the World Health Organization found a link in 2015, triggering a wave of lawsuits. Bayer, which bought Monsanto for $63 billion in 2018, has already paid over $11 billion settling roughly 100,000 cases.

The specific case involves John Durnell, who claims decades of Roundup use caused his blood cancer. A Missouri jury awarded him $1.25 million in 2023. A favorable SCOTUS ruling would limit Bayer’s liability in current and future lawsuits. Glyphosate saves farmers $21 billion annually and is the most widely used herbicide in the US.

The Trump administration is defending Bayer and supporting a farm bill with new glyphosate protections, angering MAHA advocates who helped Trump win in 2024. One wellness influencer told CNN that glyphosate “is going to be a midterm issue” in November. Hundreds of MAHA supporters rallied outside the court.

Set Up AI Teammates with ChatGPT Workspace Agents

ChatGPT’s new Workspace Agents tool (currently in research preview for Business, Enterprise, Edu, and Teachers plans) lets you create AI teammates that autonomously manage tools, databases, and processes on a schedule.

  1. Go to chatgpt.com/agents, click Create agent, and pick a tool, database, or process for this agent to “own.”
  2. Prompt: “Create an agent that manages [lead pipeline, Google Docs folder, Notion database, etc.]. It should do 3 main tasks that can run autonomously on schedules to save me time.”
  3. Follow the setup prompts to add integrations and define what the agent should touch. Codex will look around your integrations to best set up the agent.
  4. Return to the Agents page, select your new agent, and try each of the main tasks. Click the Schedule button to have the agent run every day.

Pro tip: If you don’t have a ChatGPT business plan, you can still use this prompt + approach in other tools to create helpful agents.

Legal • Privacy

Supreme Court Signals Warrants Needed for Geofence Phone Tracking 1% Better Brew

Justices heard arguments in Chatrie v. United States, a Fourth Amendment case challenging police “geofence warrants” that pull Google location data from every phone near a crime scene.

The case stems from a 2019 Virginia bank robbery where authorities pulled location data covering a 17.5-acre area over two hours, initially returning 19 users before narrowing in on Okello Chatrie. Geofence requests now make up over a quarter of US law enforcement data demands, though Google moved location history to user devices in 2023. A ruling is expected by July.

AI • Startups

AlphaGo Creator Launches $1.1B ‘Superlearner’ Lab Rundown AI

Ex-DeepMind researcher David Silver launched Ineffable Intelligence, a London lab that raised $1.1 billion at a $5.1 billion valuation — Europe’s largest seed round ever.

Silver led DeepMind’s reinforcement learning team for a decade, building AlphaGo, AlphaZero, AlphaStar, and AlphaProof. Ineffable’s models skip pre-training and human data entirely, letting agents learn from experience in simulations. Silver framed human data as “a kind of fossil fuel” and his approach as “a renewable fuel, a model that can just learn and learn and learn forever.”
