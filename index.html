Columnist Daily Dad

What a Joy to Be This Excited

Not that long ago, it was like Beatlemania when a trash truck drove down the street. Not that long ago, it was Beatlemania for your teenage girls when Taylor Swift was on the Eras Tour. At Christmas, it was like that scene in Elf where the mall announces that Santa is coming. You could get them to do anything if a popsicle or an episode of their favorite show was offered.

What a joy it is to be that excited about something, to find something so ordinary, so wonderful. We know that it is fleeting and unsustainable, however, by the simple fact of our own day-to-day existence as an adult. What could possibly elicit that kind of reaction in us today?

Understand then that this doesn't last. Soon enough they won't care about tractors. Soon enough they'll be pretending that they never liked that movie or that music. Soon enough you'll be lucky if they still get in the Christmas spirit — if they want to interrupt their busy lives and come home to visit.

Let them enjoy it. Enjoy them enjoying it. It's only here for a brief instant.

How to Take AI Notes on Phone Calls

Most people know about AI notetakers for web meetings, but you can also use them for both outbound and inbound phone calls on your iPhone. Here's how to set it up with Granola:

  1. Install Granola from the App Store, open it, and tap the phone icon in the bottom left to set up. Enter your number and follow the verification instructions.
  2. To make a call, tap the phone button, pick a contact or type a number. The call works normally while Granola listens in the background.
  3. After hanging up, wait a minute. Granola will generate a summary with action items and key takeaways — no need to stay on screen during processing.
  4. For inbound calls, open the app and create a new note. It will only transcribe your voice.

Pro tip: Name notes in a format like [name] @ [company], create folders for work and personal calls. You can also connect Notion, Zapier, Slack, HubSpot, or your CRM.

Note: Check the recording consent laws for your state before recording calls.
