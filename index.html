Cover Story Morning Brew

Jobs Surge — Wall Street Tanks

The US added 172,000 jobs in May — more than double analyst expectations of ~80,000. The labor market is healing. The stock market hated it.

Investors read the stronger-than-expected report as a signal that the Fed won't cut rates anytime soon — possibly hiking instead. The S&P 500 snapped its nine-week winning streak, the Nasdaq had its worst day since April 2025, and chip stocks were decimated: Nvidia, AMD, Micron, and Intel all dropped. A good economy turned into a bad day on Wall Street.

The hiring was driven largely by the FIFA World Cup, which begins in 11 US cities next week. Leisure and hospitality added 70,000 jobs — bars, restaurants, hotels. Local government added 55,000, likely tied to security and entertainment roles for the tournament.

Previous months were revised sharply upward too: March gained 29,000 more jobs than first reported (now 214,000), April gained 64,000 more (now 179,000). The three-month average hit 188,000 — a two-year high.

The red flags: Unemployment held at 4.3%, but long-term joblessness (27+ weeks) climbed to 27.5% — the highest since December 2021. Average hourly earnings rose 3.4% year-over-year, while inflation sits at 3.8%, meaning lower-income Americans are falling further behind. FedWatch now prices in a rate hike by year-end as more likely than a cut.

Retail / Health Morning Brew

GLP-1 Drugs Are Swamping Retailers with Returns

As weight-loss drugs reshape bodies, they're reshaping wardrobes — and the return logistics that come with them. The total value of merchandise returns doubled from 2020 to 2025.

Return management company Narvar reports a three-year trend of apparel exchanges driven by shoppers sizing down. Customers are increasingly ordering multiple sizes of the same item, sending back what doesn't fit — a costly cycle that chips away at retailer margins through shipping, labor, and resale discounts.

The ripple effects are showing up everywhere: Macy's is selling less plus-size clothing. Victoria's Secret is moving smaller bras and underwear. Brides are waiting longer to buy wedding dresses, often because GLP-1-aided weight loss is still in progress on the day they'd normally shop.

Law / Politics Morning Brew

Federal Court Strikes Down Trump Immigration Block on 39 Countries

A federal court invalidated the Trump administration policy that barred immigrants from 39 countries from receiving final decisions on asylum, work permit, green card, and citizenship applications.

The ruling is a significant legal setback for the administration's immigration enforcement strategy. It restores access to the immigration processing pipeline for nationals of the affected countries, who had been frozen out of final determinations while their cases remained pending.
