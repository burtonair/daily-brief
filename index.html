The broader operation, referred to by US officials as Operation Epic Fury, reportedly targeted hundreds of sites across Iran — Israeli officials estimated 500 targets were hit in the opening wave. Strait of Hormuz Disrupted as Iran Fires Back — Oil Supply at Risk Morning Brew

Iran launched ballistic missiles toward Israel and US positions in the Gulf following the US-Israel strikes. Fighting has disrupted shipping through the Strait of Hormuz — the chokepoint for one-fifth of the world's oil supply.

The Strait of Hormuz, the narrow passage between Iran and Oman, is the world's most critical oil transit chokepoint. Any sustained disruption could push energy prices sharply higher with global economic ripple effects. Iranian officials have long threatened to close the strait as a retaliatory measure, and the current conflict has brought that threat into reality.

Israeli officials confirmed that several senior Iranian officials were killed alongside Khamenei in the opening strikes. The scale of the operation — with an estimated 500 targets struck in the first wave — signals this is intended as a decisive campaign. Questions of succession within Iran's theocratic government and the fate of its nuclear program add profound uncertainty to an already volatile situation. The Homebuying Dream Deferred: Average First-Time Buyer Now 40 Years Old Morning Brew

The median home price of $405,000 is far above 2020's $329,000. By the time most Americans save for a down payment, they may need reading glasses to sign the mortgage. The median age of first-time buyers has climbed from 30 in 2010 to 40 in 2025.

The average US mortgage rate dropped below 6% this week for the first time since 2022 — but it's still more than double the 2.97% rate in 2021. Rising prices and mortgage rates pushed the average monthly mortgage payment to $2,329 in 2025, up from $1,924 two years prior, according to Rocket Homes. The number of home sale contracts signed last month fell to the lowest point since 2001, per the National Association of Realtors.

While Americans pay more for homes, they're getting less floor space. The size of an average new home dropped 11% over the last decade while the price per square foot rose 74%, per LendingTree. Some younger buyers are turning away from homeownership entirely, opting instead for investments in stocks and crypto. But there is a silver lining: the rate of homeownership by Gen Zers rose 1 percentage point last year to 27%, per Redfin, driven by new condos becoming more affordable.

Real Estate

Hot vs. Cold: Kenosha and Hartford Lead While Sun Belt Sinks Morning Brew

The Southeast and Southwest that boomed during Covid are now cooling — some markets in Texas and Florida have the highest rates of underwater properties in the country. Meanwhile, the hottest US housing markets last month were Kenosha, Wisconsin, and Hartford, Connecticut.

About 2.1% of US homeowners with a mortgage were underwater by the end of 2025, equaling 1.1 million people — the largest underwater portion of borrowers since 2018, representing a 60% increase from the beginning of 2025, per Intercontinental Exchange data. Many people who bought post-2022 now face the one-two punch of high mortgage rates and falling demand in their areas.

Underwater mortgages are less common on the West Coast and in the Northeast and Midwest, where demand is rising and homes are moving quickly. The two hottest housing markets in the US last month were Kenosha, Wisconsin, and Hartford, Connecticut, according to Realtor.com. The swing from Sun Belt boom to Midwest surge reflects how quickly demand dynamics can shift when remote work tailwinds fade and interest rates rise.
