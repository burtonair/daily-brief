UAE Leaves OPEC in Major Blow to Oil Cartel

The Gulf nation announced an abrupt departure from the 12-nation oil cartel, effective Friday, reshaping the global energy landscape amid the Iran war and multi-year high oil prices. Morning Brew 1% Better

The UAE — OPEC's third-largest producer after Saudi Arabia and Iraq — said it's leaving to have more freedom over how much oil to sell. Before the closure of the Strait of Hormuz forced cutbacks, the country produced 3.6 million barrels daily, maxing out its quota. It now seeks to boost production to 5 million barrels daily by 2027.

OPEC has already been losing its grip as US fracking floods global markets with American oil. Geopolitical tensions with Saudi Arabia, the de facto OPEC leader, likely played a role. The UAE says it timed the exit to minimize disruption during the Iran war, when oil prices are already elevated.

Experts warn that while the departure may not suppress prices immediately, it could make them more volatile in the long term.

Columnist Daily Dad Daily Dad

Parenting Is Slow Work

Why won't they listen? Why do they do the opposite of what you ask? Parenting can be disheartening. You talk, you explain, you repeat yourself — and it seems like nothing is getting through those thick little skulls.

Mary Shelley's father knew this feeling well. In a letter, he reflected on the slow, invisible work of raising a child: "Seeds of intellect and knowledge, seeds of moral judgment and conduct, I have sown; but the soil for a long while seemed ungrateful to the tiller's care. It was not so; the happiest operations were going on quietly and unobserved, and at the moment when it was of the most importance, they unfolded themselves to the delight of every beholder."

We think they're not listening, they're not observing — that they don't appreciate the lessons we're trying to impart. And maybe they don't. Not yet. But under the surface, something is happening. The seeds are taking root.

Our job is to be patient. No matter how impossible it seems, we have to keep sowing and toiling and tilling.

Automate Any Manual Task with OpenAI Codex

Let OpenAI's Codex click through any annoying, repetitive work using Computer Use on Mac or Windows:

  1. Open Codex, go to Plugins, find and enable the Computer Use plugin, then start a new task.
  2. Open the permissions menu and switch from Default permissions to Full access. Confirm any prompts.
  3. Give a real task, like: "Open Chrome and debug the UI of this web page. Click through, reproduce the bug I describe, then tell me what you think is causing it."

Pro tip: Codex can automate repetitive workflows in local apps too — try it for Photoshop exports, Premiere cleanup, file renaming, or any other tool.

Iran's Oil Squeeze: Crude Piling Up as US Naval Blockade Bites

Iran is repurposing derelict tanks, improvised containers, and rail shipments to China to stash unsold crude after a US naval blockade halted exports on April 13.

Tanker loadings plunged from 2.1 million barrels daily in early April to roughly 567,000 between April 14–23. Production could be cut by more than half by mid-May. Tehran has offered regional mediators a deal to halt Strait of Hormuz attacks in exchange for ending the blockade. Tightened supply has lifted global oil prices and squeezed jet fuel availability.
