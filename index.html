Europe's Planes Are About to Run Out of Fuel

The Strait of Hormuz closure has disrupted oil supplies so drastically that Europe has "maybe six weeks" of jet fuel left, with flight cancellations coming "soon" unless the waterway reopens. Morning Brew

This is "the largest energy crisis we have ever faced," IEA Executive Director Fatih Birol told the Associated Press. Europe's largest carrier, Ryanair, suggested it's on track for fuel shortages by June, while easyJet said it currently has 70% of its summertime jet fuel covered.

German carrier Lufthansa will cut some long-haul flights and take up to 40 planes out of rotation. Around the world, airlines are raising ticket prices, and all major US airlines have hiked baggage fees in recent weeks.

The US is better positioned as the world's largest net exporter of jet fuel, but major airlines still project billions in added costs. The price spike could reportedly sink Spirit Airlines entirely. If the strait reopens, it could take "up to two years to come back where we were before the war," Birol said.

Columnist Daily Dad

Don't Be One of These Parents

It sounds terrible to you. It's nothing like what you listen to. It's nothing like what you thought they'd listen to or be interested in. You think your kids' music sucks. You might not be wrong — and yet you are definitely wrong.

There's a touching scene in the documentary "I Talked to 50 Dads at the Eras Tour" where a father says, "I was never a Taylor Swift fan, but I saw a documentary about her and then I was sort of floored." He explained he never wanted to be the dad that made fun of what his kids were interested in. "My parents were like, 'Duran Duran's the worst' … so I worked hard to not be like that."

Whether you think the music is good or not, interest is interest when it comes to your kids. Take it. Be curious about it. Make an effort to like it yourself — or at least understand it. If they're connecting with the music, or with anything else, that means it's also helping them to feel things, to understand things about themselves they might not be able to express. It's a window into their world.

So don't judge. Listen. Get on their level and take a moment to get into it with them. Your interest will go far with your kids and it will give you another thing to connect over. Do not miss that opportunity.

NYC Plans to Tax Rich People's Backup Homes Morning Brew

New York Gov. Kathy Hochul and NYC Mayor Zohran Mamdani announced a proposed tax on non-primary residences in the Big Apple valued above $5 million.

The measure would affect about 13,000 second homes — aka pied-à-terres — owned by wealthy folks who mostly live elsewhere. Mamdani pitched the measure as a charge on "the ultra-wealthy and global elites" that would help close the city's $5.4 billion budget gap, bringing at least $500 million yearly.

Proponents argue that absent neighbors benefit from municipal services without paying local taxes or patronizing nearby businesses. Several major cities like Paris and Vancouver already tax empty homes. But local real estate groups say it will undermine NYC's luxury-housing market.

Even the Train to the World Cup Is Expensive Morning Brew

NJ Transit's plans to ratchet up the round-trip fare from Penn Station to MetLife Stadium to ~$150 during this summer's FIFA World Cup has sparked an outcry.

PepsiCo's Price Cuts Are Working Morning Brew
