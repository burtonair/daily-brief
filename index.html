Inflation Hits 4.2% — A 3-Year High Driven by the Iran War

Energy prices stemming from the US-Iran conflict pushed May inflation to its highest level since April 2023, though a softer core reading offers some hope that the worst may be behind us.

The consumer price index report released Wednesday put May inflation at 4.2%, driven by the energy price shock from the ongoing Iran conflict. The three major indexes closed in the red on the news, combined with renewed aggression in the region.

The core CPI — which strips out volatile energy and food prices — told a more optimistic story: core prices climbed just 0.2% in May, well below April's 0.4% rise and below analysts' 0.3% forecast. For the year, CPI is up 2.9%, in line with expectations. Gas prices have also ticked down slightly so far in June.

When asked about the report, President Trump said "I love the inflation," pointing to 22 ships the US reportedly got through the Strait of Hormuz under cover of darkness. Few expect a rate cut when the Fed makes its first announcement under new Chair Kevin Warsh next Wednesday — the odds now favor a rate hike in December.

AI / Policy The Rundown AI

Amodei's Full Regulatory Vision: UBI, Drug Approvals, and Autonomous Weapons Bans

Dario Amodei's sweeping policy essay calls for AI regulation that matches the pace of the technology — and goes much further than just model safety gates.

The full essay proposes independently screening frontier models across four risk areas, with regulators empowered to ground dangerous systems. A separate jobs framework plans for unemployment at several levels, including investment accounts holding shares of AI companies and UBI.

Other asks: faster approval of AI-designed drugs, limits and bans on autonomous weapons, and stronger advanced chip export controls. Amodei also flagged that Claude Mythos Preview's hacking risks represent a major turning point — making frontier models "tools of global and national strategic consequence."

Skeptics will note that "regulate me harder" from a market leader often reads as a moat-building tactic. But Amodei has been consistent on safety long before it was fashionable, and the urgency is only scaling with each release.

SpaceX Unveils AI1: Solar-Powered Orbital AI Datacenter, Three Days Before Its IPO

The first preview of AI1 — a satellite built to run AI chips in orbit — dropped just days before what could be the largest IPO in history.

SpaceX says the craft is "much simpler than a Starlink satellite," trading complex antennas for solar panels, radiators, and lasers that beam data to Earth. Musk says each satellite will have as much computing power as one of Nvidia's top server racks, with the ability to swap chips over time.

The appeal: orbit offers unlimited solar energy without the local grid fights that are stalling data center builds across the US. Google and Anthropic have already signed on as orbital compute customers — putting two frontier labs on the other side of Sam Altman's "ridiculous" verdict on space-based datacenters. SpaceX's planned Bastrop factory could cover 11M+ square feet, with AI1 production targeted before 2028.
