Protein Is Now in Everything — And It's a $56 Billion Industry

From Doritos to Pop-Tarts to water, protein has infiltrated nearly every corner of the grocery store. A tectonic shift in American health culture — fueled by GLP-1 drugs and a growing obsession with macros — is reshaping the food industry. Morning Brew

The protein-everywhere trend lines up perfectly with the GLP-1 boom: a 2025 Gallup report found 12% of Americans (~30 million) now take weight loss drugs, up from 6% in 2024. Meanwhile, 71% of Americans have increased their protein intake, up from 48% six years ago.

Businesses are cashing in: General Mills did $100 million in sales from protein cereals alone. Protein snacks are growing at three times the rate of the overall snacking industry. And with GLP-1 prices coming down and pill forms on the way, the global protein market is expected to surpass $100 billion by 2034.

AI & Design

Inside Canva AI 2.0: An Exclusive Interview with Co-Founder Cameron Adams Rundown AI

Canva's CPO explains how the company trained its foundation model not just on finished designs, but on the actual sequence of edits 265 million users make — creating what he calls "creative intelligence" rather than mere generation.

"Most AI systems learn from finished outputs. What they can't see is everything that happened before it," Adams told The Rundown. The Canva Design Model lives inside the editor itself, shaped by typography systems, layout rules, brand kits, and collaborative workflows — generating entirely new editable elements (powered by their 2024 acquisition of Leonardo.ai).

On whether the gap between great and average designers is shrinking: "When anyone can produce something polished, what separates the work is the thinking behind it. Judgment and empathy become more important — the instinct about what will resonate. These things only humans can bring."

Chicken Is Now King as Beef Prices Force Americans to Trade Down Morning Brew

Wholesale beef prices rose 13% year-over-year in February as domestic production declined 4%. The USDA projects beef intake will decline 2% this year.

Bargain hunters are trading down from filet mignon to sirloin, or pivoting to chicken entirely. Tyson Foods reported selling 3.7% more chicken in Q4 while beef sales dropped 7.3%. In fast food, chicken chains like Raising Cane's and Dave's Hot Chicken are booming while burger-centric value chains like Burger King struggle with declining margins. McDonald's recently added seasoned McCrispy Strips to its menu.

Canva Positions Itself as the "Last Mile" for AI Creative Work Rundown AI

As AI assistants like ChatGPT now generate and publish campaigns from chat, Adams argues the design canvas still matters — and Canva is embedding itself into AI ecosystems including ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, and Google Gemini.

"Most AI assistants are great for thinking, but they're often a dead end for doing," Adams said. Canva is also stress-testing its model by deliberately breaking designs — "perturbing" spacing and hierarchy — to train the system to catch and fix errors alongside users.

Health

How Much Protein Do You Actually Need? The Guidelines Just Changed Morning Brew

The US updated protein guidance from 0.8 g/kg to 1.2–1.6 g/kg this year. Many nutritionists consider it too much for average adults, though it may be appropriate for strength training or those over 65.

Myth busted: The popular "1 gram per pound" rule is excessive according to the Mayo Clinic — anything above 0.9 g/lb (2 g/kg) offers no additional benefit. Another myth: plant-based protein is "incomplete" compared to animal protein. Stanford's director of nutrition studies says carnivorous diets may actually lack sufficient fiber.
