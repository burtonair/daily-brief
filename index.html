How Much Is Already Gone?

It's been a crazy period. You've been digging out of it ever since they were born, since they came home and the sleep deprivation began. You've been getting them dressed, getting them to the doctor, getting them walking and talking, getting them to this birthday or that one and then into school and then to… wherever your kids happen to be in age right now.

How easy it can be in the midst of all this, especially when you're thinking of the next milestone, the next obligation, the next grade, to miss what's happening — which is to say their childhood.

You look up one day and realize that infancy is over. You look up one day and realize that there will be no more days where you have them with you all day because that phase of their and your life is through. You look up one day and realize you have a ten-year-old, that ten years have gone by and that ten years are gone. You look up one day and realize they're no longer a child at all.

Take a moment today and think about that. Not how old your kid is going to be this year, but how many years they've already lived. Think of the phases past, the grades graduated from, the milestones now receding in the distance. Tempus Fugit. It goes by very fast… and when it's gone, it is gone. Parent accordingly.

Government & Politics

TSA Faces Crisis as Partial Shutdown Deprives Agents of Paychecks Brew

Two airports publicly posted requests on X asking travelers to donate gas cards and nonperishable food to TSA agents — a move that appears to violate federal gift rules — as the partial government shutdown deprives screeners of their first full paycheck since February 14.

TSA agent positions start at roughly $40,000 a year, and most agents live paycheck to paycheck. 305 employees have left since the shutdown began, out of about 61,000 TSA workers total. Multihour security wait times were reported at Houston and New Orleans airports last weekend.

The Department of Homeland Security, which employs about 260,000 workers, remains the last US agency to lock down its budget. The gridlock centers on controversial ICE and Border Patrol funding: Senate Democrats want judicial warrants before agents can enter private homes, while Republicans call that a nonstarter.

Is Privatization the Answer?
  • Since 2004, TSA's screening partnership program has used private firms at some airports, including SFO.
  • Supporters say privatization sidesteps shutdown-related pay gaps.
  • The union representing agents says privatization would jeopardize safety and worker benefits.
  • Airlines are warning of severe staffing shortages heading into the busiest spring break travel season on record.
Energy

Trump Eyes Jones Act Waiver and Russia Sanctions Relief to Tame Gas Prices Brew

The Trump administration is pursuing a two-pronged approach to address skyrocketing gas prices caused by the Iran conflict: temporarily waiving the century-old Jones Act to allow foreign ships to move oil between US ports, and lifting sanctions on Russian oil currently at sea.

The Jones Act, devised in 1920 to protect US shipbuilders and crews from foreign competition, today leaves only 92 compliant ships afloat. Populous areas that lack pipelines — like the Northeast — remain dependent on imported oil despite the US producing more than it consumes.

Enabling foreign ships to deliver oil from Texas and Alaska domestically could reduce reliance on imports. However, JPMorgan estimated in 2022 that removing the rule would only shave about 10 cents off the price of gas per gallon — and gas has already jumped 60 cents this month.

Separately, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said temporarily lifting sanctions on Russian oil was "unfortunate" but would last only a "micro period." Read WSJ analysis 1%

Cybersecurity

AI Agent Hacks McKinsey's Internal 'Lilli' Chatbot in Under Two Hours Rundown

Security startup CodeWall revealed its AI agent broke into McKinsey's internal AI platform 'Lilli' in under two hours, gaining full read-write access to a database containing 46.5 million confidential messages, 728,000 client files, and 57,000 user accounts — all in plain text.

Lilli is used by approximately 70% of McKinsey's 65,000 staff for client work, including chat, analysis, and search across 100,000+ internal documents. CodeWall's agent found exposed API documentation with 22 endpoints that required no authentication. One endpoint had a basic security flaw enabling full database access.

The breached data included messages discussing M&A strategy and client work, 95 control prompts, and files with client data. McKinsey was notified, engaged a third-party forensics firm (which found no unauthorized access by others), and patched the vulnerability. The incident underscores that even the most sophisticated firms can miss fundamental AI security basics. FT coverage
