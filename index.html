GLP-1 Drugs Brew

Tensions Compound in Weight Loss Drug Market

Hims & Hers did its best impression of Grandpa Simpson entering and leaving a building after locking eyes with the FDA.

The telehealth company announced Thursday it would sell a $49 compounded version of Wegovy's weight loss pill, but by Saturday announced it would stop. The FDA released a statement name-checking Hims & Hers, saying it intended to "take decisive steps" against compounding pharmacies using GLP-1 active ingredients.

The gray area: Compounding pharmacies can customize drug formulations for patients, though such custom drugs are not FDA-approved. During official shortages, they can make copies to meet demand. Those shortages ended in late 2024, and the FDA ordered compounders to stop making copycats.

Zoom out: Novo Nordisk and Eli Lilly want to protect patent rights and ensure their brands don't get sullied by ineffective knock-offs, while compounding pharmacies want to appeal to price-sensitive consumers.

Industrial Brew

Corning Rides Glass to New Heights as AI Winner

The 175-year-old glassmaker's stock hit an all-time high, up 130% over the past year, as it cashes in on AI infrastructure.

Corning has long been an innovator, producing everything from Edison's first light bulbs to Pyrex bakeware. In 1970, the company developed fiber-optic wire. In 2018, Corning focused on making thinner, tougher glass cables that performed especially well in data centers.

The deals: Late last month, Corning signed a $6 billion fiber-optic cable contract with Meta. In August, it signed a $2.5 billion deal to manufacture all cover glass for iPhones and Apple Watches.

Glass bubble? Corning was on a similar trajectory from 1997 to 2000, but when the dot-com bubble popped, the company lost more than 90% of its value. The company says it's more diversified now.

Media Brew

Washington Post CEO Exits Amid Mass Layoffs

CEO Will Lewis stepped down days after the newspaper initiated layoffs of nearly one-third of staff.

Lewis, who was tapped by Jeff Bezos to lead the outlet in 2023, departed Saturday evening. Washington Post's CFO Jeff D'Onofrio was named acting CEO and publisher.

Following Lewis's departure, Bezos released his first public statement since the layoffs. It did not address the outgoing CEO, but said the Post had an "extraordinary opportunity," and its "roadmap to success" would come from readers' data telling it "what is valuable and where to focus."
